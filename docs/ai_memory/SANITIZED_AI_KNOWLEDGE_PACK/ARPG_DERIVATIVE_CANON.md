# ARPG Derivative Canon

## Product Direction

The ARPG derivative is a separate Diablo II-style isometric action RPG path connected to the same world and lore foundation. The first working title is `Aerathea: Honor and Glory`.

The ARPG is a faster shipping path, a precursor parallel commercial project, a lore foundation for the MMORPG, and a testbed for combat, loot, readability, pacing, and shared systems.

ARPG assets remain derivative outputs. ARPG-specific meshes, materials, textures, VFX, UI, icons, audio, and Blender files stay under the ARPG derivative area unless promoted back to shared source by a later production decision.

## Timeline And Story

The ARPG takes place 50 to 100 years before the MMORPG. It starts in Deepwood Vale after the Aetheric Cataclysm. The player characters share a fixed campaign origin role while still being player-created characters.

The opening uses storm-night atmosphere, fog, distant thunder, blue/purple lightning, night-to-day progression, black undiscovered map, and light radius increased by torches or enchanted items.

## Play Direction

The first prototype uses a fixed isometric camera. Rotatable camera is a deferred test option. Controls are keyboard/mouse first, with controller compatibility planned early.

The ARPG is exploratory and quest-driven rather than a generic mass-horde ARPG. Loot remains meaningful and cohesive with MMORPG identity. Aether crystals can come from gathering, mobs, chests, and quest rewards.

## Class Direction

Approved initial classes are Warrior, Ranger, Paladin, Arcane Knight, and Mekgineer. Priest remains a later class lane and MMORPG class candidate.

Warrior prototype ability sets are weapon-loadout based. Paladins are Dragonsworn champions of light, life, creation, freedom, and justice. Arcane Knights are warrior-mage hunters of supernatural and magical threats. Mekgineers begin with their first Mek at level 1 in the ARPG path.
