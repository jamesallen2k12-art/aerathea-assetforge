# World Lore

## Project Identity

Aerathea is an original stylized fantasy world supporting an MMORPG-scale project and a separate ARPG derivative path. Aetherium appears in project and product planning as the wider game identity around Aerathea, Aetheric power, and Aetherium-infused technology.

The tone target combines classic readable fantasy with stronger atmosphere, modern lighting, and original worldbuilding. Classic MMORPG structure is a pacing and production reference only; names, lore, maps, formulas, UI art, icons, and assets from other franchises are outside Aerathea canon.

## Aetheric Cataclysm Frame

The ARPG-era disaster is an Aetheric Cataclysm caused by the destruction of the ancient goddess Tiamat. The shock damaged worlds, shattered one seal on the ancient prison of the Abyss, and sent raw Aetheric essence into Aerathea. Tiamat chose to empower Aerathea rather than allow that essence to be consumed by beings within the prison.

Aerathea is permeated with Aetheric energy. Mortal races are longer-lived and more resilient than modern humans. Many people can remain youthful and healthy for centuries, with some races or bloodlines lasting thousands of years.

## Deepwood Vale

Deepwood Vale is a major continuity anchor. In the ARPG timeline it survives the wider disaster better than nearby regions because of geography and leadership. The ARPG begins there 50 to 100 years before the MMORPG era. Vale leadership sends player agents outward to discover what happened, recover resources, find survivors, and create order.

## Infernals

Infernals were once mortal cult bloodlines devoted to Balgoroth and Abyssal powers. They view their transformation as elevation, not corruption. Their culture treats strength as worship, worthy sacrifice as sacred, and weakness as contamination. They reject mortal weaponry as a sign of reliance on tools rather than personal demonic gifts.

Core Infernal identity includes reddish skin, black claws, required horns, large leathery wings, long thick tails, predatory upright posture, regeneration, invisible-sight motifs, restrained abyssal brand or eye glow, and natural-weapon combat. Their core roles are warrior, rogue, hunter, and mage.

Lesser Infernals are born through the Infernal bloodline. They are autonomous at birth and shaped by the culling temper, a violently aggressive early drive that exposes weakness and fuels growth through predation. Their lifecycle stages are Spawn, 1st Kill, Blooded, Elder, and Ancient. The first birthday roughly equals a mortal age of seven, followed by very slow apparent aging until mature stasis, with visible aging returning after about 900 years.

## Gnomes And Mekgineers

Gnomes are compact, expressive, ingenious, and craft-driven. Their visual identity uses brass, copper, dark iron, leather, blue Aetherium, goggles, tools, compact machines, and sturdy proportions. Gnome hands have four fingers plus one thumb. Ears are expressive but controlled without inflating measured height.

Mekgineers are technowizards who build, pilot, maintain, and invent Meks. Most Gnomes channel Aether through Arcane Conduits, AetherTek generators, weapons, armor, Technomancy, and Meks rather than directly through themselves.

## Ogres

Ogres are war-created, huge, brutal, and pragmatic. They are fascinated with magic and technology through crude Teknomancy. They envy Gnome inventions and counter them with bigger, tougher, rougher machines and fortifications. Ogre built environments include cairn structures, walls, gates, defensive fortifications, forges, Tek shops, barracks, necropolises, and rough settlements.

## Giants

Giants are remote mountain people from isolated highlands and hidden cave towns. Civilized Giants are master stoneworkers with cliff settlements, cave halls, terraces, bridges, waterworks, and monumental masonry. Nomadic Giants range the highlands. The Blood Axe Tribe is a separate hostile Giant faction associated with raids, enslavement, blood rituals, trophy armor, red banners, and ritual standing stones.

## Drakhar

Drakhar are lizardfolk inspired by bearded dragons and desert lizards. They are drawn to magic, can sense the general direction of magic, and can glean the nature of magic items by handling them. They sold their souls to Volcreon millennia ago. Volcreon was destroyed or sealed after a world-breaking battle; the seal is now broken and his influence is gathering again.

## Valar

Valar are highborn northern human-like people, not elves. They have rounded human ears, strong human faces, athletic warrior-priest bearing, fur-trimmed blue/silver/gold armor, cloaks, oath symbols, and restrained holy or arcane accents.
