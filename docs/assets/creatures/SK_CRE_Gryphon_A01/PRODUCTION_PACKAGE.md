Create the first Aerathea creature package: a noble gryphon with eagle head, eagle wings and talons, lion rear body, lion hind legs, and lion tail. It should feel majestic and powerful, but still buildable as a mid-poly MMO creature with aggressive LODs.

## Gameplay Purpose

Supports first creature anatomy and skeletal mesh validation, mountain/sky guardian encounters, ambient wildlife identity, future mount planning, scale/collision testing, and large-creature animation planning.

The first package should focus on the base golden/mountain gryphon, not a raid boss or heavily armored mount.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette must clearly read:

- Eagle head with strong hooked beak.
- Feathered head crest and neck ruff.
- Large wings with readable primary feathers.
- Eagle forelegs and talons.
- Lion torso/rear haunch transition.
- Lion hind legs and paws.
- Long lion tail with tuft.

Model large feathers that define wing and crest silhouette. Use texture/normal detail for dense small feather layering, fur patterning, and tiny barb detail.

## Scale Notes

- Shoulder height at forequarters: 180-220 cm.
- Head height in neutral pose: 240-300 cm.
- Body length nose to rump: 350-450 cm.
- Wingspan: 650-850 cm in full spread.
- Tail length: 180-240 cm.

Author in centimeters. Pivot at ground center under body mass. Keep rider/mount sockets planned but inactive until mount gameplay rules are approved.

## Materials And Color Palette

Base variant palette:

- Golden brown wing and body feathers.
- Cream or pale tan chest/neck feathers.
- Amber eagle eye.
- Dark horn/keratin beak and claws.
- Warm lion fur on rear haunches.
- Subtle cool gray/brown shadowing in feather recesses.

Texture style uses hand-painted broad feather groups, baked-AO-style depth at wing overlaps and talon joints, and normal-map feather/fur surface detail. No emissive map for the base gryphon.

## Concept Image Prompt

Create an original stylized fantasy MMORPG creature production sheet of a noble golden gryphon for the world of Aerathea. The design should emphasize an eagle head with hooked beak, strong feather crest, powerful wings with readable primary feathers, eagle forelegs with talons, lion rear body with hind paws, long lion tail with tuft, golden brown and cream color language, mountain and sky guardian identity, majestic protective mood, and large creature gameplay role. Use hand-painted texture detail, readable shapes, baked-AO-style depth, normal-map-style surface detail, no unnecessary emissive effects, and MMO-friendly production design. Present it as a creature turnaround with front, side, back, wing-spread view, scale callout beside a 180 cm humanoid, skeleton callout, material swatches, and LOD callout on a clean background. Avoid copying any existing franchise and avoid excessive micro-detail that would not translate to a mid-poly Unreal asset.

## Modeling Notes

Model real geometry for body mass, head, beak, crest, neck ruff, wings, forelegs, talons, haunches, hind legs, paws, and tail. Large wing primary/secondary feather slabs should define the silhouette.

Fake with texture/normal maps: dense small feather layers, fine fur, tiny feather barbs, beak scratches, minor claw scuffs, and small scale texture on forelegs.

The eagle/lion transition must be anatomically readable from side view and not look like two separate creatures glued together.

## Triangle Budget

Large creature target:

- LOD0: 35k-50k tris.
- LOD1: 22k-30k tris.
- LOD2: 10k-16k tris.
- LOD3: 3k-6k tris.

Use 2K texture sets by default. Use 4K only for a hero/mount marketing render or cinematic review.

## LOD Plan

- LOD0: full silhouette, wing feather slabs, crest, talons, paws, tail tuft, major feather/fur forms.
- LOD1: reduce secondary feather geometry, smaller crest cuts, talon bevels, and haunch fur planes.
- LOD2: merge wing feather groups into broader slabs, simplify paws and neck ruff.
- LOD3: preserve eagle head, wing span, talon stance, lion rear mass, and tail silhouette only.

Never destroy the wing outline, hooked beak, talons, lion haunches, or tail tuft first.

## Collision Notes

Use simple runtime collision:

- Movement capsule or creature body capsule tuned to body length/height.
- Physics asset bodies for head, neck, chest, pelvis, upper/lower forelegs, talons, upper/lower hind legs, paws, wing upper/lower bones, and tail segments.
- Separate combat trace zones later for head, body, wings, and legs if combat system requires it.

Do not use per-feather collision. Do not use complex-as-simple collision.
