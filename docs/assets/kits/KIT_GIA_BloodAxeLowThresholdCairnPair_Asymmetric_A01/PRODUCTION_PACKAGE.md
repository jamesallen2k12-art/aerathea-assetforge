`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeLowThresholdCairnPair_Asymmetric_A01` defines an asymmetric paired low-threshold cairn kit for Blood Axe cave approaches, hostile camp edges, and abandoned raider territory breaks. The pair uses one wider, lower cairn and one taller companion cairn so the composition feels hand-placed, crude, and hostile without forming a formal doorway, objective frame, route marker, or readable sign.

The visual language should be built from a few large Giant-placed stones, soot-darkened contacts, cold ash, trampled mud, cave grit, rawhide or rope restraint, and sparse oxide red Blood Axe accents. Blood Axe remains a hostile Giant sub-faction and must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture, including blue-gray civic masonry, terrace or waterwork motifs, warm hearth identity, carved civic ornament, restrained blue-rune culture, and peaceful highland wayfinding.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate implementation signoff.

- LOD0: full asymmetric pair, wide/low cairn silhouette, taller companion silhouette, few large stones, broad chipped planes, shallow ash/mud base, restrained red accent, and major rope/rawhide forms if used.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chip geometry, underside cuts, cloth edge cuts, lashing subdivisions, minor base undercuts, and hidden backside detail while preserving the asymmetric pair read.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify stones into larger planes, merge minor support stones, flatten cloth folds, remove non-silhouette accent pieces, and reduce ash/mud edge complexity.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the wide/low cairn, the taller companion mass, the broken non-doorway spacing, and one subdued red accent if needed for faction read.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, paint chips, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashing cuts, little knots, secondary cloth tears, tiny stone wedges, minor iron nicks, horn chips, and non-silhouette tokens.
3. Back-facing stones, underside bevels, hidden contact cuts, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Duplicate foot stones, minor cloth holes, secondary chipped planes, and optional accent geometry.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and large ash/mud edge subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary wide/low and taller companion silhouettes.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, Blood Axe red accent restraint, few-large-stones construction, the wide/low versus taller companion relationship, or the broken asymmetry before removing small detail.
