# KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCairnGuideposts_A01 Production Package

## Art Direction Summary

- Asset name: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCairnGuideposts_A01`
- Asset type: Kit docs-only production package for static Giant-scale cairn guidepost planning
- Faction/theme: Blood Axe Tribe, hostile Giant sub-faction only
- Related packages: `SM_GIA_BloodAxeApproachCairnMarker_A01`, `KIT_GIA_BloodAxePathMarkers_A01`, future `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeRitualStones_A01`, and the validated `SK_GIA_Base_A01` scale lock
- Status: docs-only planning package

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCairnGuideposts_A01` defines a small kit of crude Blood Axe cairn guideposts: single cairns, paired cairns, cloth-tied guideposts, ash-stained bases, moved-camp markers, material-discipline rows, LOD/collision planning rows, and non-shipping review rows. The kit exists to keep these pieces as visual memory markers and route-edge dressing only.

The Blood Axe Tribe should read as a brutal raider branch of Giant culture, not as the default Giant identity. This kit must stay visually separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture, including blue-gray civic masonry, refined cave-town carving, terrace or waterwork motifs, warm hearth settlement language, restrained blue runes, and peaceful highland guide markers.

This package creates no meshes, textures, material instances, source folders, validators, game assets, runtime files, or placement work. It does not select a first implementation target.

## Gameplay Purpose

The kit supports hostile route readability as visual dressing only. It gives future Blood Axe stronghold, camp, and ritual-stone approaches a consistent memory-marker rhythm through crude stone stacks, paired cairns, torn oxide red cloth, ash-stained bases, and abandoned camp-remnant markers.

Allowed planning uses:

- Suggest repeated route-edge silhouettes that imply Blood Axe territory without becoming gameplay markers.
- Show how cairns and cloth ties can mark remembered paths, moved camp edges, old warning beats, and rough approach turns.
- Support visual continuity between the existing approach cairn marker, path-marker kit, and future ritual-stone package.
- Preserve Giant-scale readability against the validated Giant baselines.

No waypoint, quest, nav/pathfinding, readable text, interaction behavior, objective markers, DCC, FBX, Unreal asset creation, startup placement, or first implementation target selection is defined by this package.

## Silhouette Notes

- Single cairns should read as blunt, uneven, Giant-built stacks of a few large stones, not dense pebble piles.
- Paired cairns should frame a route edge, bend, or old camp threshold through asymmetry and spacing, not through arrows or signage.
- Cloth-tied guideposts should use large stone posts, short rough timber, or hybrid stone-and-stake forms with broad oxide red cloth strips.
- Ash-stained bases should ground the markers with soot, charcoal, burned mud, and trampled earth while staying low and non-interactive.
- Moved-camp markers should look partially collapsed, weathered, or deliberately left behind after a Blood Axe camp moved on.
- Review rows should be visually distinct as non-shipping planning rows and should never read as in-world approved props.

Model real geometry in future packages for main stone masses, large cloth silhouettes, heavy bindings, rough stake shafts, and broad base forms. Keep tiny chips, small cracks, cloth weave, soot speckles, ash flecks, fray, rope fibers, and fine scratches in textures, normals, AO, or masks.

Avoid skull piles, graphic gore, UI arrows, quest symbols, carved readable text, polished civic stonework, blue rune markers, or a dense red-only palette.

Planning dimensions:

- Paired cairns: two markers 250-800 cm apart, varied in height so they do not form a gate or objective frame.

- Moved-camp marker: 80-220 cm tall, often wider and more collapsed than active route-edge cairns.

These dimensions are visual planning values only. They are not traversal, route width, collision, encounter, patrol, spawn, or camera metrics.

## LOD Plan

All future important child assets require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate implementation signoff.

- LOD0: full cairn stack, paired-marker spacing, cloth silhouettes, large lashings, ash base, and moved-camp collapse read.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce stone bevels, cloth edge cuts, small ties, underside detail, and minor base stones.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; merge smaller stones, simplify cloth to broad strips, flatten small base detail, and preserve route-edge silhouette.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the major cairn mass, paired read if applicable, broad oxide red cloth beat, and large ash base value.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, fray cuts, stitches, scratches, and paint chips.
2. Small rope fibers, secondary lashings, minor knots, little stones, and hidden underside contact detail.
3. Small cloth holes, small horn or bone chips, minor iron scraps, and non-silhouette debris.
4. Backside detail, interior marker clutter, duplicate foot stones, and secondary decorative cuts.
5. Small bevels on supporting stones and optional review-row helper detail.

Never reduce the Giant-scale cairn read, route-edge rhythm, moved-camp memory silhouette, or Blood Axe red/black material identity before removing small detail.
