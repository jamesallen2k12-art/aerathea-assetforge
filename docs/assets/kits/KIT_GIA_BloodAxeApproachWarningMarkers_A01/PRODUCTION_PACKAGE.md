`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeApproachWarningMarkers_A01` defines sparse static visual warnings for the route into a Blood Axe stronghold approach. The kit covers red cloth stakes, horn markers, broken shield tokens, blade fragments, rough warning posts, mixed marker clusters, non-shipping scale rows, review composition rows, material discipline, and LOD/collision planning.

Blood Axe identity must remain a hostile Giant sub-faction read. These markers may look crude, threatening, raider-made, soot-stained, and territorial, but they must not replace neutral or civilized Giant culture. Neutral/civilized Giant culture remains tied to hidden highland settlements, master stonework, blue-gray masonry, terraces, bridges, waterworks, warm hearth light, restrained blue runes, and civic stoneworker identity.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- A harsh low-to-mid-height warning language made from uneven red cloth stakes, old horn forks, broken shield tokens, blade fragments, and rough warning posts.
- Red cloth stakes should use broad torn shapes that read from MMO camera distance without becoming arrows, flags for capture gameplay, or UI-like route indicators.
- Horn markers should use one or two dominant horn or blunt bone shapes lashed to a stake, rock, or low base. Keep them sparse and non-graphic.
- Broken shield tokens should feel like discarded or claimed battlefield scraps: large shield shards, dull rims, rope ties, and chipped red paint.
- Blade fragments should be heavy, dull, and embedded into posts, stones, or mud bases as intimidation dressing, not usable weapons, pickups, or loot.
- Rough warning posts should be scorched timber or raw split logs, unevenly cut, with hide wraps, rope lashings, and one clear dominant shape.
- Mixed marker clusters should have one dominant warning silhouette and two or three supporting pieces, not dense trophy piles or fence lines.
- Review composition rows should read as non-shipping planning rows and must not be mistaken for approved final art.

Model as real geometry only if a later task approves production:

- Main stakes, large cloth silhouettes, horn forks, shield shards, blade fragments, rough posts, heavy lashings, large stones, blackened clamps, and broad mud/ash bases.

Keep in textures, normals, or baked detail:

- Fine cloth weave, fray, rope fibers, tiny chips, scratches, soot speckles, ash flecks, wood grain, shield dents, paint chips, horn rings, bone pitting, and mud streaks.

Avoid:

- Graphic gore, fresh remains, dense skull piles, readable text, UI arrows, glowing markers, polished civic signs, blue runes, refined cave-town stonework, warm hearth motifs, elegant highland wayfinding, or a one-note red-only palette.

## LOD Plan

All important future static modules require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full marker silhouette, major cloth shapes, horn/bone forms, shield shards, blade fragments, rough posts, broad lashings, large base stones, mud/ash grounding, and readable Blood Axe red/black accents.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify lashing loops, cloth edge cuts, shield bevels, horn/bone bevels, blade bevels, post cuts, and minor base forms.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve the red cloth read, horn/shield/blade/post silhouette, and Giant-scale footprint while removing back-side accents, small ties, secondary cuts, and minor base stones.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve broad stake, cloth, horn, shield, blade, or post silhouettes only, with limited base mass and a simple red/black faction read.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, paint chips, horn rings, bone pitting, cloth weave, and fine wood grain.
2. Minor rope fibers, secondary lashings, tiny knots, small cloth holes, and extra wraps.
3. Small shield splinters, small blade nicks, minor base pebbles, and small horn chips.
4. Back-facing cloth tears, underside detail, interior shield dents, and non-visible post cuts.
5. Small bevels on lashings, stake cuts, shield rims, blade fragments, and base stones.
6. Secondary support pieces in mixed clusters.

Never reduce the primary warning silhouette, Blood Axe red/black accent read, Giant-scale marker size, or approach rhythm before removing small detail.
