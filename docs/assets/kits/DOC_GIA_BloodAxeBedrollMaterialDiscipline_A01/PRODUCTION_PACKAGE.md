`DOC_GIA_BloodAxeBedrollMaterialDiscipline_A01` defines the shared material rules for future Blood Axe bedroll and hide-bundle children: hide rolls, hide-roll stacks, open hide rolls, rough bedding pallets, ground bedding, fur sleep layers, tied bundles, tied bundle sets, frame-strapped bundles, rawhide lashings, rope coil ties, stake anchors, shelter-adjacent piles, review rows, scale rows, and LOD/collision policy rows.

The material read should feel crude, hostile, field-made, dirty, and practical. It should use rough hide, patched leather, sparse fur edges, thick rope, rawhide lashings, scorched timber, blackened iron, soot, ash, mud, and oxide red cloth as restrained production language. The first read should be static Blood Axe shelter dressing and camp packing, not active loot, not usable bedding, not inventory storage, and not finalized shader direction.

Blood Axe hostile Giant dressing must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant cave-town materials. Neutral/civilized Giants retain skilled stonework, hidden highland cave towns, blue-gray civic masonry, terraces, waterworks, warm hearth light, restrained blue runes, orderly domestic craft, and refined settlement bedding. Blood Axe bedroll material may look rough and threatening, but it is not the default Giant culture material language.

## Silhouette Notes

Materials must support the bedroll and bundle silhouettes instead of competing with them. The first read should remain Giant-scale rough hide rolls, low soot-dark bedding, lumpy tied bundles, thick rawhide lashings, rope coils, stake anchors, and broad shelter-edge piles.

Material silhouette support:

- Rough hide and patched leather should read as broad folded planes, flattened roll bodies, large flap edges, and soot-dark bedding masses. Avoid dense stitch fields or tiny patch noise.
- Sparse fur edges should break silhouettes in broad strips only. Individual strands belong in future texture or normal detail.
- Rope and rawhide should read as thick Giant-tied wraps, broad lashings, heavy knots, and dirty functional bindings. Fine fibers, stitch marks, and pores belong in future texture maps.
- Scorched timber should support frame-strapped bundles, stake anchors, and rough pallet supports with broad burned ends and large worn faces. Fine wood grain belongs in future texture or normal detail.
- Blackened iron should appear as occasional crude rings, dull clamps, tie loops, or strap hardware. It should not become a dense rivet field.
- Ash and mud should ground the props through wide low-value contact zones, pale ash deposits, charcoal smears, compressed earth, and dirty undersides. They should not form route lines or gameplay trails.
- Oxide red cloth should be sparse and broad: torn tags, tied wraps, dirty repair strips, and rubbed red marks. It must not become a full red wash, objective highlight, UI flag, or loot rarity cue.

Excluded silhouette and material reads:

- Neutral/civilized Giant blue-gray civic masonry, refined cave-town bedding, terrace and waterwork forms, warm hearth domestic cues, polished highland textiles, restrained blue rune inlays, civic mason symbols, peaceful clan bedding, readable text, UI arrows, objective symbols, magic glyph panels, glow-based path cues, dense gore, trophy curtains, or micro-detail fields.

## Materials and Color Palette

Primary bedroll material families:

- Rough hide and patched leather: smoked hide, dark leather, broad worn patches, folded edges, pressure marks, dirty underside values, and large repair planes.
- Sparse fur edges: dirty cream, gray tan, charcoal tips, matted clumps, and old worn bedding edges used sparingly.
- Rope and rawhide: thick rawhide strips, sinew cord, rough rope lashings, heavy knots, smoke staining, and muddy contact grime.
- Scorched timber: blackened pallet supports, crude carry frames, rough stake bodies, weathered rails, and old burned support wood.
- Blackened iron: dull rings, old clamps, strap loops, tie hardware, hammered dark steel, rubbed edge wear, and crude repair bands.
- Ash and mud: cold ash, charcoal smears, packed earth, trampled mud, dust, grease stains, and dark compressed underside patches.

## LOD Plan

This package creates no LOD assets. Future material use must remain readable across LOD0-LOD3 on separately scoped consuming meshes.

- LOD0: full broad material contrast, rough hide folds, patched leather, sparse fur edge, rope/rawhide lashing, soot and ash grounding, mud contact zones, controlled oxide red cloth, scorched timber, and sparse blackened iron.
- LOD1: preserve major material zones and Blood Axe red/dark read; reduce small cloth tears, secondary lashing loops, paint chips, tiny cracks, ash flecks, mud droplets, timber nicks, pitted iron marks, fur-edge cuts, and hide pore detail.
- LOD2: keep hide versus bedding, rope/rawhide versus ash/mud, timber versus blackened iron, and red ownership accents readable through broad masks and value grouping; remove most non-silhouette material-driven geometry.
- LOD3: preserve the primary bedroll/bundle read: dark rough hide, broad bedding mass, thick strap/lashing value, cold ash base, dull mud grounding, and sparse oxide red ownership beat.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, fine cracks, cloth weave, rope fibers, fur strands, hide pores, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud droplets, pitting, small paint chips, timber grain, and leather pores.
2. Small knots, little hide patch cuts, secondary cloth holes, minor hide repairs, small lashing loops, and small timber splinters.
3. Small iron nicks, minor straps, non-silhouette red fragments, and tiny fur-edge breaks.
4. Hidden backside grime, underside detail, interior recess marks, and camera-hidden residue.
5. Minor support bevels, shallow groove subdivisions, small cloth fold geometry, and secondary roll-layer cuts.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the broad material zones that carry the Blood Axe bedroll and bundle read.

Never reduce the no-default-emissive baseline, hostile Blood Axe material identity, Giant-scale readability, static dressing purpose, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant materials before reducing small detail.
