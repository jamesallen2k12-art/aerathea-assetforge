## Silhouette Notes

- Primary read: two squat, broad support stones arranged as a low pair with a clear visual relationship to a dry channel slab.
- Each stone should feel heavy, blunt, and Giant-handled, with broad chipped planes, flattened bearing faces, muddy/ashy base contact, and a low wedge or block profile.
- The pair should sit visually below the channel slab read. It supports massing and scale, not route framing or traversal.
- Use asymmetry between the two supports: one may be wider, lower, more buried, more chipped, or slightly offset, but both should remain part of one paired visual composition.
- The negative space between supports is composition only. It is not a path gate, approved clearance, traversal proof, interaction affordance, or collision-valid passage.
- Favor two dominant block forms plus broad contact stains rather than many small rocks.
- Optional accents may include one blackened iron scrape, a crude wedge scar, a rawhide stain, or a faded oxide red smear, kept secondary to stone mass.
- Soot and cold ash should gather around underside contact, bearing faces, and sheltered chips. No glow, liquid, wet shine, rune panels, UI arrows, or active channel marks.

Model as future real geometry only if a later task separately authorizes asset creation:
