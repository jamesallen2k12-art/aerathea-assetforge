## 3. Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette:

- A stable stepped pile with a broad base, mid-height massing, and a lower front edge that climbs toward a heavier back or side anchor.
- Two to four Giant-scale crate forms should create the structural read: one larger base crate or flat crate stack, one offset upper crate, and optional open or partial crate form with contents hidden or covered.
- Three to six sack forms should soften gaps between crates, with sagging bases, tied necks, and varied rotations.
- Two to four baskets should add thick round or oval woven shapes at the pile edges, never arranged like a vendor stall.
- Two to five rope coils or lashing bundles should sit as secondary readable accents, tying the pile together without becoming dense per-rope detail.
- One covered bundle is required and should sit as a broad hide or canvas-covered mass wedged into the base or side of the pile.
- Restrained oxide red Blood Axe accents should appear as small cloth tags, rubbed paint strokes, or tied scraps on only a few surfaces.

Shape hierarchy:

1. First read: broad Giant-scale stepped storage pile.
2. Second read: crate block structure and covered-bundle anchor.
3. Third read: heavy sacks and thick basket forms filling gaps.
4. Fourth read: rope coils, lashings, knots, blackened iron straps, and restrained red accents.

- LOD0: full stepped cluster with crate base, offset crates, heavy sacks, thick baskets, rope coils, one covered bundle, major straps, large knots, broad folds, ground contact, and restrained Blood Axe red accents.

- LOD2: merge smaller crate offsets, simplify sack folds into normals, reduce rope coils to thicker simplified loops, collapse minor basket weave into normal detail, remove most hidden back-side detail, and keep one or two clear red accents.
