## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette families:

- Low threshold cairns: squat stacked stone masses made from a few large rocks, broad enough for Giant scale but low enough to read as threshold dressing rather than gates.
- Paired standing stones: two rough upright stones with uneven height and spacing. They may frame a visual threshold, but they must not read as a functional doorway, objective frame, nav gate, or encounter trigger.
- Cave remnant clusters: mixed low stones, partial cairns, broken slabs, cold ash bases, old rope, dull horn pieces, and weathered red cloth left near a cave edge.
- Red cloth threshold markers: broad oxide red strips, knots, ties, or draped bands attached to stone or short stakes. Cloth is a static warning accent only, not a flag animation, UI cue, or quest marker.
- Review rows: clean, non-shipping rows that compare low cairn heights, paired-stone spacing, red cloth usage, and Giant scale readability.

Future geometry, if separately approved, should model main stone bodies, broad chipped stone planes, large cloth silhouettes, heavy rope knots, short rough stakes, large horn shapes, and broad ash/mud bases. Fine cracks, stone pitting, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, frayed threads, rope fibers, mud streaks, chipped red paint, and small scratches should stay in textures, normals, AO, or shared masks.

Avoid UI arrows, readable text, carved route labels, objective symbols, magic glyph panels, glow-based entrance cues, blue civic runes, refined cave-town masonry, warm neutral settlement cues, dense skull piles, graphic gore, and micro-detail that would not survive mid-poly MMO production.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate implementation signoff.

- LOD0: full low cairn forms, paired standing-stone massing, broad red cloth silhouettes, major rope lashings, short stake bodies, ash/mud bases, and main cave remnant cluster shapes.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small bevels, secondary chips, cloth edge cuts, rope lashing subdivisions, minor base undercuts, and hidden backside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify stones into larger planes, merge small cairn stones, flatten cloth folds, remove non-silhouette accent pieces, and simplify ash bases.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve low threshold read, paired-stone silhouette, broad red cloth beat, and cave-edge remnant rhythm only.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, paint chips, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashings, secondary cloth tears, little stone wedges, small horn chips, minor iron nicks, and tiny knots.
3. Back-facing stones, underside bevels, hidden contact cuts, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Secondary marker tokens, duplicate foot stones, small cloth holes, and minor decorative cuts.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and optional review-row helper detail.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary cairn, paired-stone, cloth-marker, or remnant-cluster silhouette.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, static threshold readability only, Blood Axe red warning accents, or the main cave-approach marker silhouettes before removing small detail.
