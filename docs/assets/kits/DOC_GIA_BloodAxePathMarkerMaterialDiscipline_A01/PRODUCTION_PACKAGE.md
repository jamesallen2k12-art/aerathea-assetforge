`DOC_GIA_BloodAxePathMarkerMaterialDiscipline_A01` defines the shared material rules for future Blood Axe path-marker children: cairn single markers, cairn clusters, cloth stake markers, low red rag markers, bone/horn warnings, broken shield markers, ash-stained bases, mixed clusters, review rows, scale rows, and future LOD/collision planning rows.

The material read should feel crude, hostile, field-made, and weathered by camp movement. It should use rough field stone, scorched timber, hide, rope, blackened iron, ash, mud, oxide red cloth, dull bone, and horn as restrained production language. The first read should be static Blood Axe route dressing and camp warning residue, not an active magic device, not a UI marker, and not a finalized shader direction.

Blood Axe hostile Giant dressing must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant cave-town materials. Neutral/civilized Giants retain skilled stonework, hidden highland cave towns, blue-gray civic masonry, terraces, bridges, waterworks, warm hearth light, restrained blue runes, and orderly stoneworker craft. Blood Axe path-marker material may look rough and threatening, but it is not the default Giant culture material language.

## Silhouette Notes

Materials must support the path-marker silhouettes instead of competing with them. The first read should remain Giant-scale crude cairns, short scorched stakes, broad oxide red cloth, blunt horn or bone warning accents, broken shield scrap, and broad ash/mud grounding.

Material silhouette support:

- Rough field stone should read as a few large field rocks, stacked slabs, chipped planes, soot-dark contact pockets, and mud-packed bases. Avoid pebble carpets and dense crack networks.
- Scorched timber should support stake silhouettes with blackened ends, cut pole faces, burned knots, and broad smoke staining. Fine wood grain belongs in future texture or normal detail.
- Hide and rope should read as thick Giant-tied wraps, broad lashings, heavy knots, and dirty functional bindings. Fine fibers, stitch marks, and pores belong in future texture maps.
- Blackened iron should appear as occasional crude clamps, old rings, shield rims, hammered scrap plates, or rough repair straps. It should not become a dense rivet field.
- Ash and mud should ground the marker through wide low-value contact zones, pale ash deposits, charcoal smears, compressed earth, and dirty stone feet. They should not form route lines or gameplay trails.
- Oxide red cloth should be sparse and broad: torn strips, tied wraps, dirty knots, and chipped paint-like warning accents. It must not become a full red wash or objective highlight.
- Dull bone and horn should be sparse, large, worn, non-graphic secondary warning accents. They should not become skull piles, gore decoration, trophy curtains, or repeated surface patterning.

Excluded silhouette and material reads:

- Neutral/civilized Giant blue-gray civic masonry, refined cave-town stone blocks, terrace and waterwork forms, warm hearth settlement cues, polished highland guide markers, restrained blue rune inlays, civic mason symbols, peaceful clan wayfinding, readable text, UI arrows, objective symbols, magic glyph panels, glow-based path cues, dense gore, or micro-detail fields.

## Materials and Color Palette

Primary path-marker material families:

- Rough field stone: dark highland field rock, weathered granite, soot-stained boulders, cracked cairn slabs, chipped planes, large contact shadows, and mud-packed stone bases.
- Scorched timber: blackened stake ends, charred raw pole cuts, smoke-dark knots, dirty cut faces, splintered large edges, and old burned support wood.
- Hide and rope: thick hide ties, rawhide strips, dirty leather wraps, sinew cords, rough rope lashings, heavy knots, smoke staining, and muddy contact grime.
- Blackened iron: dull clamps, old rings, shield rims, hammered scrap plates, soot-stained dark steel, rubbed edge wear, and crude repair straps.
- Ash and mud: cold ash, charcoal smears, packed earth, trampled mud, burned grass residue, dark compressed patches, and pale dry deposits around marker bases.
- Oxide red cloth: faded maroon strips, dirty red wraps, torn cloth tabs, chipped red paint marks, soot-stained edges, and controlled Blood Axe warning beats.

- LOD0: full broad material contrast, rough field stone planes, scorched timber, soot and ash grounding, mud contact zones, controlled oxide red cloth, thick hide/rope wraps, sparse blackened iron, and sparse dull bone/horn accents.
- LOD1: preserve major material zones and Blood Axe red/black read; reduce small cloth tears, secondary lashing loops, paint chips, tiny cracks, ash flecks, mud droplets, timber nicks, pitted iron marks, horn rings, and bone pores.
- LOD2: keep stone versus cloth, timber versus ash/mud, iron versus horn/bone, and red warning accents readable through broad masks and value grouping; remove most non-silhouette material-driven geometry.
- LOD3: preserve the primary path-marker read: dark rough stone, scorched stake silhouette, cold ash base value, dull mud grounding, sparse oxide red warning beat, and one or two major accent families.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, fine cracks, cloth weave, rope fibers, hide pores, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud droplets, pitting, small paint chips, timber grain, horn rings, and bone pores.
2. Small knots, little stone chips, secondary cloth cuts, minor hide repairs, small lashing loops, and small timber splinters.
3. Small horn or bone tokens, tiny iron nicks, minor straps, and non-silhouette scrap bits.
4. Hidden backside grime, underside detail, interior recess marks, and camera-hidden residue.
5. Minor plate bevels, shallow groove subdivisions, small cloth fold geometry, and secondary rock-plane cuts.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the broad material zones that carry the Blood Axe path-marker read.

Never reduce the no-default-emissive baseline, hostile Blood Axe material identity, Giant-scale readability, static route-dressing purpose, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant materials before reducing small detail.
