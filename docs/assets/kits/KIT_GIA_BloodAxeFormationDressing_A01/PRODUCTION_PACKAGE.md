`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeFormationDressing_A01` defines how Blood Axe Giant silhouettes, banners, weapons, shields, trophies, forge elements, camp backdrops, and review markers should be composed for non-gameplay visual readability. It is a planning package for concept sheets, review-scene staging, screenshot composition, and future static dressing references. It is not an encounter design, AI group plan, combat formation, wave layout, spawn set, rank system, patrol route, nav setup, or final playable scene.

Blood Axe visual language must remain a hostile Giant sub-faction read. Red banners, blackened iron, dark steel, scorched leather, rough hide, soot, ash, and sparse trophies belong here. Neutral/civilized Giant culture remains separate: hidden mountain settlements, master stonework, blue-gray masonry, terraces, waterworks, warm hearth light, restrained blue runes, and civic stoneworker motifs must not be absorbed into this package.

## Silhouette Notes

Formation dressing should read as a staged hostile Blood Axe Giant composition, not as a tactical gameplay diagram. Use visual groupings and depth bands only to control camera readability.

Primary silhouette goals:

- Preserve towering Giant mass first: broad shoulders, thick necks, huge hands, heavy feet, and grounded stance inherited from `SK_GIA_Base_A01`.
- Use male and female Giant height silhouettes as scale anchors, with male 470 cm figures carrying the tallest intimidation reads and female 442 cm figures preserving the approved Giant range.
- Keep grouped silhouettes readable through alternating head/shoulder heights, weapon angles, banner poles, shield blocks, and negative space.
- Use banner bearers as vertical beats, not as gameplay objectives or aura sources.
- Use shield carriers as broad foreground blocks, not as active shield mechanics.
- Use weapon height markers for axe, hammer, hook spear, cleaver, bow, and banner comparisons, not attack-reach definitions.
- Use trophy carriers, forge guards, camp sentries, and raiders as visual lanes only; do not imply final troop roles.
- Use camp gates, forge silhouettes, warning cloth, scrap racks, and trophy markers as background depth only.

Composition readability rules:

- Avoid tangents where banner poles line up exactly with heads, horns, weapon tips, or camp posts.
- Leave visible air between the Giant shoulder mass and the tallest banner/weapon silhouettes.
- Keep shield wall reads as broad shape blocks with red/black material accents; do not create a seamless solid wall that hides Giant scale.
- Place trophy and forge dressing off the primary head line so the character read remains dominant.
- Keep red cloth as repeated accents, not a full one-color field.
- Do not use graphic gore, dense skull curtains, or unreadable trophy clutter.

- Tight visual cluster gutter: 80-140 cm of visible air between major shoulder/weapon silhouettes when viewed from the review camera.

- Carried banner visual top target: roughly 650-780 cm from ground when used as the tallest read; final banner dimensions remain owned by banner packages.
- Weapon height markers should reference existing Blood Axe armory ranges: double axe 360-430 cm, crusher hammer 300-380 cm, hook spear 420-560 cm, longbow 360-460 cm, and camp banners above Giant head height where visually needed.
- Backdrop scale should keep camp gates, forge racks, and shelter forms Giant-built. Smallfolk should read as intruders in an oversized hostile camp if ever shown for scale.

Do not use these numbers as gameplay reach, collision, nav, spawn, or combat radius values.

## LOD Plan

All approved future static dressing meshes need LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full visual read for Giant height markers, banner poles, red cloth blocks, weapon top-lines, shield blocks, trophy silhouettes, forge/camp backdrop forms, spacing ground marks, and review-frame markers.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small strap loops, minor banner tears, shield bevels, tiny trophy undercuts, secondary pole wraps, small ground ticks, and backside camp dressing.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; merge shield and trophy subforms, simplify weapon proxy outlines, flatten cloth folds, reduce forge rack side detail, and collapse minor markers into texture.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve Giant height read, banner verticals, shield block silhouette, weapon top-line, red cloth rhythm, and foreground/midground/backdrop separation.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny rivets, stitches, scratches, pitting, soot speckles, paint chips, and cloth fray.
2. Small straps, rope wraps, chain segments, minor hanging pieces, and small trophy chips.
3. Secondary shield bevels, weapon proxy notches, and inner camp rack details.
4. Back-side camp clutter, forge side dressing, underside forms, and camera-hidden details.
5. Secondary marker ticks and frame labels, if any are approved later.

Never reduce Giant scale markers, banner top-line, shield block silhouette, primary weapon height read, or hostile Blood Axe red/black identity before removing small detail.
