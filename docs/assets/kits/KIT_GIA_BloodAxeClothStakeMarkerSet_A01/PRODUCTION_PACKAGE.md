The set should read as rough Giant-made visual route dressing: scorched timber stakes, hide wraps, coarse rope, and broad oxide red cloth strips placed with uneven spacing and uneven heights.

Blood Axe dressing must stay separate from neutral and civilized Giant culture. The set provides static visual rhythm along Blood Axe camp paths, shelter approaches, rough turns, and trampled route edges. It helps an environment read as hostile and occupied without adding gameplay logic.

## 3. Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- Two to four rough timber stakes arranged as a loose, uneven row, never as a straight fence.
- Stake heights should vary enough to avoid a manufactured barricade read.
- Each stake should have one strong cloth element, such as a broad torn strip, sagging rag, wrapped pennant, or tied red warning swatch.
- Cloth pieces should be broad and readable from MMO camera distance, with limited cuts and large tears rather than many thin ribbons.
- Timber tops may be sharpened, broken, split, or charred, but should stay simple and readable.
- Hide wraps and rope ties should sit at large attachment points, not as dense micro-detail.
- Ground contact should feel trampled, muddy, and ash-darkened where the stakes enter the earth.

Shape rules:

- Model the stake shafts, major cloth planes, large knots or wrap bands, and the largest broken/split tips as real geometry in any later approved source task.
- Reserve cloth weave, fine fray, stitch marks, tiny splinters, soot speckles, wood grain, paint chips, and mud streaks for textures or normal maps.
- Keep the set visually lower and more ground-adjacent than `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeBannerLine_A01`.
- Do not add banner spans, rope-line thresholds, tall standard poles, readable text, arrows, icons, glowing symbols, skull piles, graphic gore, or refined carved signage.

## 10. LOD Plan

All future important variants require LOD0-LOD3 before any shipping use is approved.

- LOD0: full stake lean, major cloth planes, broad tears, large wraps, clear tie knots, readable split tips, and low ash/mud base detail.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify cloth edge cuts, merge secondary folds, reduce knot bevels, and simplify stake-tip splits.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve stake count, uneven row rhythm, red cloth read, and largest wraps while removing minor underside/back-side details.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the stake-row silhouette and broad red cloth blocks; remove small ties, ground chips, minor tears, and secondary folds.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, fiber strands, soot flecks, paint chips, scratches, and small splinters.
2. Small cloth holes, minor fray cuts, and secondary cloth folds.
3. Minor rope wraps, extra knot loops, and small hide tabs.
4. Stake bevels, back-facing cloth thickness, and small ground undercuts.
5. Ground pad detail and non-silhouette base breakup.

Never reduce the readable count of stakes, uneven spacing, hostile Blood Axe red cloth identity, or loose path rhythm before removing micro-detail.
