`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeMovedCampCairnLine_A01` defines a sparse, broken line of abandoned Blood Axe cairn remnants. The kit should read as environmental history from a hostile Giant nomad camp that moved on: half-collapsed cairns, cold ash gaps, torn cloth traces, trampled mud, and broken memory clusters left behind by a raider group.

The line is not a functional trail system. It must never read as a player route, waypoint chain, tracking mechanic, UI path, encounter lane, patrol guide, spawn guide, or navigation helper. Its purpose is to give a place a past without turning that past into a gameplay instruction.

Blood Axe identity must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Use crude raider-made stone, soot, ash, mud, oxide red cloth, rawhide, rope, and sparse dull horn or bone accents. Do not borrow neutral Giant cave-town masonry, blue-gray civic stonework, terrace or waterwork motifs, warm hearth settlement identity, peaceful highland wayfinding, or restrained blue rune culture.

This package creates no meshes, textures, material instances, source folders, game assets, validators, runtime files, startup placement, source concept edits, final visual approval, or first implementation target selection.

## Silhouette Notes

- The overall line should feel interrupted and old: short visual beats with empty gaps, collapsed stones, ash-dark ground, and occasional cloth remnants.
- Sparse line segments should use 2-4 low cairns or stone piles with uneven spacing and varied collapse, not a neat sequence.
- Broken memory clusters should group one dominant collapsed cairn with one or two secondary stones, old lashings, and ash-dark base shapes.
- Ash gaps should be low, flat, cold camp residue between visible cairn beats. They must not become glowing trails, decal arrows, objective rings, or readable symbols.
- Cloth remnants should be small, fixed, weathered oxide red strips tied to stones, trapped under rocks, or snagged on a short stake. They should not look like banners, route flags, UI color coding, or active signals.
- Review-only composition rows should show spacing and readability options without becoming in-world approved props.

Model real geometry in later approved work only for major stone masses, large collapsed stones, broad cloth silhouettes, heavy lashings, short stake remnants, and low base forms. Reserve tiny chips, hairline cracks, cloth weave, fray, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud streaks, lichen, rope fibers, and paint wear for future texture, normal, AO, or mask detail.

Avoid dense pebble trails, skull piles, graphic gore, readable text, clear arrows, glowing marks, polished civic stonework, blue runes, warm hearth motifs, or repeated identical marker spacing.

## LOD Plan

All future important child assets require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate shipping use.

- LOD0: full sparse segment, major collapsed stones, broad cloth remnants, large lashings, ash gap bases, short stake remnants, and optional sparse accents.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, minor stone cuts, cloth edge cuts, small ties, underside contacts, and base undercuts.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; merge small supporting stones, simplify collapsed clusters, flatten ash bases, and preserve the broken line rhythm through large values only.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve major cairn masses, missing-gap rhythm, one or two oxide red cloth beats, and the low abandoned-camp footprint.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny chips, hairline cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, fray cuts, scratches, lichen, and paint chips.
2. Small rope fibers, secondary lashings, minor knots, little stones, and hidden underside detail.
3. Small cloth holes, small horn or bone chips, minor iron scraps, and non-silhouette debris.
4. Backside detail, interior overlaps, duplicate base stones, and secondary decorative cuts.
5. Small bevels on supporting stones and optional review-row helper detail.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the main cairn remnant and cluster silhouettes.

Never reduce the Giant-scale remnant read, the broken/missing rhythm, the abandoned-camp history, or Blood Axe red/black material identity before removing small detail.
