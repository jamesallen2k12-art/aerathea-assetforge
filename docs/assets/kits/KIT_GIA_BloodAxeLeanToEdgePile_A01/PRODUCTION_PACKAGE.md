## 1. Art Direction Summary

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeLeanToEdgePile_A01` is a narrow, shelter-edge pile of Giant-scale hide rolls, rough bedding folds, tied hide bundles, rope, rawhide lashings, and stake anchors. It is designed to run along the side edge of a Blood Axe lean-to or hide shelter without blocking the main Giant entrance.

The read should be hostile, practical, and raider-camp specific: low, heavy, dirty, quickly shoved aside, and built from rough materials carried by the Blood Axe Tribe. Keep this visual language separate from neutral or civilized Giant culture. Do not use refined highland bedding, civic stoneworker symbols, clean cave-town craft, blue-gray monumental masonry, warm hearth comfort, restrained blue runes, or civilized terrace-settlement language as the default read.

This is static camp dressing only. It is not a usable bed, inventory container, loot source, resource node, pickup, interaction object, nav/pathfinding rule, collision proxy, cover marker, or gameplay system.

## 2. Gameplay Purpose

The purpose is environmental storytelling and scale reinforcement for a hostile Blood Axe Giant camp. The pile supports a lived-in raider shelter edge while preserving a readable main entrance lane for a 442 cm female Giant and a 470 cm male Giant.

The "does not block the main entrance" requirement is composition guidance only. It does not define nav/pathfinding behavior, AI routing, blocker rules, collision setup, cover tagging, spawn logic, or interaction logic.

## 3. Silhouette Notes

- Overall silhouette: long, narrow, side-hugging clutter mass with uneven low rolls and one or two higher lumpy bundle peaks.
- Primary shape language: flattened cylinders, sagging hide folds, blunt tied knots, thick rope coils, rough stake points, and heavy rawhide bands.
- Composition: heaviest mass sits toward the rear side of the lean-to; lower rolls and rope sit forward so the shelter opening remains visually clear.
- Edge fit: pile should read as tucked along a shelter edge, not spread across the threshold or main approach.
- Readability: avoid a single amorphous mound. Separate 3 to 5 large readable masses with gaps, straps, and value breaks.
- Micro-detail: fur strands, rope fibers, hide grain, scratches, soot speckles, fray, and mud spatters belong in texture, normal, and AO detail, not dense modeled geometry.

## 4. Scale Notes

Giant scale lock is fixed:

- Female Giant baseline: 442 cm / 14 ft 6 in.
- Male Giant baseline: 470 cm / 15 ft 5 in.

Recommended visual footprint for the kit:

The pile should feel small enough for a Giant to step past, but large enough to dwarf human-scale clutter. Do not shrink props to human camp proportions.
