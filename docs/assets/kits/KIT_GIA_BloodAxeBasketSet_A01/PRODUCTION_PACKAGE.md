## Art Direction Summary

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeBasketSet_A01` defines a reusable docs-only Blood Axe Giant basket set for hostile camp storage dressing. The set contains four static basket shapes:

- Tall basket.
- Squat round basket.
- Broken-rim basket.

## Silhouette Notes

The set should read clearly as four related but distinct Giant-scale basket forms:

- Tall basket: vertical oval or slightly tapered cylinder, high rim, wide base, thick vertical ribs, and broad woven bands.
- Squat round basket: low, heavy, circular or rounded-square footprint with a thick lip and compressed ground contact.
- Broken-rim basket: damaged but static basket with one large missing rim segment, bent ribs, and a readable broken upper contour; it is not a destructible object.
- Half-covered basket: basket body partly covered by a static rigid rawhide mat or split-timber lid panel, leaving a shadowed non-specific opening.

Model real geometry for the main basket bodies, thick rims, large vertical ribs, broad weave bands, heavy base rings, major broken-rim chunks, rigid cover slab or mat, rawhide tie bands, and large knots. Keep micro-weave, small fibers, scratches, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud spots, minor fray, small chips, and faded red paint wear in textures, normals, and AO.

Avoid human-scale picnic baskets, clean woven market goods, readable labels, price tags, treasure-basket language, visible loot, visible food piles, quest-object silhouettes, vendor display organization, UI glyphs, glowing markers, neutral/civilized Giant craft, and dense per-fiber geometry.

## LOD Plan

All future mesh work for this set requires LOD0-LOD3.

LOD0:

- Four distinct basket silhouettes with thick rims, major ribs, broad weave bands, heavy bases, large rawhide ties, readable broken-rim damage, fixed half-cover, grime, and restrained Blood Axe red accents.

LOD1:

- Reduce small rim bevels, secondary ribs, minor weave offsets, small tie tails, small cover bevels, and hidden interior detail while preserving all four basket identities.

LOD2:

- Merge minor weave bands into larger shapes, collapse small knots into normal detail, reduce rib count, simplify broken-rim edges, remove most hidden back and underside detail, and preserve one clear red accent where visible.

LOD3:

- Preserve the tall, squat, broken-rim, and half-covered reads as simple large masses with dark weave, thick rim silhouettes, Giant-scale proportions, and restrained Blood Axe identity. Remove small ties, minor ribs, interior weave, secondary red marks, small chips, and hidden detail.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny fibers, scratches, soot speckles, ash flecks, paint chips, mud specks, and small weave texture.
2. Small tie ends, secondary knots, minor rawhide tabs, and small rim bevels.
3. Secondary weave bands, small rib offsets, hidden interiors, and underside detail.
4. Minor broken-rim chips that do not define the main damaged silhouette.
5. Secondary red accents after the main Blood Axe read remains clear.

Never reduce the primary four-shape identity, Giant-scale rim thickness, broad basket footprints, broken-rim silhouette, half-covered silhouette, or hostile Blood Axe material read before removing small detail.
