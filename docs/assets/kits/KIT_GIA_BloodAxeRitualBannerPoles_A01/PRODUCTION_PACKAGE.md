- Asset name: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeRitualBannerPoles_A01`

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeRitualBannerPoles_A01` defines static ritual banner poles used as Blood Axe warning and memory dressing around abandoned camp edges, ritual-stone approaches, rough cairn paths, and hostile territory thresholds. The kit should read through tall uneven poles, tied cloth strips, rope lashings, stone weights, sparse horn markers, soot, ash, and oxide red cloth without becoming a spell device, faction signal, quest marker, or animated banner system.

Blood Axe visual language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. These poles belong to a brutal raider sub-faction and must not replace Giant cave-town, highland stoneworker, hearth-settlement, terrace, waterwork, civic banner, or restrained blue-rune material language.

## LOD Plan

All important future modules require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full pole profiles, split tops, broad cloth-strip silhouettes, rope lashings, major knots, stone weights, sparse horn markers, and clear ground contact.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce rope wrap segments, simplify stone bevels, merge small cloth tears, and reduce horn-marker bevels.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve tall vertical rhythm, red cloth read, major stone weights, and horn punctuation while removing secondary lashings and small back-side detail.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve pole height, red cloth block, base mass, and cluster read; remove most small hardware, minor knots, interior cuts, and tiny chips.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny fray, small chips, nail heads, soot speckles, and small cloth holes.
2. Secondary rope fibers, small knots, small wraps, and minor ties.
3. Interior cloth cuts, small hanging strips, and secondary torn edges.
4. Small horn chips, small stone undercuts, and minor scrap hooks.
5. Pole bevel complexity, split-top interior cuts, and hidden back-side detail.

Never reduce the Giant-scale pole rhythm, red static cloth read, stone-weight mass, or hostile Blood Axe red/black identity before removing small detail.
