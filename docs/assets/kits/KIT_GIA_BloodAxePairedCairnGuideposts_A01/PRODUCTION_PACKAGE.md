The kit covers close pairs, staggered pairs, cave-threshold pairs, moved-camp pairs, and review-only spacing rows. ## Silhouette Notes

- Close pairs should read as two unequal cairns in a compact visual beat, with asymmetrical height and offset stone profiles. They must not form an arrow, sign, gate, or objective bracket.
- Staggered pairs should place one cairn forward and one set back, creating a memory rhythm through depth and silhouette offset rather than route scripting or lane definition.
- Cave-threshold pairs should frame an old Blood Axe threshold visually through rough stone mass, ash, and cloth remnants, while avoiding gate logic, traversal metrics, or collision assumptions.
- Moved-camp pairs should look partially collapsed, weathered, or left behind after the Blood Axe camp moved, using shifted stones and dead cloth rather than fresh marker clarity.
- Review-only spacing rows should be visibly non-shipping planning arrangements used to compare pair distance, silhouette balance, and Giant scale.

Future modeled geometry should focus on a few large stones per cairn, broad cloth silhouettes, major rope or rawhide wraps, heavy stone contact forms, and low ash bases. ## LOD Plan

All future important child assets require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate shipping use.

- LOD0: full paired cairn composition, major stone forms, broad bevels, pair spacing read, cloth accents, major wraps, and low ash/mud bases.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, cloth edge cuts, minor wrap subdivisions, optional accents, and hidden contact detail.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; merge smaller upper stones, simplify broad planes, flatten ash bases, and preserve only the strongest red cloth beats.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the two-cairn read, major height contrast, base footprint, and Blood Axe red/black material identity.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, fray cuts, rope fibers, paint chips, lichen specks, scratches, and pitting.
2. Small knots, secondary lashings, tiny cloth holes, little wedge stones, minor horn or bone chips, and small iron nicks.
3. Hidden underside detail, back-facing stone contacts, interior overlaps, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Optional accent pieces, small duplicate foot stones, secondary cloth tears, and non-silhouette debris.
5. Secondary stone bevel density and minor broken-plane cuts.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary paired-cairn silhouettes while preserving visual route-memory readability and Blood Axe material identity.

Never reduce the two-marker composition, Giant-scale readability, controlled oxide red cloth beat, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant culture before removing small detail.
