- Status: docs-only production package ready

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeBedrollHideBundles_A01` defines Giant-scale rough sleeping and packing clutter for Blood Axe camps. The kit should read as hostile camp-life dressing: oversized hide rolls, rough bedding mats, tied travel bundles, rawhide lashings, rope coils, ground stakes, and shelter-adjacent piles placed near lean-tos, hide tents, cooking areas, and war-camp paths.

The kit must stay non-graphic and practical. It should imply a raider camp that moves, sleeps, packs, and raids, without becoming a loot pile, crafting resource, inventory container, usable bed, interactable storage, or cloth-simulation test. Blood Axe remains a hostile Giant sub-faction and must not replace neutral/civilized Giant cave-town, highland nomad, masonry, terrace, warm hearth, or restrained blue-rune culture.

This package is planning only. It does not create source folders, DCC files, proof renders, FBX exports, Unreal Content, material graphs, texture assets, validators, runtime source, collision proxies, physics assets, cloth setup, inventory behavior, loot behavior, resource behavior, interaction behavior, startup placement, final visual approval, or a first DCC target selection.

## Gameplay Purpose

The kit supports environmental storytelling and scale readability for future Blood Axe camp layouts.

Expected use cases:

- Static shelter-adjacent dressing around Blood Axe lean-tos, hide tents, watch areas, cooking pits, forge edges, and path bends.
- Giant-scale living evidence that makes the camp feel inhabited without adding gameplay systems.
- Low-profile foreground and midground clutter that reinforces Blood Axe material identity with rough hide, lashings, soot, mud, and red cloth tags.
- Scale reference beside shelters, beds, door flaps, and camp thresholds using the validated Giant baselines.
- Variant building blocks for later review compositions, with individual children kept reusable and cheap.

Out of scope:

- Sleeping/resting gameplay, storage, inventory, pickup, loot, resource, salvage, crafting, economy, vendor, search, interaction prompts, usable bed behavior, cover rules, nav/pathfinding rules, destructible bundles, cloth simulation, physics setup, wind animation, startup placement, and encounter scripting.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouettes should be broad, low, and readable at MMO camera distance:

- Hide rolls: oversized horizontal cylinders or flattened rolls with thick end caps, tied bands, lumpy hide layers, and one or two broad flap edges.
- Rough bedding: long low mats or hide pads sized for Giants, with uneven bedding thickness, fur-edge breaks, and visible depression shapes.
- Tied bundles: lumpy travel packs bound by two or three thick straps, with large knots, rope coils, and blunt tied ends.
- Lashings: thick rawhide cord, heavy rope, strap bundles, ground stakes, and tie rings used as silhouette-scale details, not dense string fields.
- Shelter-adjacent piles: composed clusters of hide rolls, bedding pads, tied bundles, and stakes that sit beside lean-to walls, tent entrances, or under awnings.

Model real geometry for the main roll masses, bedding pads, large folds, big knots, strap bands, rope coils, ground stakes, and broad pile silhouettes. Use textures and normal maps for hide grain, fur texture, cloth weave, tiny stitches, fine fray, small cracks, soot speckles, mud spatters, rope fibers, and minor scratches.

Avoid dense little straps, excessive dangling strips, gore, readable trophies, many tiny knots, highly shredded cloth, elegant bedding, or cozy neutral Giant camp language.

## LOD Plan

All important future child props require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full silhouette, major hide rolls, bedding mats, broad folds, large knots, strap bands, rope coils, stakes, red tags, and pile composition.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce minor cloth cuts, secondary folds, small rope loops, small strap bevels, underside detail, and small tie hardware.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve roll mass, bedding footprint, pile outline, big straps, red accent blocks, and key rope/knot reads while flattening small folds and back-side details.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the low camp-clutter mass, Giant-scale footprint, and red/dark Blood Axe read. Remove most small hardware, inner lashing detail, tiny tie ends, and fine edge breaks.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, fur strands, rope fibers, small scratches, soot speckles, and pitting.
2. Small cords, secondary straps, tiny knots, and non-structural ties.
3. Minor cloth holes, little flap cuts, and underside folds.
4. Small metal rings, back-facing bundle details, and hidden pile contact detail.
5. Secondary bevels on stakes, small wood chips, and interior bedding layers.

Never reduce the main roll silhouettes, Giant-scale bedding footprint, primary bundle masses, large strap bands, pile outline, or Blood Axe red-cloth read before removing small detail.
