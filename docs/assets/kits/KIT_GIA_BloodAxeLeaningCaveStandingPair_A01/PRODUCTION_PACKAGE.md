`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeLeaningCaveStandingPair_A01` defines a static Blood Axe cave-threshold history kit made from two rough stones with broad chipped planes. One or both stones lean in fixed, deliberately placed poses, as if the threshold was old, stressed by weather, and maintained just enough to keep its warning silhouette. Old cloth ties, rope lashings, or chipped oxide red paint are optional restrained accents.

The kit is a static visual memory marker only. It should imply hostile Blood Axe occupation near a cave mouth, cliff opening, or abandoned shelter edge without defining cave entry behavior, pathing, collision correctness, traversal clearance, route validation, readable text, VFX, audio, or final cave approval.

Blood Axe language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. These leaning stones may feel crude, threatening, ash-stained, and warning-driven, but they must not become the default Giant cave-town identity. Neutral/civilized Giants remain tied to skilled stonework, hidden highland settlements, terraces, bridges, waterworks, hearth warmth, civic blue-gray masonry, and restrained blue rune language.

- LOD0: full two-stone leaning silhouette, distinct lean angles, broad chipped planes, fractured crowns, embedded bases, chock stones, optional cloth ties, major lashings, and grounded mud/ash forms.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, minor base stones, cloth edge cuts, lashing subdivisions, and hidden backside detail while preserving the fixed leaning-pair read.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; simplify each stone into larger planes, merge or remove smaller chock stones, flatten cloth folds, reduce accent geometry, and keep the strongest red Blood Axe beat.
- LOD3: 12-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the two leaning stone masses, main gap shape, broad base footprint, and one Blood Axe color or material accent.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, chipped paint edges, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashing cuts, little knots, secondary cloth tears, small stone wedges, horn chips, iron nicks, and non-silhouette tokens.

4. Secondary fracture cuts, duplicate base stones, minor cloth holes, and optional accent geometry.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and minor base-plane subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary leaning slab silhouettes and the visual gap between them.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, the fixed leaning-pair silhouette, Blood Axe red warning read, or static cave-threshold history before removing small detail.
