## 3. Silhouette Notes

Material rows should support silhouette readability by keeping broad value separation between pale ash edges, dark soot cores, cooled slag, matte charcoal, scorched bark, split raw wood, blackened metal scraps, and restrained red cloth tags. Materials must reinforce primary forms instead of covering them with tiny high-contrast noise.

Policy target:

- Ash should read soft, dusty, and broad, with pale edge planes and darker shoveled centers.
- Soot should read as matte black falloff and dirty accumulation, not active smoke or animated residue.
- Slag should read as cooled crust and dark lumpy forge waste, not shiny ore, loot, salvage, or harvestable metal.
- Charcoal should read as matte fuel residue with a few strong fracture planes, not glittering coal or resource pickup chunks.
- Scorched wood should show charred bark, split interiors, burned ends, and smoke staining without active flame or glow.
- Red cloth should stay sparse and functional as warning tags, ties, or torn Blood Axe accent, not a dominant UI-like color field.

Reject noisy micro-scratches, dense pore fields, excessive bark strokes, bright collection highlights, glowing outlines, UI-like red marks, readable text labels, blue rune marks, or material patterns that obscure ash/slag/charcoal/wood/debris category reads.
