`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeRawhideLashingSet_A01` defines reusable Giant-scale rawhide lashing components for future Blood Axe bedroll and bundle variants: thick rawhide loops, broad strap bands, large knots, and blunt tie wraps. The set should read as harsh camp utility, not refined craft: heavy smoked hide cut into practical binding pieces, darkened by soot and mud, pulled tight over oversized rolls and bundles, and scaled for Blood Axe Giant hands.

This package is docs-only planning. Do not use civilized Giant cave-town masonry, refined highland craft, warm hearth bedding, restrained blue runes, civic stoneworker symbols, clean nomad textiles, or peaceful Giant settlement color language as the default read.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette should be thick, blunt, oversized, and readable from an MMO camera:

- Thick rawhide loops with slightly flattened oval profiles, not thin rope.
- Broad strap bands that wrap across hide rolls or bundle masses with clear width at distance.
- Large knots and tied lumps that read as hand-pulled by Giants rather than delicate cord work.
- Short tie wraps with compressed overlap points, blunt ends, and occasional broad hide tabs.
- Asymmetric placement options so future bundles can avoid repeated identical ties.

Model real geometry for major loop volumes, broad strap bands, large knot masses, blunt tie ends, and any lashing pieces that affect the prop silhouette. Use texture and normal detail only for fine fibers, tiny fray, cut-edge fuzz, small scratches, hide pores, minor stitch marks, soot speckles, dried mud, and surface cracking.

Avoid delicate rope coils, fine tassels, dangling strands, netting, decorative macrame, refined harness craft, symmetrical merchant-bundle presentation, readable loot-bag ties, neutral/civilized Giant craft language, and dense high-frequency fiber geometry that will not hold up as mid-poly game art.

## LOD Plan

All future mesh work for this set requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full readable lashing forms with thick loops, broad strap bands, large knots, blunt tie wraps, visible overlap, and strong Blood Axe rawhide material read.
- LOD1: reduce strap bevels, minor knot folds, small edge cuts, secondary overlap detail, and optional metal hardware while preserving the main lashing silhouette.
- LOD2: merge small folds into normal detail, simplify knot geometry, reduce loop cross-section sides, flatten minor overlaps, and remove optional red flecks if the rawhide read remains clear.
- LOD3: retain only the bold loop, band, knot, or wrap mass needed to read against a bedroll or bundle. Remove small tie ends, edge cuts, minor folds, optional hardware, and all secondary surface forms.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny fibers, fray, pores, stitch marks, scratches, soot speckles, mud flecks, and paint chips.
2. Small cut-edge notches, minor tie ends, tiny overlap lips, and shallow strap bevels.
3. Secondary knot folds, hidden underside shape, small hardware, and back-facing compression detail.
4. Optional red marks or cloth flecks if the parent prop already carries Blood Axe color accents.
5. Minor loop thickness variation after the primary thick lashing read is secured.

Never reduce the broad strap read, large knot mass, primary loop outline, or Giant-scale utility silhouette before removing small detail.
