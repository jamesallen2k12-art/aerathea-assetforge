`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeHideRollStack_A01` defines a low, readable stack of two to five varied Giant-scale hide rolls placed near a Blood Axe shelter wall, lean-to edge, or camp path edge. It should read as rough raider-camp dressing: heavy hides rolled for travel or sleeping, tied with rawhide lashings, stained by soot and mud, and marked only with small oxide-red Blood Axe accents.

This package is visual pile composition only. It must not become a storage container, loot table, resource node, physics pile, pickup object, usable bed, crafting source, searchable bag, cover system object, nav rule, first DCC target, Unreal Content task, startup placement, final visual approval, implementation target selection, or Hermes task.

Blood Axe camp dressing must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Do not use civilized Giant cave-town masonry, terrace craft, warm hearth bedding, restrained blue runes, refined highland textiles, civic stoneworker symbols, or clean nomad color language as the default read.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette should be broad, low, uneven, and readable from an MMO camera:

- Stack count: two to five hide rolls, with at least two visibly different lengths or diameters.
- Base layer: one or two long horizontal rolls forming the main footprint.
- Upper layer: one shorter roll or angled roll offset across the base for an asymmetric Blood Axe camp read.
- Roll shapes: oversized flattened cylinders, lumpy packed hide, heavy end caps, broad flap overlap, and compressed contact points.
- Tie details: two or three thick rawhide bands per prominent roll, one or two large knots, and one small oxide-red cloth tag or wrap for faction read.
- Placement read: pile should feel pushed against a shelter wall, timber support, rock edge, or path-side dirt line without requiring that wall or path as part of the asset.

Model real geometry for main roll masses, visible end caps, broad flap overlaps, large strap bands, big knots, and the stack's contact silhouette. Use texture and normal detail for hide grain, fur strands, rope fibers, tiny stitches, frayed edges, soot speckles, dried mud, small scratches, and worn paint.

Avoid a crate-like rectangular stack, neatly bundled storage, readable treasure pile, resource bundle, cozy bedding stack, symmetrical merchant display, dense dangling strips, gore, trophies, many tiny knots, or fine high-frequency clutter that will not hold up as mid-poly game art.

## LOD Plan

All future mesh work for this stack requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full pile silhouette with varied roll lengths, major end caps, broad flap overlaps, strap bands, large knots, sparse red cloth accent, and clear Blood Axe material read.
- LOD1: reduce small folds, underside detail, minor strap bevels, tiny tie ends, small metal hardware, and secondary contact compression while preserving roll count and pile outline.
- LOD2: merge minor folds into normal detail, simplify knots and strap loops, reduce end-cap geometry, remove most hidden back-side detail, and preserve only the strongest red accent block.
- LOD3: retain the broad low stack mass, Giant-scale footprint, large roll-end reads, and dark hide/red Blood Axe read. Remove small hardware, secondary lashings, tiny tags, inner folds, and minor edge breaks.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, rope fibers, fur strands, hide pores, soot speckles, paint chips, and small scratches.
2. Small tie ends, secondary cords, tiny knots, and minor strap cuts.
3. Minor fold lips, underside folds, and back-facing roll overlaps.
4. Small metal rings, clamp bevels, hidden contact points, and secondary compression detail.
5. Secondary red tags or paint marks if the main Blood Axe accent remains readable.

Never reduce the main roll count, pile footprint, primary horizontal mass, largest end caps, or Giant-scale readability before removing small detail.
