## Silhouette Notes

The primary read is an intimidating upper edge: dark ledge breaks, crude partial rails, tall warning poles, clustered posts, torn red cloth, and simple cutout shapes that sit above the approach as hostile silhouettes rather than as approved playable structures.

Primary silhouette targets:

- Shadowed ledge outlines: broken cliff lips and dark upper shelves that imply height over the route while staying non-traversal planning.
- Crude rail shapes: uneven heavy rail fragments, blunt timber bars, and blackened iron edge pieces that read as rough construction from below.
- Banner poles: tall vertical Blood Axe markers with sparse red cloth or warning pennants, separate from full banner-line asset approval.
- Post clusters: two to five rough logs, spikes, or scrap-reinforced poles grouped as upper-rim intimidation shapes.
- Upper-path silhouettes: distant rough linework suggesting a higher route edge without defining a path width, nav route, patrol route, or gameplay lane.
- Silhouette-only platform cutouts: simple deck-like profile blocks and rail masses used only for composition comparison, not walkable platform approval.
- Scale and review rows: non-shipping silhouette comparisons beside 442 cm and 470 cm Giant references.

## LOD Plan

All important future static modules require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full ledge, rail, pole, post cluster, upper-path, platform-cutout, red cloth, large clamp, and sparse marker silhouettes.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small rail bevels, secondary pole cuts, cloth edge tears, minor lashings, small clamp bevels, and non-silhouette chips.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; merge smaller rails/posts, simplify ledge cuts, flatten secondary cloth shapes, reduce accent geometry, and keep only the main upper-rim read.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the ledge line, crude rail block, pole verticals, post-cluster rhythm, broad red cloth, and simple cutout silhouette.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, chips, nails, fibers, soot speckles, ash flecks, paint chips, and small cracks.
2. Minor lashings, cloth fray, small scrap pieces, non-silhouette knots, and secondary edge breaks.
3. Backside and underside detail not visible from the intended lower approach composition.
4. Secondary rail subdivisions, small bevels, and non-silhouette cuts.
5. Minor Blood Axe accents that do not carry the hostile upper-rim read.
6. Primary ledge outline, rail mass, pole height, post-cluster rhythm, and Giant-scale read only after all secondary detail is reduced.

Never reduce the main upper silhouette, Blood Axe warning rhythm, or Giant-scale relationship before removing small detail.
