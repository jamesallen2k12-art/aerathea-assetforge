## Silhouette Notes

Materials must support the cave remnant cluster silhouettes without becoming the primary marker language. The first read should remain low rough stones, collapsed or half-buried slabs, cold ash and mud grounding, broad old cloth remnants, and rare secondary warning accents.

Material silhouette support:

- Rough highland stone should read as a few large field rocks, low cairn masses, broken slabs, chipped planes, half-buried faces, and heavy cave-grit contact. Avoid dense pebble carpets or refined masonry courses.
- Soot should sit in old burn pockets, underside recesses, slab cracks, cold fire-scar edges, and cloth-contact grime without turning every surface uniformly black.
- Ash should appear as cold pale deposits, broken fire-scar residue, soft base dust, and uneven cave-edge buildup. It must not form a readable ring, arrow, path, objective boundary, or ritual channel.
- Trampled mud should ground the cluster through broad brown-gray patches, dirty stone feet, dried splatter bands, and compressed cave approach dirt, not footprints, trackable travel marks, or path-width proof.
- Cave grit should read as a low dirty transition between stone, ash, and mud, with tiny grit reserved for future texture or normal detail.
- Oxide red cloth should appear as faded tied wraps, draped scraps, trapped strips, buried hems, and chipped red identity marks in sparse beats only.
- Rope and rawhide should read as thick Giant-tied lashings or old bindings only where they affect a future prop silhouette; fine fibers and stitch marks stay in future texture planning.
- Sparse blackened iron should read as rare crude clamps, old rings, short hammered straps, or plate scraps only where they support a remnant.
- Old horn and dull bone should remain worn, non-graphic secondary warning accents, not trophy piles, skull fields, or dense decoration.

Excluded neutral/civilized Giant material silhouettes:

- Refined blue-gray civic masonry, precise hidden cave-town carving, terrace retaining-stone language, waterwork channels, polished bridge stone, warm hearth cues, peaceful highland wayfinding, civic mason symbols, restrained blue rune inlays, orderly stoneworker finish, or any material read that makes Blood Axe cave residue look like neutral Giant civic craft.

## Materials and Color Palette

- Rough highland stone: dark field rock, weathered granite, fractured gray slabs, broad chipped planes, cave-damp grooves, soot-dark recesses, ash-dusted bases, and muted lichen hints.
