## Silhouette Notes

- Primary silhouette: two low, broad, broken slabs arranged as an uneven pair over an irregular ash/mud base.
- Slab hierarchy: one primary slab should carry the dominant width and mass; the second slab should sit lower, offset, half-sunk, or angled to avoid a symmetrical gate read.
- Height read: low and heavy, with the tallest slab edge below the low-standing-stone language from the remnant threshold package.
- Broken edge language: broad snapped planes, large chipped corners, visible slab thickness, and a few major wedge-like breaks. Avoid dense pebble clutter.
- Cloth accent: one fixed strip, knot, trapped fold, or low drape in faded oxide red. It must stay subordinate to the slabs and must not read as a banner, sign, UI marker, or objective cue.

Avoid arch-like framing, upright paired posts, doorway silhouettes, route arrows, readable glyphs, objective rings, magic panels, active fire, smoke, glowing runes, polished civic Giant stonework, warm neutral settlement cues, dense trophy clutter, graphic gore, and excessive micro-detail.

## LOD Plan

All future static mesh work requires LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any shipping use.

- LOD0: full paired-slab composition with primary slab, secondary slab, ash/mud base, fixed cloth, broad chipped edges, and sparse accents.
- LOD1: reduce stone edge loops, cloth edge cuts, secondary mud ridges, small chips, minor chock stone bevels, and hidden underside detail.
- LOD2: preserve the primary slab silhouette, secondary slab offset, cloth color block, and broad ash/mud footprint while removing secondary debris and simplifying broken edges.
- LOD3: simple low broken-slab mass preserving only the primary asymmetrical slab pair, low cave-edge grounding, and one restrained Blood Axe color beat.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth fibers, rope fibers, lichen, char cracks, paint chips, and small scratches.
2. Secondary cloth fray, small debris, minor stone notches, tiny horn/bone/iron accents, and small ground grit.
3. Hidden underside cuts, back-facing slab chips, non-visible ash/mud undercuts, and duplicate base details.
4. Optional chock stones, secondary ash ridges, small mud lips, and minor support fragments.
5. Cloth fold geometry, slab bevel density, and secondary decorative cuts.
6. Primary slab pair silhouette only after all small detail is removed.

Never preserve micro-detail at the expense of the paired broken-slab read, Giant-scale mass, static abandoned threshold mood, or Blood Axe/neutral Giant separation.
