## Silhouette Notes

The review rows should compare broad threshold rhythm, not create new silhouettes. Each row should use existing planning families from the referenced packages:

- Low threshold cairns: squat paired stone masses built from a few large rocks. They should create a low left/right beat without becoming a gate, route marker, or path rule.
- Paired standing stones: tall/short rough slabs with uneven height and spacing. The negative space between stones is a visual gap only.
- Broken slab remnants: broad snapped threshold stones, split slab pairs, ash-grounded slabs, chipped red paint, and sparse chock stones. They should imply old hostile occupation, not active cave functionality.
- Draped threshold cloth: one broad faded oxide-red cloth band over a low rough stone or slab. It should read as a restrained Blood Axe accent, not a banner, sign, flag, UI cue, or objective cue.
- Cave-edge grounding: dark cave-edge stone, cold ash, soot, mud, cave grit, and low remnant scatter used as supporting context only.

Row comparison targets:

- Sparse threshold row: one low cairn pair, one modest slab remnant, and no more than one red cloth beat. Reads as an older or less maintained Blood Axe edge.
- Standard threshold row: paired low cairns, one standing-pair frame, one broken slab cluster, and one restrained cloth or paint accent. Reads as the default review target.
- Dense warning row: paired stones, low cairns, two slab/remnant masses, and one or two small red accents. Reads hostile and occupied, but must stay visually readable.
- Broken memory row: collapsed cairn rhythm, offset slab remnants, ash/mud grounding, and minimal red paint. Reads abandoned or damaged.
- Overmarked reject row: too many cloth strips, too many slab fragments, repeated red hits, tight clutter, or UI-like alignment. This row exists to show what to avoid.

Avoid polished civic stonework, symmetrical monument gateways, refined cave-town masonry, warm hearth settlement cues, readable route text, route arrows, objective icons, glowing glyphs, dense trophy curtains, graphic gore, and micro-detail that would not survive mid-poly MMO production.
