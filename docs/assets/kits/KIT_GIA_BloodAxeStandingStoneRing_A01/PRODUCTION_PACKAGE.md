`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeStandingStoneRing_A01` defines an inactive Blood Axe standing-stone ring kit for old warning circles, abandoned camp memory, and hostile territory remnants. The ring should read as a rough arrangement of Giant-scale stones set into highland earth by raiders, with broken gaps, fallen slabs, ash-darkened ground, and restrained oxide red cloth or paint marks. It is not a magic arena, ritual gameplay space, combat arena, objective marker, navigation/pathfinding device, or active encounter space.

Blood Axe visual language must remain a hostile Giant sub-faction and must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. The ring can imply a harsh raider history, moved camp, warning threshold, or remembered violence without making all Giants brutal, ritualistic, or hostile. Neutral/civilized Giant culture remains tied to mountain stonecraft, hidden cave towns, terraces, waterworks, hearth light, civic blue-gray masonry, and restrained shamanic or storm accents.

This package does not approve final ring approval, ritual gameplay, VFX/audio, collision correctness, nav/pathfinding, objective markers, aura/damage behavior, DCC, FBX, Unreal, startup placement, or first implementation target selection.

## Silhouette Notes

The primary read is a large, uneven, inactive circle of rough standing stones. The ring must feel placed by Giant raiders and weathered by time, not designed as a polished civic monument or active magic diagram.

Primary silhouette families:

- Tall anchor stones: one to four dominant stones that establish the ring height, with fractured crowns, leaning profiles, broad chipped sides, and heavy base wedges.
- Secondary stones: shorter upright stones that continue the circle rhythm without making every beat equal.
- Broken stones: snapped stumps, missing tops, cracked upright remnants, and chipped slabs that show age and abandonment.
- Fallen stones: low slabs partly sunk into mud, ash, and grass, used to interrupt the circle and avoid arena readability.
- Partial ring arcs: short arc modules that can imply an old circle without forming a complete functional boundary.
- Ring-gap variants: deliberate open gaps, collapsed sections, and missing-stone breaks that keep the ring inactive and non-gameplay.
- Review rows: non-shipping comparison rows for stone height rhythm, gap spacing, partial arcs, material restraint, scale markers, and camera-distance readability.

Model future large stone bodies, broad fractured crowns, major chips, base chock stones, fallen slab profiles, heavy ash/mud berms, thick rope or hide lashings, broad cloth strips, and large blackened iron clamps as geometry if separately approved. Keep fine cracks, stone pitting, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, rope fibers, small paint chips, lichen, mud streaks, scratches, and tiny marks in textures, normals, AO, or shared trim sheets.

Avoid clean obelisks, readable letters, UI arrows, active arena boundaries, glowing ritual circles, dense skull piles, graphic gore, polished blue-gray civic masonry, terrace/waterwork motifs, warm hearth symbolism, refined neutral Giant stoneworker craft, excessive micro-cracks, or one-note red/black clutter.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes need LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full stone silhouettes, fractured planes, anchor-stone height, secondary stone rhythm, broken and fallen slab profiles, base chock stones, broad cloth/lashing shapes, major blackened iron clamps, ash/mud bases, and review-scale rows where separately approved.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small bevels, secondary chips, cloth edge cuts, lashing subdivisions, small horn cuts, minor base undercuts, and hidden backside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify stones into larger planes, reduce chock stones, flatten shallow fractures, simplify cloth tears, remove most non-silhouette accent pieces, and preserve only major gap reads.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve ring arc silhouette, tall anchor stones, broad fallen slabs, intentional gaps, and a few Blood Axe red accents only.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, small chips, cloth weave, rope fibers, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, paint chips, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashings, tiny horn chips, little stone wedges, secondary cloth tears, and small metal nicks.
3. Back-facing chock stones, underside slab bevels, hidden base cuts, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Secondary marker tokens, small iron clamps, minor bone or horn accents, and small cloth knots.
5. Stone bevel density, secondary fracture cuts, and cloth fold geometry.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the main ring rhythm, anchor stones, fallen slab reads, partial arcs, and gap silhouettes.

Never reduce the Giant-scale relationship, inactive warning-circle read, Blood Axe red warning accents, or primary stone silhouettes before removing small detail.
