`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCaveRemnantCluster_A01` defines a static Blood Axe cave-edge remnant cluster made from one crude cairn, one low standing stone, old fixed cloth, cold ash, trampled mud, cave grit, and restrained threshold variants. It should read as abandoned hostile Giant memory dressing near a cave approach, not as a cave entrance gameplay marker, route system, encounter setup, ritual device, objective cue, or active hazard.

Blood Axe language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This kit may use rough highland stone, soot, ash, mud, rope, rawhide, oxide red cloth, blackened iron, sparse old horn, and dull bone, but it must not become the default Giant cave-town identity. Neutral/civilized Giant culture remains tied to hidden highland settlements, skilled stonework, cave-town terraces, waterworks, hearth warmth, and restrained civic blue-gray stone language.

This package does not approve source concepts, final visual direction, DCC source, FBX export, Unreal Content, material instances, texture assets, validators, runtime source, startup placement, visual approval, or implementation sequencing.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette families:

- Remnant cairn: a squat, blunt stack of a few large rough stones, lower and more collapsed than the ritual cairn guidepost, with an old warning read rather than a clean waypoint read.
- Low standing stone: a short fractured slab or half-buried marker, lower than `SM_GIA_BloodAxeStandingStone_A01`, with a rough crown and heavy base contact.
- Old cloth: broad fixed oxide red scraps tied around stone, trapped under rocks, or slumped over a slab. Cloth must read as static weathered residue, not a flag, UI marker, or animated banner.
- Ash/mud base: a low irregular ground mass of cold ash, soot, packed earth, trampled mud, and cave grit. It grounds the cluster but must not form a gameplay ring, trigger area, route arrow, or objective radius.
- Threshold variants: small cluster arrangements that change density, leaning direction, cloth placement, and ash footprint while staying visual-only.
- Review-only cluster rows: clean non-shipping rows for scale, silhouette, material restraint, and camera-distance readability.

Future geometry, if separately approved, should model the main stone bodies, broad chipped planes, large cloth shapes, heavy rope knots, obvious rawhide wraps, and major ash/mud forms. Fine cracks, stone pitting, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, frayed threads, rope fibers, chipped red paint, lichen, mud streaks, and small scratches should stay in textures, normals, AO, or masks.

Avoid UI arrows, readable text, cave-entry symbols, objective icons, glowing runes, magic glyph panels, refined cave-town masonry, warm neutral settlement cues, dense trophy clutter, graphic gore, or micro-detail that would not survive mid-poly MMO production.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate implementation signoff.

- LOD0: full remnant cairn forms, low standing-stone massing, broad old cloth silhouettes, major rope or rawhide lashings, ash/mud base forms, and combined cluster contact shadows.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small bevels, secondary chips, cloth edge cuts, rope lashing subdivisions, minor base undercuts, and hidden backside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify stones into larger planes, merge small cairn stones, flatten cloth folds, remove non-silhouette accent pieces, and simplify ash bases.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the blunt remnant read, low standing-stone silhouette, broad red cloth beat, and cave-edge memory rhythm only.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, paint chips, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashings, secondary cloth tears, little stone wedges, small horn chips, minor iron nicks, and tiny knots.
3. Back-facing stones, underside bevels, hidden contact cuts, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Secondary marker tokens, duplicate foot stones, small cloth holes, and minor decorative cuts.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and optional review-row helper detail.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary cairn, low standing stone, cloth-marker, or remnant-cluster silhouette.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, static memory dressing read, Blood Axe red warning accents, or the main cave-remnant silhouettes before removing small detail.
