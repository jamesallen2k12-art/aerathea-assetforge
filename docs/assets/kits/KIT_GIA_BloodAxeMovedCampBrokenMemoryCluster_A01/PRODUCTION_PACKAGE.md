## LOD Plan

All future static mesh implementation requires LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any shipping use.

- LOD0: full collapsed cairn core, both displaced stones, broad lashing strips, low soot/mud base, major stone bevels, readable ash-dark contact, and restrained old Blood Axe residue.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, minor stone cuts, cloth edge cuts, lashing subdivisions, small base undercuts, and non-visible contact detail while preserving the one-cairn/two-stone cluster read.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; merge smaller supporting stones into broad planes, simplify displaced stones, flatten soot/mud base detail, remove most optional accents, and keep only the largest lashing or cloth beat.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the low collapsed cairn mass, two displaced-stone beats if silhouette allows, base footprint, and one muted Blood Axe residue accent.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, fray cuts, rope fibers, paint chips, lichen specks, small scratches, and minor pitting.
2. Small knots, secondary lashings, tiny cloth holes, little stone wedges, small horn or bone chips, and minor iron nicks.
3. Hidden underside detail, back-facing stone contacts, interior overlaps, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Optional accent debris, duplicate foot stones, secondary cloth tears, and non-silhouette rubble.
5. Secondary stone bevel density, minor broken-plane cuts, and low base edge complexity.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary collapsed-cairn and displaced-stone silhouettes.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, the one collapsed cairn plus two displaced stones read, the abandoned camp-memory mood, the low soot base, the old-lashing identity, or Blood Axe/civilized Giant separation before removing small detail.
