`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeClothTiedCaveStandingPair_A01` defines a static cloth-tied variant of the Blood Axe cave standing-pair family. The read is two rough Giant-scale standing stones with broad static oxide red cloth, rawhide lashings, thick Giant-tied knots, soot, ash, mud, cave grit, and restrained Blood Axe warning material.

The asset should feel crude, hostile, and handmade by raiders at the mouth of a cave or abandoned shelter edge. It is not a banner system, UI marker, quest pointer, objective cue, wind-reactive dressing, material-state object, or proof of cave gameplay. Blood Axe visual language must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture, which keeps its own skilled stonework, blue-gray civic masonry, terraces, waterworks, warm hearth identity, restrained civic runes, and highland settlement craft.

## Silhouette Notes

- Primary read: two rough upright standing stones with an asymmetric tall/short relationship, tied visually by one or two broad static oxide red cloth bands.
- The stones should carry the main silhouette through heavy slab mass, blunt fractured crowns, broad chipped faces, embedded bases, and a clear visual gap between them.
- Cloth should read as thick, heavy, dirty material tied by Giant hands, not as a fluttering banner, route arrow, map pin, UI sign, objective frame, or readable symbol.
- Rawhide lashings should be oversized and sparse: one to three broad wraps, a few thick tension bands, and a large knot or tied bundle are enough.
- Thick Giant-tied knots should sit at readable height on one stone face or across a side lashing, with clear compression against the cloth.
- Cloth can wrap around one stone, cross a short gap as a sagging fixed strip, or bind both stones as a warning tie, but it must not become an arch, curtain doorway, gate mechanism, or traversal cue.
- Low chock stones, mud pads, cold ash, and cave grit may ground the bases only as secondary support language.
- Optional old horn, dull bone, or blackened iron fragments may appear as sparse warning accents, but not as dense trophies, graphic gore, or noisy trim.
- Model large forms only if later work is separately approved: stone slabs, major chips, broad cloth shells, thick cloth edges, large knots, rawhide wraps, major chock stones, and broad base buildup.
- Keep fine cracks, cloth weave, rope fibers, rawhide pores, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud droplets, lichen, paint chips, small scratches, horn rings, and bone pores in future textures, normals, AO, or masks.
