`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeDryChannelStoneSet_A01` defines a dry, inactive, static remnant kit for Blood Axe ritual-channel stone layouts. The kit covers short run pieces, capped ends, broken channel sections, corner or turn pieces, low support stones, ash fill variants, and non-shipping review rows. These modules should read as old stone trough fragments left behind after a hostile Blood Axe camp moved or collapsed, not as active conduits.

Blood Axe identity must remain a hostile Giant sub-faction treatment and must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This kit may use rough highland stone, soot, ash, trampled mud, crude repair, controlled oxide red, blackened iron, rawhide, rope, sparse horn, and dull bone. It must not become the default visual language for Giants, and it must not borrow neutral/civilized Giant cave-town masonry, waterworks, warm hearth identity, blue-gray civic stonework, or restrained blue rune language.

The primary read is a low, heavy line of broken Giant-scale stone channel remnants. The pieces should feel dry, old, and inactive, with broad trough shapes visible from MMO camera distance.

Primary module silhouettes:

- Short run pieces: low rectangular or irregular slab segments with one dominant broad dry trough.
- Capped ends: blunt stone stops, plugged trough mouths, collapsed lips, and heavy end blocks that prevent any read of active flow.
- Broken channel sections: fractured trough pieces with missing corners, shifted slab halves, dry ash-filled gaps, and rough end damage.

## LOD Plan

All important future static modules need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any production import lane uses them.

- LOD0: full slab silhouette, broad dry trough lips, capped end mass, broken channel edges, corner turn form, low supports, ash fill silhouette, optional large iron clamps, and broad material value groups.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, minor chips, underside cuts, small support stones, small iron edge cuts, and shallow non-silhouette trough notches.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; keep the main slab footprint, one dominant trough read, capped or broken end identity, corner angle, and broad ash/soot value.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only low stone mass, dry trough line, broken or capped end read, and controlled Blood Axe red/ash accents.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, small chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud droplets, lichen flecks, paint chips, minor scratches, cloth weave, rope fibers, and iron pitting.
2. Small side stones, minor knots, tiny horn chips, small metal nicks, little rubble pieces, and secondary cloth cuts.
3. Hidden underside bevels, backside cuts, non-visible ash undercuts, and interior broken edge noise.
4. Optional secondary iron wedges, duplicate supports, extra trough notches, and non-silhouette damage.
5. Secondary trough complexity and shallow groove subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary slab mass, capped end, broken end, corner turn, or low support silhouette.

Never reduce the Giant-scale relationship, dry/no-flow read, static remnant identity, Blood Axe hostile material accents, or primary stone silhouettes before removing small detail. Follow `DOC_GIA_BloodAxeRitualStoneLODAndCollision_A01` for shared policy.
