`DOC_GIA_BloodAxeLowThresholdCairnMaterialDiscipline_A01` defines restrained material rules for future low-threshold Blood Axe cairns and their paired, ash-base, collapsed, red-cloth, spacing-review, and reference-only rows. The material language should read as old hostile territory residue: rough stone, soot, ash, trampled mud, cave grit, rope, rawhide, controlled oxide red cloth, sparse old horn, and dull bone.

This document protects the Blood Axe Tribe as a hostile Giant sub-faction. It must not redefine Giants as a whole, and it must not borrow neutral/civilized Giant settlement materials as the default low-threshold cairn read. Neutral/civilized Giants retain skilled stonework, hidden highland settlements, blue-gray civic masonry, terraces, bridges, waterworks, warm hearth light, restrained blue runes, and orderly stoneworker craft.

## Silhouette Notes

Materials must support the low, paired cairn silhouettes instead of competing with them. The first read should remain squat Giant-scale stone masses, broad ash or mud grounding, controlled cloth beats, thick bindings, and sparse warning accents.

Material silhouette support:

- Restrained rough stone should read as a few large field rocks, fractured slabs, broad chipped planes, and heavy contact shadows. Avoid dense pebble piles or crack carpets.
- Soot should sit in recesses, underside pockets, stone contacts, old burn halos, and cloth-adjacent grime without turning the cairns into flat black shapes.
- Ash should gather as cold pale value breaks around bases, under shifted stones, and across old remnant pads.
- Trampled mud should ground the cairns through wide compressed brown-gray bands and dirty stone feet, not thin paths or route lines.
- Cave grit should sit as coarse gray-brown residue at the stone feet, ash transitions, and cave-edge contact zones.
- Rope and rawhide should read as thick Giant-tied wraps, broad lashings, and functional knots. Fine fibers, pores, stitching, and fray belong to future normal or texture planning.
- Oxide red cloth should be sparse and broad: static tied strips, faded wraps, dirty knots, or chipped paint-like accents. It must never become a full red wash or UI cue.
- Sparse old horn and dull bone should be large, worn, non-graphic secondary accents, not dense trophy curtains or repeated surface patterning.

Excluded silhouette and material reads:

- Refined blue-gray civic masonry, precise cave-town stone blocks, terrace and waterwork forms, warm hearth settlement cues, peaceful highland wayfinding, carved civic ornament, restrained blue rune identity, polished bridge stone, civic mason symbols, readable text, UI arrows, objective symbols, magic glyph panels, glow-based entrance cues, dense skull piles, graphic gore, or micro-detail fields.

## Materials and Color Palette

Primary low-threshold cairn material families:

- Restrained rough stone: dark highland field rock, weathered granite, fractured gray slabs, cave-edge stone, large chipped planes, muted mineral streaks, and ash-dusted rock feet.
- Soot: matte black-gray residue, old cold fire stains, charcoal smears, under-stone shadows, darkened recesses, and smoke-stained cloth contacts.
- Ash: cold gray dust, settled camp residue, pale dry patches, old burn pads, edge deposits, and soft value breakup around cairn bases.
- Trampled mud: packed brown-gray earth, darker compressed patches, dried splash bands, dirty stone feet, and old highland dirt.
- Cave grit: coarse gray-brown mineral dust, crushed stone residue, dry cave threshold dirt, tiny gravel implied through future texture, and dull transition grime.
- Rope and rawhide: thick rope lashings, sinew ties, rawhide strips, rough hide knots, smoke staining, and muddy contact grime.
- Oxide red cloth: faded maroon strips, dirty red wraps, static knots, chipped red paint marks, soot-stained edges, and controlled Blood Axe warning beats.
- Sparse old horn: aged horn caps, worn tan fragments, dulled ridges, and a few non-graphic warning tokens.
- Dull bone: worn ivory/tan pieces, old bone tokens, rubbed edges, and sparse non-graphic accents.

Palette targets are planning values, not final color approval:

- Deep stone and soot: `#151718`, `#222527`, `#343635`, `#4E4D47`
- Weathered stone and ash: `#67645E`, `#827B70`, `#A49B8E`, `#C0B5A3`
- Cave grit and trampled mud: `#241D17`, `#382C22`, `#554332`, `#6F5B45`

Material ratio guidance:

- Rough stone, soot, ash, cave grit, and trampled mud should carry roughly 80-90 percent of the visual read.
- Oxide red cloth and chipped paint should stay around 3-8 percent of any individual cairn composition.
- Rope and rawhide should stay functional and restrained, usually 3-7 percent of the composition.
- Old horn and dull bone should remain sparse secondary accents, usually below 3 percent combined.
- No default emissive color is part of the baseline. Glow, signal light, ritual light, shamanic pulse, torch state, UI highlight, and animated material state are excluded.

Excluded neutral/civilized Giant materials:

- Refined blue-gray civic masonry, precise stone blocks, cave-town carved ornament, terrace stonework, waterwork channels, bridge finish, warm hearth light, restrained blue rune accents, civic mason marks, peaceful clan banners, orderly highland guide markers, clean hidden-settlement polish, and any material language that makes Blood Axe raider cairns read as neutral Giant civic craft.

## LOD Plan

- LOD0: full broad material contrast, rough stone planes, soot and ash grounding, cave grit and mud contact zones, controlled oxide red cloth, thick rope/rawhide wraps, and sparse horn/bone accents.
- LOD1: preserve major material zones and Blood Axe red/black read; reduce small cloth tears, secondary lashing loops, paint chips, tiny cracks, lichen specks, ash flecks, cave-grit particles, and mud droplets.
- LOD2: keep stone versus cloth, soot versus ash, mud versus grit, and horn/bone accent reads through broad masks and value grouping; remove most non-silhouette material-driven geometry.
- LOD3: preserve the primary low-threshold cairn read: dark rough stone, cold ash base value, dull mud grounding, sparse oxide red warning beat, and one or two major accent families.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, fine cracks, cloth weave, rope fibers, rawhide pores, soot speckles, ash flecks, grit particles, mud droplets, small paint chips, lichen flecks, horn rings, and bone pores.
2. Small knots, little stone chips, secondary cloth cuts, minor hide repairs, and small lashing loops.
3. Small horn or bone tokens, minor non-silhouette accents, and small loose debris.
4. Hidden backside grime, underside detail, interior recess marks, and camera-hidden cave residue.
5. Small cloth fold geometry, shallow groove subdivisions, and secondary rock-plane cuts.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the broad material zones that carry the low-threshold cairn read.

Never reduce the no-default-emissive baseline, hostile Blood Axe material identity, Giant-scale readability, static visual threshold rhythm, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant materials before reducing small detail.
