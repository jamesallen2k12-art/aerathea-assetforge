## Silhouette Notes

Scale rows should use clean row compositions with the female and male Giant baselines repeated consistently. Each row should isolate one remnant-cluster child family so reviewers can compare height, footprint, mass, and dominant silhouette without reading the board as a final scene.

Required row silhouette families:

- Cairn remnant row: low, old cairns built from a few large rough stones. They should read lower and less waypoint-like than cairn guideposts.
- Collapsed cairn row: slumped piles with shifted major stones and broad ash contact. They should read abandoned, not destructible or physics-ready.
- Low standing stone row: short fractured slabs sized below tall Blood Axe standing stones but still readable beside Giant knees, waist, and hands.
- Broken leaning stone row: half-broken or leaning slabs with broad chipped planes and heavy mud contact. They should not imply puzzle state, destructible state, gate behavior, or traversal use.
- Old cloth row: broad oxide red tied wraps and draped scraps scaled for Giant hands. Cloth should remain a restrained faction accent, not a flag system, UI marker, or animated banner.
- Ash/mud base row: low irregular grounding shapes for cold ash, soot, packed earth, trampled mud, and cave grit. Bases should not read as trigger zones, damage fields, objective rings, or route paths.
- Threshold cluster row: compact combinations of cairn, low stone, fixed cloth, and ash base. The row should read as static cave-edge memory dressing, not cave entrance gameplay.

Avoid polished monuments, refined civic masonry, neutral Giant wayfinding, blue rune identity, warm settlement cues, readable route signage, arrows, icons, magic glyph panels, dense trophy clutter, graphic gore, and micro-detail that would not survive mid-poly MMO production.
