## Art Direction Summary

- `DOC_GIA_BloodAxeMovedCampCairnLineLODAndCollision_A01` is a docs-only LOD and collision planning package for `MovedCamp_LODCollisionPlanning_A01`.
- Planning scope covers sparse moved-camp cairn segments, broken memory clusters, ash gaps, cloth remnants, short stake remnants, and review-only composition rows.
- The visual read is abandoned Blood Axe camp evidence: interrupted low cairns, collapsed stones, cold ash, trampled mud, oxide red cloth scraps, rawhide ties, rope lashings, sparse blackened iron, old horn, and aged bone.
- Blood Axe is a hostile Giant sub-faction only. This package must keep Blood Axe raider language separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture, highland masonry, cave-town civic carving, terraces, waterworks, hearth settlement language, and restrained blue rune traditions.
- The cairn line is non-graphic environmental history about a camp that moved or passed through. It must not read as an active trail, waypoint chain, encounter lane, camp route, objective marker, or tracking system.

## Silhouette Notes

- Primary silhouette language: low, interrupted, uneven stone beats with visible gaps and collapsed edges, never a continuous directional line.
- Sparse segments should use 2-4 low cairn remnants with missing beats, asymmetrical spacing, and at least one empty ash or mud gap that breaks the read.
- Broken memory clusters should feel like abandoned camp residue: one collapsed cairn, displaced stones, low soot, buried lashings, and small secondary remnants.
- Ash gaps should stay flat and broad, reading as cold ground residue rather than footprints, rings, arrows, ritual circles, decals for route following, or readable travel instructions.
- Cloth remnants should be small, weathered, and trapped under stone or tied around one low element. They must not become banners, signal flags, faction UI colors, or route markers.
- Short stake remnants should stay smaller than banner-line or guidepost assets, with splintered, scorched, non-functional forms.
- Review rows are non-shipping document comparisons for spacing, density, ash breaks, cloth restraint, and camera readability. They are not assets, actors, validators, captures, or startup-scene placements.

## LOD Plan

- LOD0: Preserve the full readable abandoned-camp silhouette: few large stones, collapsed cairn massing, ash gaps, mud scuffs, restrained oxide cloth, rawhide ties, and any short stake remnant that defines the row.
- LOD1: Remove or merge the smallest loose stones, reduce cloth folds, simplify rope/rawhide geometry, lower ash-edge tessellation, and keep only the main broken spacing rhythm.
- LOD2: Collapse secondary stone piles into fewer forms, bake small chips and lashings into texture/normal detail, reduce cloth to simple planes or merged shapes, and keep ash/mud as broad low-density residue.
- LOD3: Preserve only the largest stones, the overall interruption pattern, and broad color-value read. Remove cloth fray, small stakes unless silhouette-critical, tiny debris, individual chips, and most ash edge complexity.
- Sparse segments must never simplify into a continuous line at any LOD. Missing beats and offset placement are silhouette requirements.
- Memory clusters must retain the "collapsed and abandoned" read at distance, not become clean marker piles or guideposts.
- Ash gaps must remain non-directional at all LODs and must not become route decals, objective rings, footprints, or lane indicators.
- Cloth and short stake remnants should be the first details reduced unless they are the only clear Blood Axe identity cue in a given future asset.
- Review rows use LOD terminology only to compare readability. They do not create LOD source files, DCC tasks, Unreal LOD groups, validators, or shipping assets.
