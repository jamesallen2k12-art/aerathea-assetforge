`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeApproachCliffWalls_A01` defines modular visual planning for the cliff walls that compress the route toward a Blood Axe stronghold approach. The kit covers tall fractured wall segments, overhang-shadow faces, ledge-back faces, narrow choke walls, rubble-foot strips, dark recess inserts, scale/review rows, material discipline rows, and LOD/collision planning rows.

Blood Axe language must remain a hostile raider sub-faction read. It may use harsh fractured highland rock, soot, ash, mud, dark recesses, blackened metal scraps, rough timber remnants, oxide red warning cloth, and sparse non-graphic bone or horn accents. It must not overwrite neutral or civilized Giant culture, which remains tied to hidden highland settlements, master stonework, blue-gray masonry, terraces, waterworks, warm hearth light, restrained blue runes, and civic stoneworker identity.

## Silhouette Notes

The primary read is a hostile vertical corridor: tall broken rock planes, sharp ledge shadows, narrow squeezes, rubble at the base, and dark recesses that imply a dangerous approach without defining terrain or navigation.

Primary silhouette targets:

- Tall fractured wall: broad vertical slabs, broken faces, large angular cracks, jagged but readable upper edges, and occasional inset scars.
- Overhang-shadow face: heavy upper mass casting a deep visual shadow, with underside detail kept broad and readable.
- Ledge-back face: flatter rear cliff panels and shelf-back walls that visually support approach ledges without claiming walkable surfaces.
- Narrow choke wall: opposing cliff masses that compress the route visually and frame Blood Axe warning beats.
- Rubble-foot strip: low broken stone bands at the wall base, sized as visual grounding and not as terrain implementation.
- Dark recess insert: cave-mouth-like shadow pocket, split cleft, or black interior plane used sparingly for intimidation, not as an explorable space.
- Review rows: scale rods, camera-read composition blocks, and non-shipping silhouette comparisons for future visual checks only.

Model large forms as geometry only if a later task approves production: main cliff planes, overhang blocks, ledge-back slabs, big rubble pieces, broad shadow inserts, and major stone breaks. Push tiny cracks, chip noise, gravel, soot speckles, ash flecks, mineral streaks, mud stains, scratched warning marks, and small stone pitting into textures, normals, AO, masks, or trim sheets.

Avoid refined cave-town masonry, terrace walls, waterwork cuts, warm hearth cues, civic stoneworker symbols, restrained blue runes, readable tactical markings, UI arrows, dense skull walls, graphic gore, Ogre Teknomancy cues, or a single-value dark cliff mass that loses MMO camera readability.

## LOD Plan

All important future static modules require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full cliff-wall silhouette, broad fractured planes, major overhangs, ledge-back forms, choke-wall compression, rubble-foot stones, dark recess shapes, and limited Blood Axe warning accents.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary rock cuts, small bevels, non-silhouette chips, minor rubble, cloth edge cuts, and shallow recess subdivisions.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; merge smaller rock planes, simplify rubble clusters, flatten secondary ledge backs, reduce accent geometry, and keep only the primary shadow/recess read.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the vertical cliff mass, large overhang shape, narrow choke silhouette, dark recess block, and a few major rubble forms.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, paint chips, mineral streaks, and small cracks.
2. Small rubble stones, minor ledge undercuts, cloth fray, small scrap fragments, and secondary edge breaks.
3. Backside and underside detail not visible from intended approach composition.
4. Secondary rock-plane subdivisions, small bevels, and non-silhouette cuts.
5. Minor Blood Axe accents that do not carry the faction read.
6. Primary cliff height, overhang mass, choke compression, recess shape, and Giant-scale read only after all secondary detail is reduced.

Never reduce the main vertical cliff silhouette, Blood Axe approach compression, or Giant-scale relationship before removing small detail.
