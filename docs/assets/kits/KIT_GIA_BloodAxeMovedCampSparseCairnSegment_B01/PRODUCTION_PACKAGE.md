## Silhouette Notes

- Overall read: a wide sparse span with one visually dominant collapsed cairn, one subordinate low support pile, and a long empty ash gap between or beside them.
- Dominant collapsed cairn: asymmetrical, slumped, heavy, and Giant-made, built from a few large stones rather than many small pebbles.
- Low support pile: 2-5 broad stones, lower and quieter than the main cairn, placed off-axis so it does not create a repeated marker rhythm.
- Empty ash gap: long, flat, cold, and visually quiet. It should read as residue and absence, not a path, lane, arrow, footprint trail, objective ring, or traversal hint.
- Spacing must feel uneven and broken. Avoid neat left-to-right sequencing, repeated intervals, or any composition that becomes a guide line.
- Cloth remnants, if used, should be small fixed oxide red scraps trapped under stone or slack around one heavy rock. They must not read as flags, route tags, UI color coding, or active signals.
- Optional horn, bone, or blackened iron accents must stay sparse, old, non-graphic, and subordinate to the stone and ash shapes.

Model real geometry in later approved work only for major stone masses, broad collapsed profiles, the low support pile, simple cloth silhouettes, large lashings, and broad ash/mud base forms. Reserve tiny stone chips, hairline cracks, ash flecks, soot dust, cloth weave, fray, rope fibers, paint chips, lichen, and mud streaks for future texture, normal, AO, or mask detail.

Avoid skull piles, graphic gore, readable signs, arrows, trail diagrams, bright signal colors, glowing marks, polished civic stonework, blue runes, warm hearth motifs, or dense pebble chains.
