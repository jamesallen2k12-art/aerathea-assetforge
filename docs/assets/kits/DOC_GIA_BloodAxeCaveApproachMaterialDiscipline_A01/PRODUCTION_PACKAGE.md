`DOC_GIA_BloodAxeCaveApproachMaterialDiscipline_A01` defines the shared material rules for future Blood Axe cave-approach markers: low threshold cairns, paired threshold cairns, collapsed cairns, cave ash remnant bases, red cloth threshold markers, cave remnant clusters, review rows, and related planning documents.

The cave-approach material read should feel cold, hostile, abandoned, and warning-driven. It should use rough highland stone, soot, ash, cave grit, trampled mud, oxide red cloth, rope, rawhide, blackened iron, old horn, and dull bone as restrained production language. It must not become an active magic read, a UI cue, a route-system cue, or a finalized shader direction.

Blood Axe material language remains separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Neutral/civilized Giants keep their own hidden highland settlement identity: skilled stonework, blue-gray civic masonry, terraces, waterworks, warm hearth light, restrained blue runes, and orderly stoneworker craft. Blood Axe cave approach materials may look crude and threatening, but they are not the default Giant cultural material language.

## Gameplay Purpose

The gameplay purpose is visual planning only. This package gives future cave-approach marker documents a consistent material discipline so threshold dressing reads as Blood Axe hostile territory without becoming gameplay signage or interaction logic.

Allowed planning uses:

- Keep low cairns, red cloth markers, cave ash bases, and remnant clusters visually consistent across future docs-only package work.

## Silhouette Notes

Materials must support the broad cave-approach silhouettes instead of becoming the primary read. The first read should remain Giant-scale low cairns, uneven standing stones, collapsed slabs, ash bases, broad cloth strips, large rope ties, and sparse warning accents.

Material silhouette support:

- Rough highland stone should read as broad broken planes, chunky chipped edges, dark fractured seams, ash-stained bases, and cave-worn field rock.
- Soot should collect in underside shadows, recesses, stone-contact pockets, old burn zones, and cave-edge creases without turning the full kit into flat black silhouettes.
- Ash should gather as cold pale value breaks at bases, between stones, inside collapsed cairns, and across old remnant pads.
- Cave grit should sit as coarse, dry, gray-brown residue along stone feet, mud transitions, and cave-mouth edges.
- Trampled mud should ground markers through broad compressed bands, not thin gameplay paths or route lines.
- Oxide red cloth should be sparse and large: static tied strips, faded wraps, dirty knots, and chipped paint-style accents. It should never become a red wash across every asset.
- Rope and rawhide should read as thick Giant-tied wraps, lashings, and functional knots. Fine fibers, pores, and stitch marks belong to later texture or normal planning.
- Blackened iron should appear only as occasional crude clamps, rings, strap plates, or old fragments where a secondary hard accent helps the read.
- Old horn and dull bone should be sparse warning accents, not dense trophy curtains, gore decoration, or repeat surface patterning.

Excluded silhouette reads:

- Neutral/civilized Giant blue-gray civic masonry, refined cave-town carving, terrace and waterwork construction language, warm hearth settlement cues, restrained blue rune inlays, peaceful highland wayfinding, UI arrows, readable text, objective symbols, magic glyph panels, glow-based entrance cues, dense skull piles, graphic gore, or micro-detail fields.

## Materials and Color Palette

Primary cave-approach material families:

- Rough highland stone: dark granite, weathered slate, cave-edge field rock, chipped slabs, fracture lines, muted lichen hints, mineral streaks, and ash-dusted rock feet.
- Soot: matte black-gray residue, old fire stains, charcoal smears, darkened recesses, under-stone shadows, and cave-mouth scorch.
- Ash: cold gray dust, settled camp residue, pale dry patches, edge deposits, and soft value breakup around cairn bases.
- Cave grit: coarse gray-brown dirt, dry mineral dust, crushed stone residue, tiny gravel implied through texture, and dull cave threshold dirt.
- Trampled mud: packed brown-gray earth, darker compressed patches, dried splash bands, heel-worn gradients, and old highland dirt.
- Oxide red cloth: faded maroon strips, dirty red wraps, static knots, chipped red paint marks, soot-stained edges, and controlled Blood Axe warning beats.
- Rope and rawhide: thick rope lashings, sinew ties, rough hide strips, old leather wraps, muddy contact grime, smoke staining, and stretched fibers implied through later detail maps.
- Blackened iron: dull clamps, old rings, rough straps, soot-stained dark steel, rubbed edge wear, and crude repair plates.
- Old horn and dull bone: aged horn caps, worn ivory/tan fragments, dull bone tokens, and non-graphic warning pieces.

- No default emissive color is part of the baseline. Glow, signal light, ritual light, shamanic pulse, torch state, UI highlight, and animated material state are excluded.

## Texture and Material Notes

This document creates no textures, material instances, material functions, parent materials, shader graphs, trim sheets, texture assets, VFX materials, or audio-linked states. Names and channels below are planning language only and require separate scoping before authoring.

Standard future map outputs, if a later package owns asset creation:

- Base Color / Albedo (`BC`): broad stone value groups, soot darkening, ash lightening, mud transitions, cloth fade, rawhide wear, iron dullness, horn/bone age, and restrained lichen. Do not encode glow or gameplay highlights in `BC`.
- Normal (`N`): broad chipped edges, shallow stone pitting, cloth weave, rope fiber direction, rawhide pores, horn rings, bone pores, and minor iron pitting. Do not fake deep silhouette changes that should belong to geometry in a consuming mesh.
- Packed Occlusion/Roughness/Metallic (`ORM`): `R` for AO around stone contacts, cloth knots, rope wraps, ash bases, mud undercuts, and iron overlaps; `G` for high roughness on stone, soot, ash, grit, mud, cloth, rope, rawhide, horn, and bone, with medium-high variation on blackened iron; `B` for metallic only on blackened iron or dull steel accents.
- Emissive (`E`): excluded from the baseline. Any future glow, signal, shamanic, torch, ritual, forge, or magic variant needs a separately named package.

Mask and reuse guidance:

- Reuse shared material families across low cairns, paired cairns, collapsed cairns, ash remnant bases, red cloth threshold markers, and cave remnant clusters.
- Plan broad reusable masks for `Stone_EdgeWear`, `Soot_Ash_Deposit`, `Grit_Mud_Blend`, `Cloth_FadeBreakup`, `RopeRawhide_Wear`, `Iron_RubbedEdge`, and `HornBone_Age`.
- These labels are material-policy vocabulary, not texture assets or material instances.
- Avoid unique masks for each stone, cloth strip, rope knot, ash fleck, mud splash, horn chip, bone token, lichen speck, or paint chip.
- Favor atlas or trim reuse where future child packages share the same cave-approach family.

Material-slot discipline for future consuming meshes:

- Stone, soot, ash, cave grit, and trampled mud should usually share one material family.
- Oxide red cloth, rope, and rawhide should share a second family when they need separate UV density or tint control.
- Blackened iron, old horn, and dull bone should share accent space whenever possible instead of demanding separate slots.
- Stone-only low cairns and ash bases should target 1 material.
- Cloth-tied cairns, red cloth threshold markers, and mixed remnant clusters should target 1-2 materials.
- Use 3 materials only when stone/soil, cloth/hide/rope, and iron/horn/bone truly need separation in a separately scoped child package.
- Do not assign unique materials per crack, soot patch, ash deposit, mud fleck, cloth strip, rope strand, iron chip, horn ring, bone token, or paint mark.

## LOD Plan

This package creates no LOD assets. Future material use must remain readable across LOD0-LOD3 on separately scoped consuming meshes.

- LOD0: full broad material contrast, rough stone planes, soot and ash grounding, cave grit and mud contact zones, controlled oxide red cloth, thick rope/rawhide wraps, sparse blackened iron, and sparse horn/bone accents.
- LOD1: preserve major material zones and Blood Axe red/black read; reduce small cloth tears, secondary lashing loops, paint chips, tiny cracks, lichen specks, ash flecks, and mud droplets.
- LOD2: keep stone versus cloth, soot versus ash, mud versus grit, iron versus horn/bone, and red warning accents readable through broad masks and value grouping; remove most non-silhouette material-driven geometry.
- LOD3: preserve the primary cave-approach read: dark rough stone, cold ash base value, dull mud grounding, sparse oxide red warning beat, and one or two major accent families.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, fine cracks, cloth weave, rope fibers, rawhide pores, soot speckles, ash flecks, grit particles, mud droplets, pitting, small paint chips, lichen flecks, horn rings, and bone pores.
2. Small knots, little stone chips, secondary cloth cuts, minor hide repairs, and small lashing loops.
3. Small horn or bone tokens, tiny iron nicks, minor straps, and non-silhouette scrap bits.
4. Hidden backside grime, underside detail, interior recess marks, and camera-hidden cave residue.
5. Minor plate bevels, shallow groove subdivisions, small cloth fold geometry, and secondary rock-plane cuts.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the broad material zones that carry the Blood Axe cave-approach read.

Never reduce the no-emissive baseline, hostile Blood Axe material identity, Giant-scale readability, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant materials before reducing small detail.
