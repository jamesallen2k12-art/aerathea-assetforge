## 1. Art Direction Summary

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeFlatCrateStack_A01` defines a low stack of two to five flat rough crates for Blood Axe camp shelter edges, gate interiors, and camp path corners. The stack should read as heavy Giant-scale utility storage shoved into practical camp edges, not as a treasure chest, loot pile, vendor display, interactable container, barricade, or polished settlement supply.

## 3. Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette:

- Low, broad, stepped stack of two to five flat rectangular crates.
- Squat crate bodies with exaggerated plank thickness, blunt corner caps, heavy cross straps, thick rope lashings, and uneven stacked offsets.
- Strong horizontal read from MMO camera distance, with one or two crates slightly rotated or pushed out to avoid a clean warehouse pile.
- Stable camp-corner footprint rather than a tall tower or barricade wall.
- Oxide-red Blood Axe marks used as small surface accents, not large signage.

Model real geometry for crate bodies, major planks, lid slabs, broad side rails, large iron straps, thick corner caps, oversized rope lashings, large knots, pallet skids, and the stack's ground-contact silhouette.

Keep in texture, normal, and AO detail: wood grain, tiny chips, minor cracks, soot speckles, ash, dried mud, rope fibers, small nails, faded paint wear, small scratches, and dark grime under overlaps.

Avoid treasure chest silhouettes, shiny locks, readable labels, vendor tags, UI arrows, resource-node symbols, cover-marker language, neutral/civilized Giant storage, cozy hearth supply crates, dense tiny rivets, excessive red paint, gore, or destructible fracture seams.

## 10. LOD Plan

All future mesh work for this stack requires LOD0-LOD3.

LOD0:

- Full low stack with two to five crate forms, broad planks, lid slabs, iron straps, corner caps, rope lashings, large knots, pallet skids, contact shadows, and restrained Blood Axe red accents.

LOD1:

- Reduce minor plank bevels, small strap bevels, hidden underside detail, secondary rope wraps, tiny cloth tags, and minor crate offsets while preserving crate count, stack footprint, and the main lashing read.

LOD2:

- Simplify crate bodies into larger merged forms, reduce lashing turns, collapse small knots into normal detail, remove most backside and underside faces, and keep one clear red accent plus the largest iron strap reads.

LOD3:

- Preserve the low rectangular mass, broad stepped outline, Giant-scale footprint, dark timber/iron identity, and one visible Blood Axe accent. Remove small hardware, minor plank splits, rope tails, cloth patches, tiny caps, and most interior overlap detail.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny nails, scratches, chips, soot speckles, paint chips, rope fibers, and wood grain.
2. Small rope tails, secondary knots, minor cloth or hide tabs, and small strap bevels.
3. Minor plank splits, underside boards, hidden rails, and backside-only detail.
4. Secondary crate offsets that do not affect the main silhouette.
5. Small red accents after the main Blood Axe read remains clear.

Never reduce the low stack silhouette, crate count read, ground footprint, broad horizontal mass, or Giant-scale readability before removing small detail.
