## 10. LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full oversized rope coil, heavy knot, tie-down loop, or loose binding silhouette with thick rope diameter, broad rawhide wraps, cut rope ends, ground-contact shaping, and restrained Blood Axe accent.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Preserve coil outline, rope diameter, major knot mass, tie-down loop silhouette, and largest rawhide wraps while reducing secondary rope turn subdivisions, small end shaping, and minor wrap bevels.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve the broad rope mass, primary loop path, main knot block, and dirty material identity while simplifying pass-through forms and flattening minor rope overlap detail.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0. Preserve a chunky coil or loop proxy with visible rope color and one large knot or end mass. Remove most secondary turns, small wraps, small red accents, and pass-through cuts.

LOD reduction order:

1. Fine rope fibers, tiny strand ridges, fray, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud grains, small scratches, and paint chips.
2. Small rawhide tabs, tiny rope ends, minor tie tails, small red marks, and tiny edge cuts.
3. Secondary rope-turn waviness, small pass-through gaps, underside cuts, and minor coil height variation.
4. Secondary knot shaping, rawhide wrap bevels, and optional static clamp bevels.
5. Only after these reductions, simplify the main coil footprint, rope diameter, primary knot mass, tie-down loop silhouette, and Blood Axe material identity.

Never reduce the Giant-scale rope read, thick rope diameter, primary coil or loop silhouette, heavy knot mass, or hostile Blood Axe identity before removing small detail.
