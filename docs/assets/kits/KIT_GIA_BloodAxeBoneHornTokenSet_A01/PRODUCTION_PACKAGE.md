`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeBoneHornTokenSet_A01` defines a small reusable set of blunt horn caps, old bone tokens, hide ties, and rope knots for Blood Axe path-marker variants. The pieces should read as crude Giant-scale camp dressing used to customize cairns, cloth stakes, horn markers, broken shield markers, ash bases, and mixed warning clusters without becoming interactable objects.

The set must reinforce Blood Axe hostility through rough material handling, oxide red accents, soot, dull bone, old horn, rawhide, and rope. It must not replace neutral or civilized Giant culture. Civilized Giant cave-town, stoneworker, highland route-marker, terrace, waterwork, warm hearth, blue-gray carved masonry, and restrained blue-rune language remain separate from Blood Axe raider dressing.

This package does not authorize pickups, inventory items, trophies, crafting ingredients, loot, ritual mechanics, source creation, engine import, startup placement, final visual approval, implementation target selection, Hermes work, runtime source, external source concept movement, or global-index edits.

## 2. Gameplay Purpose

The kit supports visual variation for hostile Blood Axe path markers. Its purpose is to give future cairns, stakes, horn markers, shield scraps, ash bases, and mixed marker clusters a shared library of small non-interactive dressing parts.

Allowed planning purpose:

- Add repeated Blood Axe identity to marker variants through lashed horn caps, bone tabs, hide ties, and heavy rope knots.
- Break visual repetition across static path-marker props while keeping the parent kit readable from MMO camera distance.
- Provide reusable accents that can sit on cairn sides, stake bases, rope wraps, shield-scrap corners, ash-pad edges, or mixed cluster compositions.
- Keep all pieces oversized and field-made enough to feel built by hostile Giants rather than scaled from human trinkets.

Out of scope:

- Pickup behavior, inventory behavior, trophy displays, crafting ingredients, loot drops, salvage resources, ritual mechanics, interaction prompts, objective markers, waypoint behavior, trail-marker gameplay, nav/pathfinding helpers, AI markers, spawn markers, damage fields, aura behavior, VFX/audio, destructible states, runtime logic, startup placement, or any gameplay system.

## 3. Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette language:

- Blunt horn caps with rounded, chipped ends and thick rope or hide wraps.
- Old bone tokens shaped as simple oval, wedge, rib-like, or flattened shard forms with drilled holes or lash notches kept broad and readable.
- Hide ties as wide rawhide straps, folded tabs, twisted loops, and rough knots with large silhouette breaks.
- Rope knots as chunky overhand, wrap, loop, and binding forms sized for Giant-made markers.
- Optional oxide red paint daubs, soot rubs, and ash staining should read through texture and color, not through tiny modeled marks.

Model as real geometry in any later approved source task:

- Main horn cap bodies, large bone token shapes, major strap loops, broad hide folds, primary rope knots, thick lash wraps, large attachment holes, and major silhouette chips.

Keep in texture or normal detail:

- Fine cracks, pores, scratches, rope fibers, leather grain, stitch marks, soot speckles, ash dust, old paint chips, minor pitting, and tiny edge chips.

Avoid:

- Sharp gore-coded fragments, skull piles, fresh remains, readable symbols, refined carved tokens, jewelry-like ornament, trophy presentation, collectible silhouettes, UI-arrow shapes, blue-rune motifs, polished civic Giant marker language, or dense micro-detail.

## 4. Scale Notes

Use the validated Giant scale lock:

- Female Giant baseline: 442 cm / 14 ft 6 in.
- Male Giant baseline: 470 cm / 15 ft 5 in.
- Approved Giant range: females 14-15 ft / 427-457 cm; males 14 ft 10 in-16 ft 0 in / 452-488 cm.
- Authoring scale assumption for future work: centimeters; 1 Unreal unit = 1 cm if a separate engine task is later approved.

Token set dimensions:

- Blunt horn caps: 18-45 cm long, 10-28 cm wide, visibly blunt at both ends.
- Old bone tokens: 15-40 cm long, 8-24 cm wide, thick enough to read at path-marker distance.
- Hide tie strips: 30-90 cm long, 5-18 cm wide, with broad folded ends and simple wrap silhouettes.
- Rope knots: 12-35 cm across, with 3-8 cm rope thickness for Giant-made lashings.
- Small token cluster on one marker: 35-120 cm combined footprint, usually two to six pieces maximum.
- Repeated marker accents should remain subordinate to parent marker scale. They should not create a separate item display or collectible cluster.

These parts are small relative to a Giant but large relative to normal humanoid hands. The read should be "oversized marker dressing," not pocketable inventory.

## 10. LOD Plan

All future shipping pieces should support LOD0-LOD3 if promoted by a separate task.

- LOD0: Full blunt horn cap forms, old bone token silhouettes, hide folds, rope knots, large attachment holes, major chips, and broad lash wraps.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify bevels, merge small chips, reduce secondary folds, and lower rope-knot loop count while preserving the main shape.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve token type read and large attachment silhouette, remove underside cuts and most secondary tie detail.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; retain only broad horn/bone/hide/rope silhouette and color read for distant markers.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, pores, soot flecks, paint chips, stitch marks, and rope fiber detail.
2. Minor bevel loops, small chips, and small drilled-hole edge detail.
3. Secondary strap folds, extra wraps, and small dangling tabs.
4. Interior knot undercuts and back-facing contact detail.
5. Non-shipping review-only callout detail.

Never remove the clear token type, blunt horn/bone read, Blood Axe red/soot material identity, or Giant-scale lashing read before removing small detail.
