`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeSackGroup_A01` defines a group of three to seven heavy sacks with varied heights and rotations for Blood Axe camp utility clutter. The group should read as bulky Giant-scale field storage pushed against shelter edges, rough walls, forge-adjacent ground, gate interiors, or camp path corners, while remaining inert environmental dressing.

Blood Axe identity must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. These sacks belong to a hostile raider sub-faction: dirty sackcloth, patched hide, thick rope, rawhide ties, soot, mud, ash, and restrained oxide-red Blood Axe accents. Do not use civilized Giant cave-town craft, refined highland market supplies, warm hearth storage, blue-gray stoneworker motifs, restrained blue runes, or peaceful Giant settlement language as the default read.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette should be a broad, lumpy, practical sack cluster scaled for hostile Giants:

- Sack count: three to seven heavy sacks, with at least three visibly different heights, widths, or tied-neck shapes.
- Composition: uneven triangular or crescent grouping with one or two larger anchor sacks, smaller sacks tucked into gaps, and rotations varied enough to avoid a repeated clone read.
- Height rhythm: tallest sack 180-260 cm, mid sacks 120-200 cm, low slumped sacks 70-140 cm.
- Footprint read: stable ground pile, not a tower, barricade, merchant display, or neatly sorted resource stack.
- Sack bodies: sagging rounded bases, compressed ground contact, broad folded sides, heavy tied necks, and lumpy contents hidden under fabric.
- Ties: thick rope, rawhide bands, large knots, wrapped neck cords, and a few tied tails large enough to read from MMO camera distance.
- Faction accents: small oxide-red cloth strips, faded red paint dabs, or crude Blood Axe marks used sparingly on one or two sacks only.

Model real geometry for main sack volumes, silhouette-defining folds, tied necks, broad rope bands, large knots, compressed bases, and the combined cluster footprint. Use texture and normal detail for cloth weave, fray, stitching, small patches, rope fibers, soot speckles, mud splatter, faded paint wear, and tiny scratches.

Avoid tiny grain bags, market sacks, cozy village pantry read, readable labels, flour symbols, vendor pricing marks, UI glyphs, glowing markers, treasure bag silhouettes, overfilled coin-pouch shapes, destructible seams, fracture cuts, and dense micro-folds that would not hold up in mid-poly game art.

## LOD Plan

All future mesh work for this sack group requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full sack count with varied heights and rotations, major tied necks, broad folded bases, large rope bands, big knots, visible patch silhouettes, restrained red accent, and clear Blood Axe material read.
- LOD1: reduce small folds, secondary tie tails, minor patch bevels, hidden underside detail, and duplicate small contact creases while preserving sack count and pile outline.
- LOD2: merge minor folds into normal detail, simplify tied neck geometry, reduce knot loops, remove most hidden back-side detail, and preserve the strongest red accent block.
- LOD3: retain the broad sack-group mass, Giant-scale footprint, largest tied-neck reads, and dark cloth/red Blood Axe read. Remove small hardware, secondary lashings, small patches, minor folds, and tiny tags.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, cloth fibers, rope fibers, soot speckles, paint chips, small scratches, and mud flecks.
2. Small tie ends, secondary cords, tiny knots, minor patch cuts, and frayed edges.
3. Minor fold lips, underside folds, secondary base compression, and back-facing overlaps.
4. Rare small metal rings, clamp bevels, hidden contact faces, and redundant sack-to-sack occlusion geometry.
5. Secondary red tags or paint marks if the main Blood Axe accent remains readable.

Never reduce the primary sack count, broad pile footprint, tallest anchor sack, strongest tied-neck shapes, or Giant-scale readability before removing small detail.
