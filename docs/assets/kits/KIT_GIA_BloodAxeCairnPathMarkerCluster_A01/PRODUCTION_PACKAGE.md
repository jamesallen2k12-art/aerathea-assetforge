`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCairnPathMarkerCluster_A01` defines a small group of two to five varied cairns arranged as a rough Blood Axe camp path bend or approach compression beat. The cluster should feel like hostile Giant raiders dragged large field stones into place with brute force, then packed the base with mud, soot, ash, and a few restrained Blood Axe warning accents.

The read is crude, heavy, and threatening, but not ornate. Each cairn needs a distinct height and lean so the group forms a readable bend from MMO camera distance: one dominant stack, one or two supporting mid-height stacks, and optional low collapsed stones at the inside edge of the bend.

Blood Axe visual language must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This cluster is not a civilized cave-town guidepost, highland mason marker, civic stoneworker route sign, warm hearth settlement marker, blue-gray carved terrace marker, or restrained blue-rune Giant cultural asset.

This task creates only this markdown package. It does not create source meshes, exports, engine content, runtime behavior, placement work, external concept edits, global index edits, final signoff claims, target selection, or Hermes file/configuration work.

## Gameplay Purpose

The cluster supports static visual readability in hostile Blood Axe camp spaces. It gives a path bend, rough approach squeeze, or camp threshold compression point a stronger visual beat than a single cairn without turning the prop group into gameplay logic.

Allowed planning purpose:

- Visually compress an approach into a rough Blood Axe camp bend.
- Signal a hostile Giant-made route edge through stone massing, soot, mud, and red cloth or paint accents.
- Break up wide ground planes near camp entries, forge paths, shelter lanes, palisade approaches, or stronghold outskirts as static dressing.
- Provide scale context for future composition review against validated Giant baselines.
- Support repeated path-marker rhythm when paired with the parent kit, while staying lower and more ground-adjacent than banner lines or gate silhouettes.

Out of scope:

- Interactive route guidance, quest logic, pickup behavior, loot behavior, combat behavior, AI behavior, spawn logic, destructible behavior, resource behavior, crafting/economy behavior, audio cues, VFX pulses, UI-like symbols, pathfinding setup, blocking volumes, editor helper systems, actor authoring, combat-lane authoring, source-art target selection, engine work, placement work, final signoff, or Hermes/configuration work.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette:

- Two to five crude cairns in an asymmetric cluster, arranged in a shallow arc, hooked bend, pinched approach, or staggered S-curve.
- One dominant cairn at 200-260 cm tall, one or two mid-height cairns at 130-210 cm tall, and optional low collapsed cairns at 60-120 cm tall.
- Stones should be large, chunky, and irregular, with broad contact shapes and visible stacked weight.
- The cluster footprint should read as Giant-built: heavy stones, wide spacing, trampled mud, and a rough route edge rather than small folk trail markers.
- A single red cloth tie, red paint smear, dull shield scrap, or horn fragment may be used on one cairn, but the cluster should remain stone-led.
- The bend should be readable from above and from a third-person camera angle without arrows, readable text, glowing signs, or refined carved symbols.

Model as real geometry in any later approved production pass:

- Major cairn stones, large base slabs, dominant leaning rocks, broad mud-packed bases, shield scrap if used, major horn/bone accent if used, heavy rope or hide lashings, and large red cloth silhouette.

Keep in texture or normal detail:

- Fine stone cracks, chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, dried mud streaks, cloth weave, small fray, minor rope fibers, scratches, pitting, chipped paint, and shallow stone pores.

Avoid:

- Dense pebble piles, many tiny stacked rocks, polished masonry, refined civic carvings, readable signs, blue-rune language, elegant stoneworker marks, warm hearth motifs, graphic gore, skull piles, glowing quest symbols, or symmetrical shrine-like layouts.

## Modeling Notes

This section is a planning handoff only. It defines the intended shape language for a later separately approved modeling task without creating source files or selecting a build target.

Composition guidance:

- Build the cluster from two to five distinct cairn forms, not duplicated stacks with simple rotation.
- Use one dominant cairn to establish direction, then stagger supporting cairns to compress the visual approach.
- Keep the arc asymmetric: one side should feel pushed inward by heavier stones, while the opposite side uses lower collapsed stones or mud base mass.
- Use a few large stones per cairn. Each stack should have readable weight, overhang, and contact shadow.
- Add only one or two Blood Axe accents across the whole cluster so the cairn read stays stone-led.
- Shape the base as trampled ground dressing: low mud shelves, ash rings, soot-dark contact points, and crushed gravel.
- If a shield scrap is used, make it wedged and dull, not readable as a usable equipment pickup.
- If horn or bone is used, keep it blunt, old, and non-graphic.

Geometry priority:

- Spend geometry on primary stone profiles, stacked contact planes, large bevels, silhouette lean, broad base pads, and one or two major accents.
- Use shared stone modules only if they are altered enough to avoid obvious repetition inside the cluster.
- Keep undersides and back-facing details simple unless they affect the visible silhouette.
- Preserve ground contact and scale readability over tiny detail density.

Texture/normal priority:

- Fine cracks, small chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, dried mud streaks, paint chips, rope fibers, cloth weave, and shallow pitting should be painted or normal-mapped rather than modeled.

Do not include actor logic, helper objects, physics setup, destructible states, sockets, collectible affordances, UI arrows, route-control diagrams, or runtime behavior in this package.

## LOD Plan

All future approved versions require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: Full two-to-five cairn composition with dominant stack, supporting stacks, low collapsed stones, mud/ash base, broad bevels, visible soot, and sparse accent geometry.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce stone bevels, remove underside cuts, simplify secondary stones, merge small base shapes, and simplify accent geometry.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve cluster arc, dominant cairn height, supporting cairn mass, route-compression read, and one red/black Blood Axe accent; remove most small stones and base variation.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0. Preserve only the broad cluster footprint, dominant stack silhouette, supporting mass blocks, and rough bend read; remove small accents unless needed for faction readability.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny chips, ash flecks, small cracks, paint chips, cloth holes, and scratch detail.
2. Minor pebbles, small mud ridges, tiny stone wedges, and small loose gravel.
3. Secondary lashings, thin cloth cuts, rope wraps, and small accent fragments.
4. Back-facing stone cuts, undersides, and interior contact detail.
5. Small supporting stones that do not affect the cluster arc.
6. Low collapsed stones after the dominant and supporting cairn silhouettes are preserved.

Never reduce the dominant cairn read, the two-to-five cairn grouping, the bend/compression silhouette, the Giant-scale stone mass, or Blood Axe hostile material identity before small detail is removed.
