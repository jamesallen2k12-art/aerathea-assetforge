# KIT_GIA_BloodAxeBannerLine_A01 Production Package

## Art Direction Summary

- Asset name: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeBannerLine_A01`
- Asset type: Kit production package / static Blood Axe camp banner-line dressing and review-composition planning
- Parent planning kit: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCamp_A01`

- Faction/theme: Blood Axe Tribe, hostile Giant sub-faction only
- Status: docs-only production package ready

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeBannerLine_A01` defines static camp-perimeter banner lines for Blood Axe Giant spaces: tall poles, rope-line variants, tattered cloth banners, warning pennants, camp-threshold markers, and review composition rows. The kit should read as crude hostile raider territory from MMO camera distance through vertical timber beats, sagging rope spans, torn oxide red cloth, blackened hardware, soot, mud, and sparse non-graphic horn or bone warning shapes.

Blood Axe identity must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This kit must not become the default Giant banner language, civic highland marker language, cave-town masonry language, stoneworker pride symbol set, warm hearth settlement dressing, or restrained blue-rune culture package.

This package is planning only. It does not authorize DCC source creation, FBX export, Unreal Content creation, cloth/physics setup, banner animation, faction buff behavior, morale/AI behavior, startup placement, source concept copying, final visual approval, or first DCC target selection.

## Gameplay Purpose

The kit supports non-interactive Blood Axe camp readability. Its purpose is to visually mark hostile camp edges, path bends, rough perimeters, threshold crossings, warning zones, and future review compositions without defining gameplay rules.

Allowed planning uses:

- Mark Blood Axe camp approaches, paths, perimeter runs, and shelter or forge-zone edges.
- Provide repeated red cloth and rope rhythm between larger camp props such as gates, shelters, watch platforms, and static war banners.
- Frame camp thresholds so players read "hostile Blood Axe territory" before individual enemies appear.
- Provide non-shipping review composition rows for later concept, DCC, or Unreal visual checks.
- Coordinate with `SM_GIA_BloodAxeWarBanner_A01` while staying smaller and more repeatable than the main war banner prop.

Out of scope:

- Faction buff behavior, morale behavior, AI behavior, patrol logic, spawn logic, aggro logic, encounter scripting, objective logic, capture mechanics, aura volumes, debuffs, UI markers, quest markers, loot, resource, crafting, economy, destructible behavior, interaction behavior, VFX pulses, audio cues, or material-state gameplay.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- A repeated hostile camp boundary made from huge rough poles, rope spans, torn red cloth, and warning pennants.
- Tall poles as vertical beats that stay below or between the main `SM_GIA_BloodAxeWarBanner_A01` silhouette but still feel Giant-built.
- Rope-line variants with heavy sag, thick knots, crude blackened iron clamps, hide lashings, and occasional stone-weighted bases.
- Tattered cloth banners with broad readable tears, split tails, and large red blocks rather than many tiny strips.
- Warning pennants as small repeatable red shapes that create rhythm along paths without becoming UI arrows.
- Camp-threshold markers as paired poles, crossed rope, broad cloth hangs, and ground warning accents.
- Review composition rows as simplified non-shipping height/spacing markers that show line rhythm, threshold framing, and camera readability.

Model as real geometry when promoted later:

- Main poles, thick rope spans, major knots, cross ties, broad cloth panels, large tear outlines, iron collars, stone or scrap base weights, and sparse horn/bone warning shapes.

Keep in textures or normal maps:

- Tiny rope fibers, cloth weave, fray threads, small nail heads, light scratches, soot speckles, mud flecks, wood grain, pitting, small chips, and minor cloth holes.

Avoid:

- Dense dangling strips, many small skulls, graphic gore, readable text, UI-like arrows, glowing quest-marker silhouettes, elegant civic banners, blue rune symbols, refined stoneworker carving, or a one-note red-only palette.

Normal humanoid compatibility is not required. The kit should look built by Giants and placed for Giant camps, not resized from human camp dressing.

## LOD Plan

All important future modules require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full pole shapes, rope sag, broad cloth panels, major tears, warning pennants, large knots, clamps, bases, and sparse warning accents.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify cloth edge cuts, reduce knot bevels, merge small lashings, reduce rope twist segments, and flatten small base chips.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve pole rhythm, rope line, red cloth blocks, and threshold framing while removing minor interior cuts, secondary ties, and back-side accents.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve vertical pole rhythm, red warning shapes, broad rope span, and camp-threshold read; remove most small hardware, base detail, and accent tokens.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny fray cuts, nail heads, stitches, soot chips, and small cloth holes.
2. Small rope fibers, secondary knots, minor wraps, and extra ties.
3. Minor pennants, small hanging scraps, and interior cloth tears.
4. Small horn/bone chips and broken shield detail.
5. Clamp bevels, pole chip complexity, and base stone undercuts.
6. Back-facing line details and non-visible underside detail.

Never reduce the Giant-scale pole rhythm, red cloth read, rope-line span, threshold framing, or hostile Blood Axe red/black identity before removing small detail.
