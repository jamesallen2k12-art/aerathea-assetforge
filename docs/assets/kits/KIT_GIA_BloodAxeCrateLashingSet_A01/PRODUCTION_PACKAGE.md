## 1. Art Direction Summary

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCrateLashingSet_A01` defines reusable Giant-scale static lashing components for future Blood Axe storage props: crossed rope bands, rawhide lashings, wide tie-down straps, and large knots sized to attach visually to crates, covered bundles, sacks, and stacked camp clutter in later DCC work.

The kit should read as harsh raider logistics: thick, blunt, oversized binding pieces pulled tight over rough camp goods by Blood Axe Giants. Forms should be practical and aggressive, with smoked rawhide, coarse rope, soot, ash, dried mud, compressed tie points, and sparing oxide red accents. It must not read as refined cargo rigging, neutral Giant highland craft, merchant packing, loot affordance, player equipment, or usable rope.

Keep this visual language separate from neutral and civilized Giant culture: no blue-gray civic masonry, no refined cave-town storage craft, no warm hearth market presentation, no restrained blue rune identity, no peaceful highland nomad craft, and no civilized stoneworker symbols.

## 3. Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette:

- Crossed rope bands forming large X shapes over crate faces or covered bundle surfaces.
- Wide tie-down straps that read as broad bands, not thin cords.
- Rawhide lashings with flattened, uneven profiles and compressed contact points.
- Large knots, tie lumps, and short blunt tails that are readable from MMO camera distance.
- Optional short red cloth wrap or rubbed oxide red mark, used as a restrained Blood Axe accent only.

Shape hierarchy:

- First read: oversized crossed binding pattern across a crate or bundle surface.
- Second read: broad strap width and thick rope/rawhide diameter.
- Third read: one or two large knot masses and blunt tie ends.
- Fourth read: soot, ash, mud, rough fibers, cut hide, rubbed red accent, and contact grime through material detail.

Model real geometry in future work for major rope bands, rawhide straps, wide tie-down strips, crossing overlaps, large knots, compressed wrap points, blunt strap ends, and any broad red cloth tabs that affect silhouette.

## 10. LOD Plan

All future mesh work for this kit requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full readable crossed rope bands, wide tie-down straps, rawhide lashings, large knots, blunt tie tails, compressed contact points, optional small red accent, and strong soot/mud material read.
- LOD1: reduce strap bevels, knot undercuts, minor overlap lips, secondary tie tails, small edge cuts, and optional hardware bevels while preserving the X pattern, broad strap width, and largest knot silhouette.
- LOD2: merge small folds into normal detail, simplify knot geometry, reduce rope cross-section sides, flatten minor strap variation, remove backside-only details, and keep one clear lashing path plus a simplified knot read.
- LOD3: retain only bold band or X shapes, a blocky knot mass, and one readable material/accent contrast where needed. Remove small tie tails, minor folds, optional hardware, underside contact detail, and secondary surface forms.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny fibers, fray, pores, stitch marks, scratches, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud flecks, paint chips, and small cracks.
2. Small cut-edge notches, minor tie tails, shallow strap bevels, and secondary overlap lips.
3. Secondary knot folds, hidden underside detail, backside-only compression, and optional hardware.
4. Optional red accents if the parent crate or bundle already carries Blood Axe color identity.
5. Minor rope/profile variation only after the broad X pattern, strap read, and Giant-scale knot remain clear.

Never reduce the crossed band read, broad tie-down width, large knot mass, or Giant-scale utility silhouette before removing small detail.
