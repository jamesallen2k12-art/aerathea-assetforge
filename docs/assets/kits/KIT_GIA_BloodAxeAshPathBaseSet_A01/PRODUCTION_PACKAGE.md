# KIT_GIA_BloodAxeAshPathBaseSet_A01 Production Package

## 1. Art Direction Summary

- Asset name: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeAshPathBaseSet_A01`
- Asset type: Docs-only production package for static visual ground-dressing kit
- Parent planning kit: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxePathMarkers_A01`
- Source planning row: `BloodAxeCamp.png#PathMarkers_AshTrailPad_A01`
- Faction/theme: Blood Axe Tribe, hostile Giant sub-faction only
- Status: docs-only package; no final approval or implementation target

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeAshPathBaseSet_A01` defines a set of low ash-stained base pads, soot-dark burned-earth patches, charcoal scuffs, mud-ash transition shapes, and oversized burned-earth footprints for visual route rhythm in Blood Axe Giant camp compositions. The set grounds cairns, cloth stakes, horn markers, shield scraps, and rough camp approaches without becoming gameplay guidance.

The visual read should be brutal, crude, and ground-adjacent: trampled black soil, gray ash dragged through mud, crushed charcoal, scorched grass edges, and broad Giant foot pressure marks. It should support hostile Blood Axe camp identity while staying clearly separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture.

Blood Axe is a hostile Giant sub-faction. This package must not become the default Giant ground language, civilized cave-town route language, refined highland trail dressing, civic stoneworker paving, warm hearth settlement texture set, or restrained blue-rune Giant culture package.

## 2. Gameplay Purpose

The purpose is static visual composition only: low ground pads and burned-earth footprint shapes that add environmental rhythm around Blood Axe camp paths, marker bases, gate approaches, shelter edges, forge-side trampled zones, and rough camp circulation spaces.

Allowed planning uses:

- Ground visual rhythm beneath or near Blood Axe cairns, cloth stakes, horn markers, shield scraps, and mixed marker clusters.
- Ash and mud breakup for wide camp ground planes.
- Oversized Blood Axe Giant foot impressions as environmental wear, not trackable clues.
- Repeated low silhouettes that help the art team compose hostile camp routes from an MMO camera without creating player-facing guidance systems.

## 3. Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- Very low, irregular ash pads with broad oval, crescent, smear, and broken-edge shapes.
- Burned-earth footprint impressions that read as Giant-scale pressure marks from oblique camera angles.
- Charcoal clusters and mud edges used sparingly as chunky silhouette anchors, not dense pebble scatter.
- Soot halos around future marker bases, especially below cairns, low rags, horn forks, and shield scraps.
- Uneven repetition: pads should vary in size, rotation, spacing, and darkness so they feel trampled by raiders rather than laid out as a diagram.

Model as real geometry if promoted later:

- Main pad outline planes or very low raised rims.
- Broad depressed footprint shapes if mesh relief is required.
- Large charcoal chunks, broken burned turf plates, thick mud lips, and major edge curls.

Keep in textures or normal maps:

- Ash flecks, soot speckles, fine cracked earth, small charcoal crumbs, mud streaks, boot sole roughness, powdery transitions, tiny burned grass fragments, and small surface pitting.

Avoid:

- Arrow-like pad sequences, dotted-line patterns, UI-like outlines, glowing trails, readable symbols, ritual runes, quest marks, clean civilized paving, blue-gray cave-town masonry, warm hearth cobbles, polished stone paths, or precise footprint chains that imply tracking.

## 10. LOD Plan

All important future pad variants require LOD0-LOD3 if they become shipping static meshes.

- LOD0: full irregular pad perimeter, main ash/mud value zones, broad footprint depression, major charcoal chunks, burned grass edge clumps, and large mud lips.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify perimeter cuts, reduce relief on footprint edges, merge small charcoal chunks, and flatten minor mud curls.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; preserve overall pad silhouette and strongest dark/light ash read while removing most small perimeter notches and secondary clumps.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0; preserve only broad ash patch or footprint mass as a low simple shape; rely on material value for read.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny ash flecks, soot speckles, small cracks, and mud grain.
2. Small charcoal crumbs, minor burned grass strands, and tiny edge chips.
3. Secondary footprint edge noise and minor mud curls.
4. Small perimeter cuts and underside detail.
5. Minor relief across flat interior areas.

Never reduce the broad ash-pad read, Giant-scale footprint size, or Blood Axe burned-ground identity before removing small detail.
