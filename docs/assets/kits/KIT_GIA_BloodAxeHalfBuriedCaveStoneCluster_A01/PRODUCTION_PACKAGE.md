`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeHalfBuriedCaveStoneCluster_A01` is a low, old Blood Axe cave-remnant kit for environmental memory dressing. It should read as stone markers that have sunk into ash, mud, cave grit, and years of Giant-scale trampling, with restrained oxide-red cloth partly pinned below stone weight. The kit is quieter and older than a fresh threshold marker: its power comes from buried mass, weathered contact shadows, and hostile Giant raider residue rather than active ritual display.

Blood Axe visual language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This kit may use rough highland stone, soot, ash, packed mud, cave grit, rawhide, rope, restrained oxide-red cloth, dull bone, old horn, and sparse blackened iron. It must not use refined Giant cave-town masonry, blue-gray civic stonework, terrace or waterwork forms, warm hearth identity, peaceful highland wayfinding, carved civic ornament, or restrained blue-rune culture as its default read.

## LOD Plan

All future static mesh implementation requires LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before shipping use.

- LOD0: full half-buried cluster with primary stone, secondary stones, ash/mud base, mud-sunk cloth, and sparse accents.
- LOD1: reduce stone edge loops, cloth edge cuts, secondary mud ridges, small chips, accent bevels, and hidden underside detail.
- LOD2: preserve the dominant stone cap, two secondary stone reads, cloth color block, and broad ash/mud footprint while removing secondary debris.
- LOD3: simple low cave-remnant mass preserving only the primary buried-stone silhouette and one restrained Blood Axe color beat.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth fibers, rope fibers, lichen, paint chips, char cracks, and small stone chips.
2. Secondary cloth fray, tiny horn/bone/iron accents, minor stone notches, and small ground grit.
3. Secondary ash ridges, duplicate mud lips, hidden underside cuts, and small accent stones.
4. Cloth fold geometry, mud lip subdivisions, and buried contact bevel density.
5. Secondary stone caps and broad footprint cuts.
6. Main exposed stone silhouette only after all smaller detail has already been removed.

Never preserve micro-detail at the expense of the low half-buried read, Giant-scale stone mass, or Blood Axe/neutral Giant separation.
