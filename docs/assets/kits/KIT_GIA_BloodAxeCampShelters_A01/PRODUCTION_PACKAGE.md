# KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCampShelters_A01 Production Package

## Art Direction Summary

- Asset name: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCampShelters_A01`
- Asset type: Production kit / multi-asset hostile camp shelter set
- Task: `AET-MA-20260629-095`
- Faction/theme: Blood Axe Tribe, hostile Giant sub-faction
- Source basis: Aerathea Giant/Blood Axe anchors, `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeArmory_A01`, `SM_GIA_BloodAxeWarBanner_A01`, and `MI_GIA_BloodAxeReforgedMetal_A01`
- Status: docs-only production package ready

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCampShelters_A01` defines crude, temporary, hostile Giant raider shelter language for Blood Axe camps. The kit should read as a brutal war-camp living zone: huge hide tents, rough timber lean-tos, red warning cloth, blackened iron stakes, trophy poles, oversized bedding pallets, barricade leaners, torn awnings, lash-tied support frames, and sparse non-interactive shelter clutter.

This kit must not become neutral or civilized Giant cave-town architecture. It does not use blue-gray stoneworker pride, monumental masonry, warm hearth identity, restrained blue rune language, or hidden cave-town refinement. It is Blood Axe raider camp language: crude, mobile, intimidating, dirty, and built around war readiness.

This package is planning only. It does not create source folders, DCC files, FBX exports, Unreal Content, runtime source, validators, startup placements, collision proxies, cloth simulation, modular snapping implementation, destructible behavior, shelter interaction, final visual approval, or a first DCC target selection.

## Gameplay Purpose

The shelter kit supports environmental storytelling and encounter readability for future Blood Axe locations. It gives Giant raider camps a believable living footprint without adding gameplay behavior.

Expected use cases:

- Non-interactive camp dressing around Blood Axe patrol routes, armory yards, boss staging areas, forge edges, prisoner routes, and raid-camp perimeters.
- Scale reference for Giant-sized sleeping, storage, cover silhouettes, walk paths, and shelter entrances.
- Visual contrast against neutral/civilized Giant cave-town assets by using crude hide, rough timber, red cloth, trophies, soot, mud, and blackened hardware.
- Modular-looking set pieces that can be arranged later after a separate snapping and layout task.

Out of scope:

- Player shelter interaction, resting, looting, crafting, vendor behavior, storage, quest objective logic, destructible tents, fire spread, cloth simulation, animated wind deformation, gameplay cover rules, nav-blocking rules, and final encounter layout.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary shelter silhouettes should be huge, rough, and readable at MMO camera distance:

- Hide tents: squat wedge or conical masses with thick hide flaps, overbuilt poles, heavy stakes, and torn red repair cloth.
- Lean-tos: asymmetric sloped shelter roofs, massive rough logs, hide panels, heavy bindings, and sagging but static cloth edges.
- Trophy poles: vertical warning shapes beside shelters, using a few large bone/horn/armor trophies instead of dense clutter.
- Bedding pallets: oversized low rectangles made from logs, hide rolls, straw mats, and scavenged cloth.
- Barricade leaners: leaning defensive boards, sharpened logs, broken shield slabs, and spiked scrap leaned against tent frames.
- Awnings: broad torn cloth or hide shade panels tied between poles, with strong red/black Blood Axe read.
- Support frames: A-frames, tripods, ridge poles, lashings, and cross braces scaled for Giants.
- Shelter clutter: large practical camp objects such as hide rolls, tether stakes, ash buckets, bone hooks, cracked bowls, water skins, and stored bundles.

Keep the shape language crude and sparse. Model poles, hide sheets, primary seams, large stakes, major lashings, large trophies, barricade boards, and broad pallet shapes. Texture or normal-map small stitching, hide pores, thread fray, nail heads, tiny scratches, soot speckles, mud spatters, fine wood grain, and minor cloth tears.

## LOD Plan

All important shelter children require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full shelter silhouette, major hide/cloth planes, entrance flaps, support frames, big stakes, main bindings, sparse trophies, bedding forms, barricade leaners, and Blood Axe red accents.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce minor cloth cuts, secondary lashings, small frame bevels, inner bedding detail, and small trophy undercuts.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve shelter roofline, entrance read, support-frame footprint, red cloth blocks, trophy pole verticals, and barricade mass while flattening small folds and back-side details.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve camp silhouette, shelter footprint, red/black Blood Axe read, and major vertical/horizontal forms. Remove most small hardware, inner clutter, and fine hide/cloth edge detail.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, fray cuts, nail heads, small scratches, and pitting.
2. Small cords, secondary straps, and non-structural lashings.
3. Minor cloth holes and interior flap folds.
4. Small trophy accents and back-facing clutter.
5. Secondary pole bevels, underside details, and ground debris.
6. Minor barricade chips and inner bedding layers.

Never reduce the shelter roofline, Giant-scale entrance, primary support frame, trophy pole vertical, barricade mass, or Blood Axe red-cloth read before removing small detail.
