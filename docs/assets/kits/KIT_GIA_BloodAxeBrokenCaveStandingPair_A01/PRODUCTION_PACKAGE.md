## Silhouette Notes

- Primary read: one heavy upright rough stone and one snapped or fallen companion stone forming an uneven broken pair.
- Upright stone: tall, blunt, asymmetrical slab with a fractured crown, broad front plane, irregular side cuts, and a deeply embedded base.
- Companion stone: either a snapped stump with the fallen upper slab lying nearby, or a single fully fallen slab with a clear broken end. The break face should be broad and readable, not a dense crack field.
- Grounding: old ash, trampled mud, cave grit, two to five large chock stones, and partial burial should tie both stones into the ground.
- Broken planes should be large and game-readable from MMO camera distance. Use broad diagonal fracture cuts, chipped corners, and exposed stone faces rather than many thin cracks.
- Add restrained Blood Axe accents only where they clarify faction: a faded oxide red cloth wrap, a dirty red paint smear, a rawhide lashing remnant, or one sparse old horn or dull bone token.
- The pair may imply past violence or abandonment, but it must not imply an active collapse sequence, weak point, puzzle affordance, objective marker, trigger boundary, or interaction target.
- Keep front, side, and three-quarter reads clear. Avoid clean obelisks, refined civic monuments, UI arrows, readable text, magic glyph panels, glow-based entrance cues, dense skull piles, graphic gore, or neutral Giant cave-town masonry.
- Future geometry, if separately approved, should model the upright slab, fallen slab mass, snapped stump, major break planes, large chock stones, broad cloth strips, thick lashings, and old ash/mud base forms.
- Fine cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, rope fibers, mud streaks, lichen specks, chipped paint, stone pitting, and small scratches belong in textures, normals, AO, or masks.

- LOD0: full upright stone, fallen or snapped companion, broad break planes, embedded bases, chock stones, old ash/mud grounding, restrained red accents, major lashings, and sparse optional warning accents.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, minor base stones, cloth edge cuts, lashing subdivisions, and hidden backside detail while preserving the upright/fallen broken-pair read.

- LOD3: 12-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the single upright stone, fallen companion mass, major broken plane, broad base footprint, and one Blood Axe color or material accent.

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, chipped paint edges, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashing cuts, little knots, secondary cloth tears, small stone wedges, horn chips, iron nicks, and non-silhouette tokens.
3. Back-facing accent pieces, hidden underside bevels, buried base cuts, and non-visible mud undercuts.
4. Secondary fracture cuts, duplicate base stones, minor cloth holes, and optional accent geometry.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and minor base-plane subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary upright stone, fallen companion, exposed break plane, and ash/mud grounding silhouette.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, Blood Axe red warning read, the static broken-pair silhouette, or the old cave-edge memory marker identity before removing small detail.
