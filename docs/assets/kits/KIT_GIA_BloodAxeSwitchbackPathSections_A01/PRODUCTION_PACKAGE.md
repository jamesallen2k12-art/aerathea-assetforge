## Silhouette Notes

The primary read is a brutal switchback route carved, crushed, and reinforced through hostile Giant territory. Each section should feel physically large beside a 470 cm male Giant while staying modular and readable from top-down and three-quarter views.

Silhouette goals:

- Straight shelf segment: broad flattened ledge, rough cliff-side back edge, compressed outer edge, scattered stone chocks, and trampled center lane.
- Packed-earth strip: low dark strip with worn mud, ash, broad rut shapes, boot depression language, and sparse red warning scraps.
- Stone-chocked edge: chunky field stones, wedged cliff rubble, rough stone anchors, and small retaining beats that visually hold the route edge without becoming refined masonry.
- Log-edged segment: massive half-buried logs, broken side timbers, crude cross stakes, hide lashings, and soot-dark mud shoulders.
- Narrow choke run: visual compression between cliff wall, edge chocks, and warning dressing; it is composition language only, not a gameplay choke.
- Widened staging shelf: broader visual rest beat with reinforced edge treatment, rough earth, stone anchors, and a few Blood Axe identifiers; it is not spawn, encounter, or AI staging.

Model future large forms only if separately approved: broad shelf planes, major edge lips, large embedded stones, half-buried logs, rough chocks, heavy lashings, big red cloth scraps, and major side silhouette pieces. Push tiny cracks, grit, pebbles, wood grain, ash speckles, rope fibers, scratches, cloth weave, stitch rows, bark chips, and fine mud breakup into textures, normals, AO, or masks.

Avoid clean stone roads, civilized Giant civic markers, blue rune road language, polished bridge craft, UI arrows, readable tactical text, dense trophy clutter, graphic gore, tiny modeled debris fields, or anything that makes Blood Axe raider culture the default Giant identity.

## LOD Plan

All future approved static modules require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full route-section read, broad shelf shape, edge lips, stone chocks, half-buried logs, mud shoulders, major ruts, large lashings, sparse warning cloth, and Giant-scale silhouette.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small stones, minor edge undercuts, small lashings, cloth cuts, and hidden backs.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; flatten small rut relief, merge stone groups, reduce log end detail, simplify edge chocks, and remove non-silhouette warning pieces.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve broad route strip, cliff shelf read, rough edge silhouette, large log or stone beats, and sparse Blood Axe red accents.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, stitch rows, rope fibers, ash speckles, pitting, small cracks, cloth weave, and wood grain relief.
2. Small stones, tiny mud clumps, small red scraps, minor edge chips, and little bark cuts.
3. Secondary lashings, small clamp plates, non-silhouette horn or bone tokens, and hidden backs.
4. Log bevel complexity, stone undercuts, rut subdivisions, and shelf edge loops.
5. Only after secondary detail is gone, simplify the main shelf plane, Giant-scale width read, route edge silhouette, and major Blood Axe warning beats.

Never reduce the primary switchback route silhouette, hostile Blood Axe material read, Giant-scale relationship, or cliff-shelf composition before removing small detail.
