`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeLowThresholdCairnPair_Primary_A01` defines the primary paired composition for Blood Axe low-threshold cairns: two squat Giant-scale cairns made from a few large stones, cold ash, soot, mud, cave grit, heavy contact shadows, and optional restrained oxide red cloth. The pair should read as static visual threshold rhythm at a hostile cave approach or raider camp edge, not as a formal doorway, route system, objective marker, or interactive gate.

Blood Axe visual language must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This package belongs only to the hostile Blood Axe sub-faction: crude, soot-stained, cold, abandoned, and threatening. It must not become the default identity for civilized Giant cave towns, hidden highland terraces, skilled blue-gray masonry, waterworks, hearth-warm interiors, orderly stoneworker craft, or restrained blue-rune culture.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any shipping use.

- LOD0: full paired low-cairn composition, two readable squat clusters, few large stones per side, broad chipped planes, cold ash/mud/cave grit bases, optional red cloth strips, major rope/rawhide lashings, and sparse accent pieces.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, underside cuts, cloth edge cuts, lashing subdivisions, minor mud forms, and hidden backside detail while preserving the paired rhythm.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; simplify stones into larger planes, merge small base forms, flatten cloth folds, remove non-silhouette accent geometry, and preserve the left/right threshold beat.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the paired low mound shapes, broad base footprint, dominant stone masses, and one controlled red accent if needed for faction read.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, chipped paint edges, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashing cuts, little knots, secondary cloth tears, tiny wedges, small iron nicks, and non-silhouette tokens.
3. Back-facing accent pieces, hidden underside bevels, buried base cuts, and non-visible mud undercuts.
4. Duplicate base stones, minor cloth holes, secondary chipped planes, and optional accent geometry.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and minor base-plane subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary paired cairn rhythm and broad threshold footprint.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, the paired low-threshold silhouette, Blood Axe red warning read, or the few-large-stones construction before removing small detail.
