`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeTiedBundleSet_A01` defines a reusable set of three to six Giant-scale tied bundle silhouettes for Blood Axe camp repetition. The set should include squat, long, strapped, and overstuffed variants that can dress shelter edges, path bends, wall bases, and raid-camp storage zones without reading as interactive storage or loot.

The set should read as rough hostile Giant field packing: lumpy hide-wrapped masses, broad rawhide lashings, large knots, dark hide covers, soot-dark leather, ash, mud, and sparse oxide-red cloth identifiers. It is camp dressing for a hostile Blood Axe raider environment, not neutral/civilized Giant culture.

This package is docs-only/no DCC/no Unreal/no startup placement/no final approval/no implementation target/no randomized loot/no resource bundles/no destructibility/no economy. It does not authorize source folders, DCC files, FBX exports, Unreal Content, runtime source, validators, startup actors, external source concept handling, global index edits, Hermes files, or Hermes configuration.

Blood Axe visual language must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Do not use civilized Giant cave-town masonry, terrace craft, warm hearth bedding, clean highland textiles, civic stoneworker symbols, restrained blue runes, or refined nomad craft as the default read.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette language should be broad, lumpy, low-to-mid height, and readable from an MMO camera. The set should provide three to six reusable silhouettes, selected from these planning variants:

- Squat bundle: compact ground mass with a low dome, compressed base, one heavy rope band, and one blunt knot.
- Long bundle: stretched travel roll or packed hide mass with tied ends, flattened underside, and broad strap bands across the length.
- Strapped bundle: medium oval bundle with two thick rawhide straps crossing over the top or wrapping around the body.
- Overstuffed bundle: swollen uneven mass with bulging hide panels, cinched sides, and a large visible knot.
- Slumped side bundle: optional half-collapsed bundle leaning to one side, useful for shelter-edge variation.
- Ring-tied bundle: optional bundle with one blackened iron tie ring or crude clamp as a small silhouette break.

Model real geometry for the main bundle masses, broad hide folds, large strap bands, major knots, tied ends, crude rings, and contact flattening. Use texture and normal detail for hide grain, rope fibers, frayed edges, small stitches, soot speckles, dried mud, tiny scratches, and worn red paint.

Avoid tidy merchant parcels, neat civilized luggage, readable treasure sacks, resource bundles, destructible crates, cozy bedding piles, UI-like tags, dense dangling strips, gore, trophy escalation, many tiny knots, or fine high-frequency clutter that will not hold up as mid-poly game art.

## LOD Plan

All future mesh work for this kit requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full silhouette set with squat, long, strapped, overstuffed, and optional slumped or ring-tied variants; broad strap bands; large knots; readable hide folds; sparse red accents; and clear Blood Axe material read.
- LOD1: reduce small folds, minor strap bevels, tiny tie ends, small hardware bevels, underside detail, and secondary contact compression while preserving each variant silhouette.
- LOD2: merge minor folds into normal detail, simplify knots and strap loops, reduce tied-end geometry, remove most hidden back-side detail, and preserve only the strongest red accent block per relevant variant.
- LOD3: retain the broad lumpy bundle masses, Giant-scale footprint, strongest tied-end reads, and dark hide/red Blood Axe read. Remove small hardware, secondary lashings, tiny tags, inner folds, and minor edge breaks.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, rope fibers, fur strands, hide pores, soot speckles, paint chips, and small scratches.
2. Small tie ends, secondary cords, tiny knots, and minor strap cuts.
3. Minor fold lips, underside folds, and back-facing overlaps.
4. Small metal rings, clamp bevels, hidden contact points, and secondary compression detail.
5. Secondary red tags or paint marks if the main Blood Axe accent remains readable.

Never reduce the primary silhouette family, Giant-scale mass, broad strap reads, large knot reads, or variant distinction before removing small detail.
