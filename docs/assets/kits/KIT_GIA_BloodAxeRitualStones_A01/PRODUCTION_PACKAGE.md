## Silhouette Notes

The primary read is a Giant-scale field of rough stone remnants, not an active magic site. Shapes should be large, spare, and readable at MMO camera distance.

Primary silhouette families:

- Standing-stone rings: uneven monolith arcs, broken ring gaps, one or two taller anchor stones, low side stones, and trampled open centers. The ring should read as old warning territory, not a glowing gameplay arena.
- Altar stones: broad low slabs, tipped blocks, heavy support stones, worn top planes, chipped edges, and blackened bases. Keep them non-graphic memory objects, not offering stations.
- Cairn guideposts: stacked field-stone guide piles, paired cairns, cloth-tied guide cairns, ash-stained bases, and moved-camp marker lines. These are visual guideposts only, not waypoint systems.
- Ritual channel stones: dry shallow grooves, split trough stones, eroded carved channels, and capped ends. No liquid flow, emissive channel, damage path, or magic conduit is approved.
- Ritual banner poles: tall rough poles, stone-weighted bases, rope lashings, old red cloth strips, and sparse horn caps. These are static warning and memory dressing only.
- Cave approach markers: low threshold cairns, paired standing stones, stone rows leading toward a cave mouth, and rough red cloth or horn accents. These do not define navigation/pathfinding.
- Review-only rows: simplified scale, silhouette, density, and camera-readability rows that are clearly non-shipping planning artifacts.

Model future large stones, slabs, poles, broad cloth silhouettes, major rope lashings, big horn shapes, large blackened iron clamps, and major ash/mud bases as geometry if separately approved. Keep fine cracks, small chips, soot speckles, cloth weave, rope fibers, tiny scratches, small runes, paint chips, lichen, ash flecks, and mud streaks in textures, normals, AO, or shared trim sheets.

Avoid readable rune text, UI arrows, magical glyph panels, ritual circles as gameplay diagrams, excessive skull density, graphic gore, dense micro-cracks, refined civic masonry, blue runes, warm hearth settlement language, or a one-note red/black palette.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes need LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full large stone forms, altar slabs, cairn stack silhouettes, dry channel groove profiles, pole height, broad static cloth shapes, major horn/bone silhouettes, blackened iron clamps, ash/mud bases, and review-scale rows where separately approved.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small bevels, secondary chips, cloth edge cuts, rope lashing subdivisions, small horn cuts, minor base undercuts, and hidden backside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify stones into larger planes, merge small cairn stones, flatten shallow groove detail, simplify cloth tears, and remove non-silhouette accent pieces.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve ring silhouette, tall stone anchors, altar slab mass, cairn guidepost height, banner pole read, cave approach rhythm, and broad red cloth beats only.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, small chips, cloth weave, rope fibers, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, paint chips, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashings, tiny horn chips, little stone wedges, secondary cloth tears, and small metal nicks.
3. Back-facing cairn stones, underside slab bevels, hidden pole base cuts, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Secondary marker tokens, tiny blade fragments, minor blackened iron clamps, and small cloth knots.
5. Shallow groove subdivisions, stone bevel density, and cloth fold geometry.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the main standing-stone ring, altar slab, cairn guidepost, channel stone, banner pole, or cave approach silhouette.

Never reduce the Giant-scale relationship, static remnant read, Blood Axe red warning accents, or primary stone/pole silhouettes before removing small detail.
