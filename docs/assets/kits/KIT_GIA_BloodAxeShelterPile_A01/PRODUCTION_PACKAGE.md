`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeShelterPile_A01` defines a clustered shelter-side arrangement of Giant-scale hide rolls, rough bedding, one tied bundle, stakes, and a rope coil placed beside a Blood Axe lean-to or hide tent. The pile should read as hostile raider-camp clutter: low, heavy, practical, weather-stained, and improvised from rough hide, fur edges, rawhide lashings, scorched timber stakes, blackened iron, soot, ash, mud, and restrained oxide-red Blood Axe accents.

Blood Axe shelter dressing must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Do not use civilized Giant cave-town masonry, terrace craft, warm hearth bedding, restrained blue runes, refined highland textiles, civic stoneworker symbols, clean nomad craft, or welcoming domestic color language as the default read.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette should be low, wide, uneven, and readable from an MMO camera:

- Overall read: a compact shelter-side pile pushed beside a lean-to post, hide-tent edge, or sheltered dirt patch without owning the shelter structure itself.
- Hide rolls: two or three oversized flattened cylinders, varied in length and diameter, forming the main mass.
- Rough bedding: one broad sagging hide or fur layer partly unfolded under or beside the rolls.
- Tied bundle: one lumpy Giant-scale camp bundle with thick straps, a large knot, and a dark hide cover.
- Stakes: two or three blunt ground stakes or tie anchors at the edge of the pile, kept readable but secondary.
- Rope coil: one oversized flattened rope or rawhide coil near the front or side, with thick visible turns and a blunt tied end.
- Shelter context: the package may include a simple implied edge marker such as a dirt compression line, short fixed lash, or shadow contact, but it must not include a full tent, full lean-to, shelter frame, or structural ownership.

Shape hierarchy:

- First read: shelter-side Giant camp pile.
- Second read: hide rolls and rough bedding.
- Third read: one tied bundle, stake anchors, and rope coil.
- Fourth read: soot, mud, fur edges, rawhide, blackened hardware, and sparse oxide-red accents through material detail.

Model real geometry for main roll masses, bedding thickness, bundle body, large strap bands, big knots, rope coil turns, stake silhouettes, and strong contact shapes. Use textures and normal maps for hide grain, fur strands, rope fibers, fine fray, scratches, soot speckles, ash flecks, dried mud, small stitches, worn paint, and minor pitting.

Avoid a tidy storage heap, merchant display, bedroll inventory rack, loot pile, resource bundle, cozy bedding nook, neutral Giant home camp, ritual trophy mound, gore read, dense rope web, nav blocker, or anything that looks usable by the player.

## LOD Plan

All future shipping-quality mesh work requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full shelter-side composition with varied hide rolls, rough bedding spread, one tied bundle, rope coil, stake anchors, large straps, big knots, restrained red accents, and strong Blood Axe material read.
- LOD1: reduce small folds, underside detail, minor strap bevels, small tie ends, secondary stake cuts, small metal hardware, and minor contact compression while preserving the pile footprint and object count read.
- LOD2: merge minor folds into normal detail, simplify knots and strap loops, reduce rope-coil turn geometry, simplify bedding edge breaks, remove most hidden back-side detail, and preserve one clear red accent block.
- LOD3: retain the broad shelter-side mass, Giant-scale footprint, hide-roll shapes, bedding slab read, bundle bump, rope-coil disk read, and dark hide/red Blood Axe identity. Remove small hardware, secondary lashings, tiny tags, inner folds, and minor stake details.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, rope fibers, fur strands, hide pores, soot speckles, ash flecks, paint chips, and small scratches.
2. Small tie ends, secondary cords, tiny knots, and minor strap cuts.
3. Minor bedding edge breaks, underside folds, back-facing overlaps, and hidden contact compression.
4. Small metal rings, clamp bevels, stake chips, and non-silhouette rope-coil undercuts.
5. Secondary red tags or paint marks if the main Blood Axe accent remains readable.

Never reduce the primary low pile footprint, main hide-roll masses, bedding silhouette, tied-bundle read, or Giant-scale readability before removing small detail.
