## LOD Plan

All important future static low-threshold cairn modules need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separately scoped production import lane uses them. This package does not create LOD source.

Global LOD policy:

- LOD0: preserve full paired low-cairn forms, broad left/right rhythm, height offset, major stone planes, large chipped edges, broad cloth silhouettes, heavy rope or rawhide lashings, ash/mud bases, collapsed-pair readability, and authored material detail.
- LOD1: reduce secondary bevels, small chips, cloth edge cuts, rope subdivisions, minor knots, small stone wedges, minor base undercuts, back-facing stones, and hidden underside forms while keeping the pair readable.
- LOD2: merge smaller stones into larger planes, simplify cloth to broad strips, flatten shallow fold and crack geometry, remove non-silhouette accents, reduce ash/mud ridge geometry, and preserve only the strongest red/stone/ash reads.
- LOD3: preserve primary asset identity only: two squat stone masses or one broad paired composition, one low threshold spacing relationship, the main height contrast, and one restrained Blood Axe material cue where needed.

Reduction order for static paired low-cairn modules:

1. Remove texture-only detail from geometry assumptions first: tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, paint chips, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, mud flecks, minor scratches, horn rings, bone pores, and iron pitting.
2. Remove small accent geometry: small lashings, minor knots, small cloth holes, tiny horn chips, little stone wedges, small metal nicks, small bone tokens, tiny rubble, and optional paint chips.
3. Remove hidden or low-value geometry: back-facing stones, underside slab bevels, buried contact cuts, interior clutter, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Remove secondary decorations: duplicate foot stones, minor blackened iron scraps, small cloth tears, small horn or bone punctuation, and non-silhouette side tokens.
5. Simplify secondary form detail: stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, ash/mud ridge geometry, shallow groove subdivisions, and optional review-row helper detail.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary paired low-cairn rhythm while preserving Giant-scale readability.

Family-specific LOD requirements:

- Paired primary cairns: preserve the two-cluster threshold rhythm and main stone masses through LOD3.
- Asymmetric pairs: preserve the wide/low versus taller companion read through LOD3; remove small offset stones before changing the height relationship.
- Ash-base pairs: preserve broad ash/mud grounding as a value block through LOD3; remove fine ground ridges, grit, and small mud crumbs first.
- Collapsed pairs: preserve the fallen large-stone read and low abandoned profile through LOD3; remove small rubble before altering the main shifted slabs.
- Red-cloth tied pairs: preserve one broad static oxide red cue through LOD3 where the variant depends on it; remove fray, knots, holes, and rope segment detail first.
- Review rows: if future non-shipping geometry exists, simplify aggressively and preserve row comparison intent through layout only. Collision remains disabled.

Never reduce Giant-scale relationship, Blood Axe hostile sub-faction identity, static visual threshold rhythm, controlled red accent restraint, ash/mud grounding read, or primary paired low-cairn silhouettes before small detail is removed.
