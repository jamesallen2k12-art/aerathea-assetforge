It supports abandoned environmental history from a camp that once occupied or crossed an area and then moved on.

The cairn line must remain broken, old, and non-directive. It is not a functional trail, waypoint chain, tracking mechanic, route proof, UI path, spawn guide, patrol guide, encounter lane, quest marker, objective ring, salvage target, pickup object, or final camp-route approval.

It must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture, which remains tied to hidden highland settlements, master stonework, blue-gray civic masonry, terraces, bridges, waterworks, warm hearth light, restrained blue runes, and orderly stoneworker identity.

## Silhouette Notes

Materials must support the moved-camp cairn-line silhouettes without becoming a readable route. The first read should remain interrupted low stone remnants, collapsed cairn beats, broad ash and mud gaps, small cloth traces, and a few old warning accents with missing spaces between them.

Material silhouette support:

- Rough stone should read as a few large highland rocks, collapsed cairn faces, chipped slab planes, and heavy contact shadows. Avoid dense pebble fields or continuous guidepost rhythm.
- Soot should sit in recesses, old burn contact zones, underside pockets, and ash-gap transitions without turning the entire line black.
- Ash should appear as cold, pale, broken deposits between or under stones; it must not form a readable ring, path, arrow, trail, or objective boundary.
- Mud should ground remnants through broad trampled patches, dirty stone feet, and broken scuffs, not footprints or trackable travel marks.
- Oxide red cloth should appear as weathered tied scraps, buried strips, faded knots, or chipped red marks in sparse beats only.
- Rawhide and rope should read as thick Giant-tied lashings or old bindings where they affect a future prop silhouette; fine fibers stay in future texture planning.
- Sparse blackened iron should read as rare clamps, short straps, crude rings, or old hammered fragments only where they reinforce a remnant.
- Old horn and aged bone should remain worn, non-graphic secondary accents, not dense trophy curtains, skull piles, or graphic remains.

Excluded neutral/civilized Giant material silhouettes:

- Refined blue-gray civic masonry, precise cave-town carving, terrace retaining-stone language, waterwork channel marks, polished bridge stone, warm hearth cues, peaceful highland wayfinding, civic mason symbols, restrained blue rune inlays, orderly hidden-settlement finish, or any material read that makes Blood Axe raider residue look like neutral Giant civic craft.

## Materials and Color Palette

Primary moved-camp cairn-line material families:

- Rough stone: dark highland field rock, weathered granite, fractured gray slabs, heavy chipped planes, soot-dark grooves, ash-dusted bases, and muted lichen hints.
- Soot: matte black-gray residue, cold fire stains, old charcoal smears, under-stone shadows, and darkened lash-contact grime.
- Ash: cold pale-gray dust, broken camp residue, uneven dry patches, soft edge deposits, and value breakup around abandoned remnants.
- Mud: trampled brown-gray earth, darker compressed patches, dried splatter bands, dirty stone feet, and old highland dirt.
- Oxide red cloth: faded maroon strips, buried cloth edges, static knots, chipped red paint marks, soot-stained hems, and controlled Blood Axe identity beats.
- Rawhide and rope: thick rope lashings, sinew ties, worn rawhide strips, smoke staining, muddy contact grime, and frayed ends reserved for future texture detail.
- Sparse blackened iron: dull iron clamps, short hammered straps, soot-stained rings, crude plate scraps, and limited worn edges.
- Old horn and aged bone: worn tan fragments, old horn caps, dull bone tokens, rubbed edges, and sparse non-graphic warning accents.

Palette targets are planning values, not final color approval:

Material ratio guidance:

- Rough stone, soot, ash, and mud should carry roughly 80-90 percent of the read.
- Oxide red cloth should stay around 3-7 percent of a composition and must not become a UI cue or full red environment wash.

- Blackened iron should stay sparse, usually below 3 percent unless a separately scoped child asset requires a visible clamp or ring.
- Old horn and aged bone should remain sparse secondary accents, usually below 3 percent combined.

- LOD0: full broad material contrast, rough stone planes, soot and ash grounding, mud contact zones, controlled oxide red cloth, thick rope/rawhide wraps, sparse blackened iron, and sparse horn/bone accents.
- LOD1: preserve major material zones and Blood Axe red/black read; reduce small cloth tears, secondary lashing loops, tiny iron nicks, paint chips, fine cracks, lichen specks, ash flecks, and mud droplets.
- LOD2: keep stone versus cloth, soot versus ash, mud versus rawhide, blackened iron versus soot, and horn/bone accent reads through broad masks and value grouping; remove most non-silhouette material-driven geometry.
- LOD3: preserve the primary moved-camp cairn-line read: dark rough stone, cold ash base value, broken mud grounding, sparse oxide red warning beat, and one or two large accent families.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, fine cracks, cloth weave, rope fibers, rawhide pores, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud droplets, small paint chips, lichen flecks, horn rings, bone pores, and metal pitting.
2. Small knots, little stone chips, secondary cloth cuts, minor hide repairs, small lashing loops, and tiny iron nicks.
3. Small horn or bone tokens, minor non-silhouette accents, and small loose debris.
4. Hidden backside grime, underside detail, interior recess marks, and camera-hidden camp residue.
5. Small cloth fold geometry, shallow groove subdivisions, and secondary rock-plane cuts.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the broad material zones that carry the moved-camp cairn-line read.

Never reduce the no-default-emissive baseline, hostile Blood Axe material identity, Giant-scale readability, broken non-directive spacing, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant materials before reducing small detail.
