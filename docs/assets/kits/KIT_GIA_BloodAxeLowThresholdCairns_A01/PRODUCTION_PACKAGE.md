`KIT_GIA_BloodAxeLowThresholdCairns_A01` defines a reusable planning kit for paired low cairns used as visual threshold rhythm near Blood Axe cave approaches, camp edges, and old hostile territory breaks. The pair should read as a squat, crude, Giant-built warning beat made from a few large stones, cold ash, soot, mud, rough rope, hide ties, and restrained oxide red cloth.

The kit is static visual dressing only. It does not define gate behavior, route validation, path-width rule, nav/pathfinding, encounter lane, quest pointer, collision guarantee, DCC, FBX, Unreal Content, startup placement, final approval, or first implementation target.

Blood Axe language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. These cairns may look rough, raider-made, soot-stained, abandoned, and hostile. They must not become the default identity for civilized Giant cave towns, hidden highland settlements, monumental masonry, terrace systems, waterworks, hearth-warm interiors, blue-gray civic stonework, or restrained blue-rune culture.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette families:

- Paired primary cairns: two squat stone clusters with a clear left/right rhythm, built from a few large stones rather than dense pebble piles.
- Asymmetric pair: one cairn wider and lower, the other slightly taller or offset, so the pair feels hand-placed and hostile without forming a formal doorway.
- Ash-base pair: low cairns sitting in broad cold ash, soot, mud, and cave grit, with the base acting as visual grounding only.
- Collapsed pair: two cairns with shifted large stones, one partly broken down, retaining the paired rhythm without implying destruction behavior.
- Red-cloth tied pair: broad oxide red cloth strips tied around one or both cairns, used as a faction accent rather than a UI cue.
- Spacing review rows: non-shipping planning rows comparing pair width, height variance, red cloth density, and Giant-scale readability.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate implementation signoff.

- LOD0: full paired low-cairn forms, broad asymmetry, major stone planes, red cloth silhouettes, large rope lashings, ash/mud bases, and collapsed-pair readability.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small bevels, secondary chips, cloth edge cuts, rope lashing subdivisions, minor base undercuts, and hidden backside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify stones into larger planes, merge small support stones, flatten cloth folds, remove non-silhouette accent pieces, and simplify ash bases.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the paired low-threshold rhythm, broad red cloth beat, and main stone masses.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, paint chips, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashings, secondary cloth tears, little stone wedges, small horn chips, minor iron nicks, and tiny knots.
3. Back-facing stones, underside bevels, hidden contact cuts, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Secondary marker tokens, duplicate foot stones, small cloth holes, and minor decorative cuts.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and optional spacing-row helper detail.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary paired cairn rhythm.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, static visual threshold rhythm, controlled Blood Axe red warning accents, or the main paired low-cairn silhouettes before removing small detail.
