# KIT_GIA_BloodAxePathMarkers_A01 Production Package

## Art Direction Summary

- Asset name: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxePathMarkers_A01`
- Asset type: Kit production package / static Blood Axe path-marker dressing and review-composition planning
- Parent planning kit: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeCamp_A01`
- Source planning row: `Blood Axe Village.png#Path_CairnMarkers`

- Faction/theme: Blood Axe Tribe, hostile Giant sub-faction only
- Status: docs-only production package ready

`KIT_GIA_BloodAxePathMarkers_A01` defines static, low-to-mid-height hostile route dressing for Blood Axe Giant camp spaces: cairns, cloth stakes, bone/horn markers, broken shield markers, ash-stained bases, mixed marker clusters, and non-shipping review composition rows. The kit should read as crude raider-made path language from MMO camera distance through stacked field stones, scorched stakes, oxide red cloth, old horn, dull bone, broken shield scrap, soot, ash, and mud.

Blood Axe identity must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This kit must not become the default Giant route-marker language, cave-town wayfinding language, stoneworker civic marker set, warm hearth settlement dressing, refined highland trail language, or restrained blue-rune culture package.

This package is planning only. It does not authorize DCC source creation, FBX export, Unreal Content creation, waypoint behavior, trail-marker gameplay, objective logic, nav/pathfinding behavior, pickup/loot behavior, startup placement, source concept copying, final visual approval, or first DCC target selection.

## Gameplay Purpose

The kit supports non-interactive Blood Axe camp readability. Its purpose is to visually clarify hostile route edges, camp approach bends, rough perimeter trails, shelter-to-forge paths, stronghold approach beats, and future review compositions without defining gameplay rules.

Allowed planning uses:

- Mark Blood Axe camp footpaths, switchback edges, trampled-earth route bends, and rough approach lanes as visual dressing only.
- Provide low, repeated hostile markers that complement larger gates, banner lines, watch platforms, shelters, forge props, and stronghold approach pieces.
- Break up broad camp ground planes with crude Giant-made warning clusters sized against the validated Giant baselines.
- Show how cairns, cloth stakes, bone/horn markers, broken shield markers, and ash-stained bases can repeat without becoming UI arrows or quest markers.
- Provide non-shipping review composition rows for later concept, DCC, or Unreal visual checks.

Out of scope:

- Waypoint behavior, trail-marker gameplay, objective logic, nav/pathfinding behavior, pickup/loot behavior, resource nodes, harvesting, salvage rules, crafting/economy systems, AI patrol markers, spawn markers, aggro logic, encounter scripting, combat cover, damage volumes, interaction behavior, destructible behavior, aura volumes, UI markers, VFX pulses, audio cues, material-state gameplay, or startup placement.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- A brutal ground-level and mid-height path-marker language made from crude cairns, short cloth stakes, horn forks, bone tokens, broken shield scraps, and ash-blackened bases.
- Cairns should be chunky, irregular, and Giant-built, with large readable stone masses instead of many tiny pebbles.
- Cloth stakes should use short sharpened timber, hide wraps, and oxide red cloth strips with large torn silhouettes.
- Bone/horn markers should be sparse, non-graphic warning shapes: forked horns, lashed old bones, or blunt trophy tokens without gore.
- Broken shield markers should use large shield fragments, dull scrap plate, rope ties, and red paint smears as hostile warning dressing.
- Ash-stained bases should visibly anchor markers into burned earth, soot, mud, trampled ground, and charcoal rings.
- Review composition rows should read as non-shipping planning rows, not final in-world marker assets.

Model as real geometry when promoted later:

- Main cairn stones, large base stones, stake shafts, major cloth silhouettes, large horn/bone shapes, shield fragments, scrap plates, major rope ties, heavy lashings, and broad ash base shapes.

Keep in textures or normal maps:

- Fine stone cracks, tiny chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, minor fray, rope fibers, leather stitching, small scratches, shield dents, paint chips, mud streaks, and subtle wood grain.

Avoid:

- Dense skull piles, graphic gore, readable text, UI arrows, glowing quest markers, refined civic trail signs, elegant carved stone, blue rune symbols, warm hearth motifs, polished cave-town masonry, or a one-note red-only palette.

- Author all future source in centimeters. 1 Unreal unit = 1 cm.

- Cairn single marker: 80-180 cm tall, 120-300 cm footprint, built from a few large stacked stones.
- Cairn cluster: 120-260 cm tall, 250-600 cm footprint, used sparingly near path bends or approach compression beats.
- Cloth stake marker: 140-280 cm above ground, with 40-160 cm cloth drops or strips.
- Cloth stake pair or row: 250-700 cm combined footprint; spacing should feel rough and uneven, not like a fence.
- Bone/horn marker: 80-220 cm tall, 80-250 cm footprint, using one or two major silhouette pieces.
- Broken shield marker: 100-240 cm tall, 100-280 cm footprint, large enough to read as Giant-scale scrap.
- Ash-stained base: 200-600 cm footprint, very low height; use as ground dressing around markers, not as gameplay trail decal behavior.
- Mixed marker cluster: 300-900 cm footprint, 120-300 cm height range, with one dominant silhouette and two or three supporting pieces.
- Review rows: include 442 cm and 470 cm height references beside marker variants for later scale checks.

Normal humanoid compatibility is not required. The kit should feel built and placed by hostile Giants, not resized from human camp dressing.

## LOD Plan

All important future modules require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full cairn stacking, stake shafts, broad cloth silhouettes, horn/bone warning shapes, shield fragments, base ash, large lashings, and readable paint marks.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify stone bevels, reduce cloth edge cuts, merge small ties, reduce horn/bone bevels, and flatten small base chips.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve route-marker silhouette, red cloth/paint read, cairn mass, and shield/horn warning read while removing minor interior cuts and back-side accents.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve broad cairn, stake, cloth, horn, or shield silhouette only; remove most small hardware, base detail, ash clumps, and accent tokens.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny ash flecks, stone chips, cloth holes, nail heads, stitches, scratches, and paint chips.
2. Minor rope fibers, secondary lashings, small ties, and extra wraps.
3. Small pebbles, small bone chips, small horn chips, and minor shield splinters.
4. Interior cloth tears, back-facing shield details, and non-visible underside detail.
5. Bevels on small stones, small stake cuts, and base ground undercuts.
6. Non-shipping review-row helper detail.

Never reduce the Giant-scale marker read, hostile Blood Axe red/black identity, cairn/stake/horn/shield silhouette, or path rhythm before removing small detail.

## Collision Notes

- Review composition rows: collision disabled by default; editor selection bounds only if a later implementation task needs them.
