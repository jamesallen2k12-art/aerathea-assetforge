# SM_GIA_BloodAxeAltarStone_A01 Production Package

## Art Direction Summary

- Asset name: `SM_GIA_BloodAxeAltarStone_A01`
- Asset type: Static Mesh prop, docs-only production package
- Parent planning direction: future `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeRitualStones_A01`
- Faction/theme: Blood Axe Tribe, hostile Giant sub-faction
- Role: static non-graphic memory/warning object only
- Status: planning document only

`SM_GIA_BloodAxeAltarStone_A01` is a massive low stone slab left behind by Blood Axe nomads in abandoned highlands, cave approaches, and old camp thresholds. It should read as a warning, memory marker, and territorial claim through shape and material language, not through mechanics, readable text, gore, or effects.

The asset must separate Blood Axe brutality from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Use rough dark stone, uneven cairn support stones, soot/ash staining, weathered red mineral pigment, raw iron clamps, rope scars, and sparse bone/horn silhouette accents if readable and non-graphic. Avoid refined Giant civic masonry, blue rune craft, warm hearth settlement cues, and polished cave-town stonework.

This package creates no source art, mesh, texture, material, engine asset, validator, placement, or implementation selection. It is only a production planning document for a possible future static mesh.

## Gameplay Purpose

The altar stone supports environmental storytelling around Blood Axe highland routes and abandoned camp remains. It gives players a readable sense that a hostile Giant group claimed or remembered the site without turning the prop into an active system.

Allowed visual planning uses:

- Mark an old Blood Axe territory claim as static dressing.
- Provide a low focal object for abandoned camp remains, cave approaches, snowline passes, and rough highland clearings.
- Reinforce the contrast between Blood Axe nomad warning stones and neutral/civilized Giant culture.
- Pair visually with standing stones, cairn guideposts, banner poles, and warning markers only as non-mechanical scene dressing.

Out of scope:

- Offering mechanics, ritual activation, VFX/audio, damage/aura behavior, interaction behavior, liquid/gore details, quest marker behavior, nav/pathfinding behavior, AI behavior, loot/resource/crafting behavior, destructible behavior, pickup behavior, readable text, startup placement, first implementation target selection, or any runtime logic.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- Massive low slab, broad and heavy, with a rough axe-cut or split-stone profile.
- Uneven cairn support stones beneath the slab, built from a few large readable rocks instead of many pebbles.
- Low, grounded footprint that feels Giant-made and too heavy for normal humanoids to move.
- Soot/ash staining concentrated around the top surface, underside shadows, and support-stone contact points.
- Sparse bone/horn silhouette accents if readable and non-graphic, used as edge markers or lashed side accents rather than trophy piles.
- Optional raw iron clamp, hide tie, or rope-scar read for Blood Axe nomad construction.

Model as future real geometry only after separate approval:

- Main slab mass, broad chipped corners, large cracks that affect silhouette, major support stones, large clamps, thick ties, and any large bone/horn accent.

Keep in texture, normal, or baked detail:

- Hairline cracks, fine stone grain, small chips, soot speckles, ash dust, red pigment wear, lichen, rope fibers, minor scratches, and tiny weathering.

Avoid:

- Tall ceremonial throne reads, refined civic plinths, polished carved runes, skull stacks, fresh remains, liquid channels, UI-arrow shapes, excessive spikes, dense dangling pieces, dense micro-cracks, or a one-note red-only palette.

## LOD Plan

All future static mesh implementation requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: Full low slab silhouette, uneven cairn support stones, broad chips, large clamps/ties, optional sparse bone/horn accents, base wear, and authored material detail.
- LOD1: 55-65 percent of LOD0; simplify underside cuts, reduce support-stone bevels, merge small clamp geometry, and reduce accent edge cuts.
- LOD2: 30-40 percent of LOD0; preserve the slab footprint, main support-stone rhythm, red pigment read, and optional bone/horn silhouette while removing backside and underside detail.
- LOD3: 10-18 percent of LOD0; preserve only the massive low slab, broad base footprint, and largest support-stone masses.

Reduction order:

1. Fine chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen, pigment scratches, and hairline cracks.
2. Minor bevels on support stones, clamps, ties, and underside shapes.
3. Small secondary stones, small tie ends, and small accent notches.
4. Backside and underside detail not visible from normal camera angles.
5. Optional bone/horn accents only after their silhouette stops reading.

Never reduce the massive low slab, uneven cairn support read, or Giant-scale footprint before removing small detail.
