`SM_GIA_BloodAxeTrophyGate_A01` is a Giant-scale trophy entry gate for hostile Blood Axe camps. It should read as an intimidation threshold: two massive rough-log or split-trunk towers, a brutal crossbeam, blackened iron bands, torn red gate drapes, sparse aged trophy markers, and heavy lashings arranged into a strong, readable raider silhouette.

This is not neutral or civilized Giant architecture. Blood Axe visual language must stay separate from remote Giant cave towns, master stonework, blue-gray masonry, terraces, waterworks, warm hearth craft, restrained blue runes, and highland civic culture. The gate communicates a dangerous raider sub-faction through scale, soot, raw timber, blackened metal, red cloth, and restrained trophy symbolism.

This package is planning only. It does not authorize DCC source creation, FBX export, Unreal Content creation, collision proxy creation, material graph work, runtime source, source concept movement, startup placement, final visual approval, first build target selection, interaction behavior, destructible behavior, loot behavior, or trophy mechanics.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette: a hostile Giant-built gate that is wider, taller, heavier, and rougher than any normal humanoid camp gate. The read should be clear from front and three-quarter views: vertical power, an oppressive overhead beam, red warning cloth, and a few large trophy accents.

Core silhouette requirements:

- Two main gate posts or post clusters made from massive split trunks, raw logs, or rough squared timber.
- Heavy top crossbeam or lintel, preferably asymmetrical, with a sagging or crooked raider construction read.
- Wide open passage kept clear for Giant movement; the gate can imply threat without physically cluttering the doorway.
- Blackened iron bands, broad straps, crude plates, and large chain loops used as structural accents.
- Torn red cloth drape, split banner, or hanging gate panel large enough to identify Blood Axe territory.
- Sparse non-graphic trophy details: horn arcs, aged bone plates, old helm fragments, cracked shield pieces, broken weapon heads, carved warning tokens, or wrapped dry bundles.
- Optional side spike clusters or horn markers at the upper corners, kept large and readable.
- Ground anchors, stone weights, log braces, or scrap-metal collars that make the gate feel planted by Giants.

Detail restraint:

- Baseline `A01` should use only a few trophy accents. Avoid trophy curtains, dense charm strings, many small teeth, gore ropes, fresh remains, wet material reads, or piles of small fragments.
- Model the major gate posts, crossbeam, braces, large plates, major cloth panels, broad chains, horn silhouettes, and large trophy forms.
- Push small cracks, rope fibers, stitch lines, nail heads, scratches, soot speckles, wood grain, cloth weave, and tiny metal pitting into textures or normal maps.
- Preserve negative space through the entry. The passage must stay readable and must not collapse into a clutter wall.

## Scale Notes

Use the validated Giant scale lock exactly:

- Female Giant baseline: 442 cm / 14'6".
- Male Giant baseline: 470 cm / 15'5".
- Approved Giant ranges: females 14-15 ft / 427-457 cm; males 14'10"-16'0" / 452-488 cm.
- Author future source in centimeters. 1 Unreal unit = 1 cm.

Recommended static gate scale:

- Clear passage height: 720-850 cm minimum, allowing a 470 cm male Giant plus helmet, banner, weapon, or harness visual clearance.
- Clear passage width: 600-780 cm, wide enough for a Giant to pass without the entry reading as a normal humanoid doorway.
- Overall height: 900-1150 cm including top horns, spikes, upper trophy markers, and banner poles.
- Overall width: 900-1250 cm including post clusters, side braces, and outer trophy silhouettes.
- Main post diameter or post-cluster thickness: 70-140 cm.
- Crossbeam thickness: 60-120 cm, with larger visual mass near the post seats.
- Red drape or gate cloth drop: 250-420 cm, kept above or to the side of the required clear passage unless a future blocked-gate variant is approved.
- Trophy accents: 50-140 cm each, large enough to read as Giant-scale symbolic dressing.
- Base footprint depth: 300-600 cm including braces, stones, collars, and ground anchors.

Smallfolk should read as intruders under an oversized hostile threshold. Do not shrink the gate to normal humanoid fortification scale, and do not claim normal humanoid compatibility as a requirement.

## LOD Plan

All future mesh implementations require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full Giant-scale gate silhouette, post clusters, crossbeam, braces, large metal bands, red drapes, broad cloth tears, sparse trophy markers, base anchors, and major ground-contact forms.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce post bevel density, small brace cuts, secondary cloth folds, minor metal bevels, small trophy undercuts, and backside details.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify post clusters into larger planes, reduce chain-loop segments, merge clamp details, flatten cloth folds, simplify trophy markers, and remove non-silhouette base dressing.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the two-post gate read, high crossbeam, open passage, red cloth block, and major Blood Axe color/material zones.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny rivets, scratches, soot speckles, stitch lines, rope fibers, and fine cracks.
2. Small straps, knots, little nails, minor edge chips, and small cloth holes.
3. Secondary trophy fragments, trophy undercuts, small chain segments, and back-facing tokens.
4. Interior/backside brace detail, hidden post intersections, and non-visible base clutter.
5. Clamp bevels, pole chip complexity, and secondary cloth folds.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary post shapes, crossbeam mass, red drape silhouette, and clear passage outline.

Never reduce the Giant-scale clear opening, oppressive crossbeam read, main post height, red Blood Axe marker, or primary trophy-gate silhouette before removing small detail.
