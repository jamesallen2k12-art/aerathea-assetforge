## 10. LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full covered bundle mass, uneven hidden underform, large tarp folds, rope tie-downs, large knots, mud-caked base, optional red tag, and grounded contact.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Preserve the broad bundle silhouette, ridge, largest folds, rope bands, main knots, and mud-base read while reducing secondary folds and tag thickness.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve the bulky covered mass, rope band read, and grounded lower edge while flattening smaller fold cuts, knot lobes, and tie tails.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0. Preserve a simple low covered mound with broad rope-band impressions and darker base staining. Most rope roundness, tag thickness, and fold detail may become texture-only.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, weave, hide pores, rope fibers, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud grains, small scratches, and tiny fray.
2. Small red tag thickness, fine fold waviness, tiny tie tails, small holes, and minor edge cuts.
3. Secondary folds, underside cuts, small rope bevels, and knot subdivisions.
4. Secondary ridge variation and small lower-edge bunching.
5. Only after these reductions, simplify the main covered mass, largest folds, rope tie-down read, mud-caked base, and Blood Axe material identity.

Never reduce the Giant-scale covered bundle read, hidden-content ambiguity, broad tarp folds, rope tie-down identity, or hostile Blood Axe material language before removing small detail.
