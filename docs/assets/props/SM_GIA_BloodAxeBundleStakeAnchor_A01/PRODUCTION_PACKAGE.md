`SM_GIA_BloodAxeBundleStakeAnchor_A01` is a ground stake, tie ring, and rawhide strap anchor used to visually pin Blood Axe hide rolls, tied bundles, rough bedding, and shelter-edge piles. It should read as crude, practical Giant-scale camp hardware: a heavy hammered stake, blackened tie ring or loop, thick rawhide strap, mud-packed base, and restrained oxide-red Blood Axe mark.

Blood Axe stake anchors must remain hostile raider-camp dressing. They must not become neutral/civilized Giant craft, refined highland tent hardware, clean nomad equipment, cave-town masonry detail, blue-rune marker props, or polished domestic fixtures.

This package is planning documentation only. It does not authorize DCC source work, source folders, sculpt, retopo, UV, bake, collision proxy authoring, proof renders, LOD source, FBX export, Unreal Content, material instances, texture assets, validators, runtime source, Blueprint behavior, startup placement, final visual approval, first DCC target selection, implementation target selection, external concept movement, or Hermes file/configuration work.

## Gameplay Purpose

The anchor supports static Blood Axe camp readability by explaining how oversized rolls, bundles, and bedding stay grounded in rough shelter areas. It is a visual prop only and does not create tent rigging behavior, rope simulation, physics constraints, collision blocking, nav rules, or interaction behavior.

Allowed purpose:

- Static visual anchor near bedroll piles, tied bundles, rope coils, shelter edges, and camp path clutter.
- Scale cue for Giant-sized tie-down hardware.
- Material bridge between hide bundles, rawhide lashings, blackened iron, scorched timber, mud, and oxide-red Blood Axe accents.
- Low foreground detail for future camp compositions without gameplay affordances.

Out of scope:

## LOD Plan

All future shipping-quality mesh work requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full stake shape, battered cap, ring or loop, strap/knot, optional red accent, and mud-packed base if included.
- LOD1: 55-65 percent of LOD0; reduce cap bevels, ring undercuts, knot loops, small strap wrinkles, and mud-base cuts.
- LOD2: 25-40 percent of LOD0; preserve stake height, ring silhouette, one strap read, and base footprint while flattening secondary damage.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0; preserve a simple stake with a ring bump and dark/rawhide material blocks.

Reduction order:

1. Wood grain, iron pits, rawhide fibers, soot speckles, mud flecks, and scratches.
2. Small cap chips, minor strap folds, tiny knot cuts, and red-paint chips.
3. Mud-base irregularities and hidden underside detail.
4. Ring undercuts, non-silhouette bevels, and secondary strap thickness changes.

Never reduce the main stake silhouette, tie-ring read, or thick strap anchor before removing small detail.
