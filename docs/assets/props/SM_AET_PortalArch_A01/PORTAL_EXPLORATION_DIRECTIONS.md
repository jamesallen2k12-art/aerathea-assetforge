# SM_AET_PortalArch_A01 Portal Exploration Directions

## Purpose

The current portal arch is a first-pass review direction, not a final locked art
target. Aerathea portals should be universal, ancient, mysterious,
awe-inspiring, and large enough to make normal player characters feel small.
Final portal design should be selected from several visual directions before any
DCC rebuild or final Unreal signoff.

## Locked Requirements

- Base portal is universal and race-neutral.
- Race or faction portals may become skins or alternate meshes later, but should
  keep the same shared gameplay logic.
- Minimum clear traversal height: 1000 cm / 10 m / about 33 ft.
- Must accommodate Giants, major NPCs, large enemies, and epic dungeon, raid, or
  city traversal.
- Normal players should feel small beside the portal.
- Visual tone: old, mysterious, awe-inspiring, magical, and world-scale.
- Glow is restrained; mystery should come from scale, silhouette, age, and
  portal depth rather than excessive particles.
- Use large readable forms, not dense micro-detail.

## Ten Exploration Directions

1. Ancient Megalith Ring: enormous broken standing stones forming a rough circle,
   with blue Aetherium veins inside old cracks and a deep portal aperture.
2. Titan Gate Arch: massive squared stone uprights and a single impossible
   capstone, like a forgotten pre-kingdom construction.
3. Sunken Ruin Portal: half-buried ancient city gate with worn steps, buried
   symbols, and a vertical blue core rising from the ground.
4. Astral Observatory Portal: old stone-and-brass alignment rings, restrained
   star-map grooves, and a calm blue aperture.
5. Hollow Mountain Door: carved cliff-face portal, monumental but simple, with
   stone channels and a blue inner void.
6. Broken God-Road Gate: cracked road stones leading through a huge shattered
   arch, implying a lost network older than modern kingdoms.
7. Root-Stone Convergence: ancient stone blocks partly claimed by roots and
   age, with Aetherium seams glowing under moss and dust.
8. Deep Vault Threshold: fortress-like portal with thick dark-iron bands, giant
   locking stones, and a quiet blue core.
9. Floating Keystone Portal: grounded side pylons with several immense suspended
   keystones held by restrained Aetherium force.
10. Spiral Monolith Portal: tall asymmetrical monoliths spiraling around a clear
   10 m opening, old enough to feel discovered rather than built.

## Approval Criteria

Approve a direction only if it:

1. Preserves the 10 m / 33 ft clear traversal requirement.
2. Makes a normal player feel small.
3. Reads clearly from settlement distance.
4. Feels ancient, mysterious, and awe-inspiring.
5. Supports universal portal gameplay rather than one race-specific culture.
6. Can become a mid-poly Unreal asset with LOD0-LOD3.
7. Uses restrained Aetherium glow rather than dense particles.
8. Leaves the opening collision-clean for traversal.
9. Can support later race/faction visual variants.
10. Does not copy existing franchise portal designs.
