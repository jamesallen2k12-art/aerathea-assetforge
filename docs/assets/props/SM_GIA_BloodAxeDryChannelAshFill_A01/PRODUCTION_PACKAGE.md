`SM_GIA_BloodAxeDryChannelAshFill_A01` is a shallow, static, matte, dry ash-fill insert for inactive Blood Axe dry-channel troughs. It should read as cold ash, soot, and old camp residue settled inside a Giant-scale ritual-channel stone, not as a liquid, aura, hazard, magic surface, gameplay volume, or active ritual effect.

Blood Axe identity must remain a hostile Giant sub-faction treatment. This ash fill may support abandoned-camp memory, moved-camp residue, and grim threshold dressing, but it must not become the default material language for neutral/civilized Giants. Exclude refined cave-town masonry, blue-gray civic stonework, terrace or waterwork channel language, warm hearth settlement identity, peaceful highland guide markers, civic mason symbols, restrained blue rune culture, and hidden-settlement polish.

## Silhouette Notes

The silhouette should be intentionally quiet: a shallow irregular fill that sits inside a trough or broken recess and supports the channel stone rather than becoming the main read.

Primary silhouette goals:

- Low, settled ash mass with uneven edges and soft slumped banks.
- Slightly raised center ridges only where they help sell old dry residue.
- Broken, wind-scattered margins that feather into stone trough edges.
- Broad matte value shape visible from above and three-quarter camera angles.
- No vertical plume, splash, ripple, flame, glow, swirl, or moving shape language.

Model as future geometry only where the fill needs a visible shallow surface profile: broad edge banks, a few low undulations, and a simple footprint that fits inside Giant-scale dry channel modules. ## LOD Plan

All important future static variants need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separately approved production import lane uses them.

- LOD0: full shallow footprint, broad ash banks, low settled ridges, feathered edges, subtle trough-fit shape, and broad material value grouping.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce minor ridge loops, secondary edge variation, tiny silhouette bites, and underside detail.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; keep the main matte ash footprint, one or two broad raised forms, and the major soot/ash value separation.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the low dry ash mass, footprint relationship, and broad pale/dark value read.

Reduction order:

1. Remove geometry assumptions for ash grains, soot speckles, powder flecks, tiny chips, small grit, and hairline cracks.
2. Reduce minor edge waviness, shallow non-silhouette ridges, and small surface dimples.
3. Remove hidden underside detail and any undercut that does not affect the top read.
4. Simplify secondary banks and merge small surface planes.
5. Preserve the primary low footprint and dry/no-flow read until the final LOD.

Never let LOD reduction create a puddle, ripple, aura ring, hazard field, quest marker, readable rune, or active flow read.
