`SM_GIA_BloodAxeRoughBeddingPallet_A01` defines a static Giant sleeping pallet for hostile Blood Axe camp dressing. It should read as an oversized, rough, low sleeping platform: log or hide-roll support beneath a rough mat layer, soot-dark hide bedding, broad lumpy depressions, thick rawhide lashings, sparse fur edges, mud-dark contact points, and restrained oxide-red Blood Axe tags.

This is not neutral Giant domestic bedding. Blood Axe pallet language must stay hostile, temporary, dirty, and raider-camp specific. Avoid civilized Giant cave-town stonework, warm hearth blankets, refined highland craft, blue-gray masonry, peaceful clan motifs, restrained blue runes, and any cozy village bedding read.

## Gameplay Purpose

The pallet supports Blood Axe camp readability and environmental storytelling. Its role is visual only: players should understand that hostile Giants sleep, pack, and occupy the camp without introducing bed use, rest systems, loot, or storage behavior.

Expected static uses:

- Shelter-adjacent dressing inside or near Blood Axe lean-tos, hide tents, awnings, sleeping rows, and camp edge clusters.
- Giant-scale reference beside shelters, path edges, cooking pits, rough work areas, and watch-rest zones.
- Low-profile foreground or midground clutter that reinforces camp occupation through soot-dark bedding, hide layers, rope, rawhide, mud, and red identifiers.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette: a long, low, heavy Giant-scale sleeping pallet with uneven support understructure, a rough mat layer, soot-dark bedding mass, and one or two broad folded hide edges. It should read at MMO camera distance as something made for a 470 cm male Giant, not a normal humanoid bed scaled up without design changes.

Key visual reads:

- Long rectangular or slightly tapered footprint, 500-650 cm long, with uneven sides and a sagging center line.
- Low log cradle, split-log rails, hide-roll support, or rough timber sled base visible under the mat at a few points.
- Broad rough mat layer made from hide, bark matting, reed-like rough weave, or patched leather strips, kept chunky and readable.
- Soot-dark bedding layer with lumpy compression, large fold ridges, and broad contact depressions.
- Thick rawhide lashings or rope bands crossing the support and bedding in two or three large readable positions.
- Sparse fur edge breaks, dirty bedding flaps, and one restrained oxide-red cloth tag or repair strip for Blood Axe identity.
- Optional small blackened iron ring or crude clamp only if it helps anchor a strap or support.

Model the support logs or hide supports, mat slab, main bedding mass, large fold edges, big straps, large knots, and major sag forms as real geometry. Use texture and normal maps for hide grain, cloth weave, fur texture, soot speckles, tiny stitches, rope fibers, small cracks, leather pores, mud spatters, and minor scratches.

Avoid clean bed frames, pillows, blankets, mattress comfort language, blue-rune Giant culture, refined carpentry, dense dangling strips, many little knots, tiny stitch geometry, gore, trophy bedding, or any shape that implies a player-usable rest object.

## LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: 3k-6k tris target, 8k hard cap. Full long low pallet footprint, support logs or hide-roll base, rough mat layer, soot-dark bedding mass, broad folds, large knots, strap bands, fur-edge breaks, red cloth accent, and underside contact detail.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce secondary folds, small edge cuts, minor support bevels, knot subdivisions, small fur breaks, and non-silhouette underside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve length, width, low height, support/base read, dark bedding color block, main fold edge, and red accent while flattening small wrinkles and removing secondary lashings.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0. Preserve the Giant-scale long rectangle, low camp-bedding mass, dark Blood Axe palette, broad support shadow, and red accent. Remove most lashings, knots, underside detail, small folds, and fur-edge cuts.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, fray threads, rope fibers, fur strands, soot speckles, scratches, and small pitting.
2. Minor cloth holes, small mat cuts, tiny mud clumps, and fine hide edge breaks.
3. Secondary lashings, small straps, small knots, and small clamp bevels.
4. Back-side or underside detail not visible from normal camp camera angles.
5. Support-log bevels and mat-layer subdivisions.
6. Only after these reductions, simplify the main low pallet footprint, broad bedding mass, support silhouette, and red Blood Axe accent.

Never reduce the Giant-scale length, low broad sleeping footprint, soot-dark bedding mass, or Blood Axe red-cloth signal before removing small detail.
