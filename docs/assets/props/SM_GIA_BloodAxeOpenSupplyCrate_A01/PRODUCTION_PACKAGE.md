## 10. LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full open crate body, thick uneven plank walls, high rim, visible interior lip, two to four broad covered shapes, major rope lashings, large knots, corner caps, blackened straps, underside skids, and restrained red accent.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce plank bevels, secondary rim cuts, knot subdivisions, lashing roundness, minor covered-shape folds, small cap bevels, and hidden interior floor detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve the open-top crate silhouette, rough rectangular mass, largest covered interior shapes, major corner reinforcement, and strongest exterior lashings while flattening minor folds and plank variation.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0. Preserve a blocky open crate with dark interior fill impression, broad rim, heavy ground footprint, and a simple Blood Axe red accent block. Remove most knot detail, small straps, small caps, underside cuts, and minor covered folds.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, rope fibers, cloth weave, hide pores, soot speckles, mud flecks, small scratches, wood grain, and metal pitting.
2. Fine fray, tiny splinters, small cloth tears, small paint chips, and minor plank edge cuts.
3. Secondary lashing loops, small knots, small cap bevels, extra nails, and non-structural tie ends.
4. Hidden underside, interior floor detail behind covered masses, and back-facing reinforcement.
5. Secondary plank height variation, extra rim subdivisions, and minor covered-shape folds.
6. Only after these reductions, simplify the open crate silhouette, rim read, main covered interior masses, and hostile Blood Axe material identity.

Never reduce the Giant-scale open-crate read, lidless top opening, primary covered interior masses, or Blood Axe faction separation before removing small detail.
