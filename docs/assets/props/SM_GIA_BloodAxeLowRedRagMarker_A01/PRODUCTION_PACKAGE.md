## 1. Art Direction Summary

- Asset name: `SM_GIA_BloodAxeLowRedRagMarker_A01`
- Asset type: Static Mesh prop production package, docs-only
- Parent kit: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxePathMarkers_A01`

`SM_GIA_BloodAxeLowRedRagMarker_A01` is a low, crude Blood Axe route-dressing prop: a torn oxide-red rag tied near the ground to a stub stake, broken branch, or stone-weighted stick. It is meant to read from MMO camera distance as a hostile near-ground visual cue for a rough camp path bend or direction change, using one broad cloth shape rather than many small scraps.

The design belongs only to the Blood Axe Tribe. It must remain visually separate from neutral and civilized Giant culture, which uses mountain stonework, cave-town masonry, terraces, waterworks, warm hearth light, refined highland settlement cues, and restrained blue rune or storm language. This marker should feel improvised by brutal raiders, not crafted by Giant masons, civic builders, or nomadic highland guides.

This package is docs-only. It does not authorize collectible flag behavior, capture marker behavior, patrol marker behavior, trail gameplay, DCC work, Unreal work, startup placement, final approval, visual signoff, implementation target selection, or Hermes work.

## 2. Gameplay Purpose

The marker supports non-interactive environment readability in Blood Axe camps and approaches. Its role is to help artists compose near-ground path turns, ground-plane breaks, camp edge accents, and hostile faction rhythm without defining gameplay systems.

Allowed purpose:

- Static visual direction-change dressing for Blood Axe camp paths.
- Near-ground color accent that complements cairns, cloth stakes, ash bases, broken shield scraps, and horn or bone markers in the parent kit.
- Small-scale hostile sub-faction storytelling through torn cloth, scorched wood, crude rope, field stones, soot, and trampled mud.
- Reusable dressing piece for rough bends, shelter approaches, stronghold approach edges, or camp interior route beats after a separate authorized asset task.

Out of scope:

- No collectible flag, capture marker, patrol marker, trail gameplay, waypoint behavior, objective logic, nav/pathfinding helper, interaction prompt, pickup behavior, loot behavior, resource node, trap, tripwire, combat cover, spawn marker, AI marker, UI marker, VFX pulse, audio cue, material-state gameplay, or faction buff.

## 3. Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- Low rag silhouette with one broad torn red cloth shape, roughly triangular or ragged rectangular, slumped close to the ground.
- Stub stake or broken stick angled slightly off vertical, short enough to feel like ground dressing rather than a banner pole.
- Optional stone-tied base with one or two large field stones pressing the stick into mud.
- Crude hide strip or rope lashing that clearly binds the cloth to the stake.
- Uneven Blood Axe read: scorched, dirty, field-made, and hostile without becoming graphic or ornate.

Shape priorities:

- The cloth must be the main read, using a single bold red mass with two or three large tears.
- The support should be squat and chunky, not elegant, carved, polished, or civic.
- The base should visually anchor into ash, soot, mud, or trampled earth without becoming a separate ash pad package.
- The marker should sit below taller cloth stakes from the parent kit so it fills the near-ground lane.

Avoid:

- Tall banner-pole silhouettes, pennant lines, UI-arrow shapes, readable symbols, refined carved guideposts, polished waystones, dense rag tassels, skull piles, gore escalation, glowing runes, and civilized Giant route-marker language.

## 10. LOD Plan

Create LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 for any future shipping version.

- LOD0: Full broad cloth silhouette, major cloth sag, stake shape, primary rope/hide tie, optional large stones, and base contact.
- LOD1: Reduce cloth edge cuts, simplify rope wrap, reduce stake bevels, and simplify stone bevels while preserving the red rag read.
- LOD2: Preserve a simple red cloth mass and stake silhouette; flatten most folds, remove minor base cuts, and merge lashings into the texture.
- LOD3: Keep only the low red cloth mass, short support silhouette, and a simple base shape if present.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cloth holes, ash flecks, mud spots, small scratches, and paint chips.
2. Minor cloth edge cuts and small fray shapes.
3. Secondary rope turns, hide-tail ends, and tiny knots.
4. Small stone chips, stake splinters, bark cuts, and underside detail.
5. Minor cloth folds and back-facing geometry.

Never remove the low red rag silhouette, short support read, or Blood Axe red/black hostile identity before removing small detail.
