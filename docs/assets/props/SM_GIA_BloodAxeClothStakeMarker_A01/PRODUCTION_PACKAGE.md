`SM_GIA_BloodAxeClothStakeMarker_A01` is a crude, static Blood Axe path marker built from a single blackened timber stake, rough hide wrapping, rawhide or rope lashing, and one broad oxide red cloth strip. It should read quickly from an MMO camera as hostile camp route dressing: low enough to sit below banner-line language, large enough to feel made by Giants, and simple enough to repeat along paths without becoming visual noise.

Blood Axe identity must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This marker belongs to the hostile raider sub-faction associated with rough camps, enslavement, slaughter, red banners, trophy armor, and ritual standing stones. It must not become the default Giant route-marker language, civic cave-town marker, refined highland trail sign, stoneworker wayfinding prop, or warm hearth settlement dressing.

This package is planning documentation only. It does not create source meshes, textures, engine content, validators, runtime behavior, review captures, external concept edits, or any approval claim beyond this written package.

## 2. Gameplay Purpose

The marker supports static visual route readability inside Blood Axe Giant camp and stronghold approach spaces. It gives level artists a simple hostile dressing element for path bends, trampled camp lanes, burned clearings, perimeter trails, and repeated warning beats without defining gameplay systems.

Allowed purpose:

- Mark a hostile Blood Axe route edge visually through red cloth, scorched timber, and ash-dark material language.
- Add low-to-mid-height rhythm between larger cairns, broken shield markers, horn markers, banner lines, palisades, gates, forge pieces, and shelters.
- Break up broad ground planes with one readable Giant-made marker that can be repeated in sparse placement.
- Reinforce faction tone without readable text, sign icons, magic glow, gore escalation, or player-facing marker affordances.

Out of scope:

- No cloth simulation, wind animation, UI arrow, waypoint logic, trail-marker gameplay, objective behavior, nav/pathfinding helper, pickup behavior, loot behavior, resource behavior, crafting/economy behavior, AI behavior, spawn marker, combat cover definition, damage volume, audio cue, VFX pulse, aura, interaction behavior, startup placement, final visual approval, or first implementation target selection.

## 3. Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- One short, crude, slightly uneven vertical stake with a hacked or fire-split top.
- One broad oxide red cloth strip tied around the upper third or upper half of the stake.
- Hide wrap and rope/rawhide lashing forming a thick band that reads as Giant-made and practical.
- Slight forward or side lean is acceptable, but the stake must remain clearly upright and route-marker-like.
- Cloth should have a large torn outline with one or two broad missing chunks, not many tiny rag strands.

## 7. Modeling Notes

This is modeling guidance only for a later authorized build lane. No source asset, sculpt, retopo, UV, bake, export, engine asset, validator, capture, or placement work is part of this task.

Model as real geometry:

- Main stake shaft with a slightly irregular cross-section.
- Hacked, split, or burned top silhouette.
- Major cloth strip shape with broad tears, thick folds, and a readable bottom edge.
- Hide wrap band and major lashing turns around the cloth tie point.
- A small pushed-earth or ash base mound only if needed to ground the prop.

Keep in texture or normal maps:

- Fine wood grain, small burn cracks, ash speckles, soot streaks, cloth weave, small fray fibers, tiny stitch marks, leather pores, rope fibers, mud streaks, and minor scratches.

Construction notes:

- Spend geometry on the stake profile, the broad cloth outline, and the heavy wrap band.
- Keep the cloth as fixed shaped geometry with enough thickness to avoid a paper-thin read at side view.
- Use a small number of large cloth folds; do not model many separate rag strands.
- Keep lashings chunky and readable. Fine fibers belong in the normal map.
- Avoid perfect symmetry. The marker should feel field-made, scorched, and hammered into rough ground by Blood Axe Giants.
- Do not include sockets, attachment points, gameplay locators, readable sign plates, or hidden marker helpers.

## 10. LOD Plan

All shipping-quality versions require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full stake profile, hacked top, broad cloth strip, major cloth folds, hide wrap, heavy lashings, and optional small base mound.
- LOD1: 50-60 percent of LOD0; simplify stake bevels, reduce cloth edge cuts, merge small lashing loops, and flatten minor base forms.
- LOD2: 25-35 percent of LOD0; preserve the upright stake, red cloth mass, and wrap band while removing small tears, underside folds, and base chips.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0; keep a simple vertical stake shape with one red cloth plane or thickened strip, plus a minimal base if required.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cloth edge cuts, small holes, ash flecks, soot chips, and small scratches.
2. Minor rope fibers, stitch marks, and secondary wrap ridges.
3. Small cloth folds, underside folds, and back-facing cloth detail.
4. Small base mound pieces, pebble shapes, and mud ridges.
5. Stake bevels and subtle silhouette wiggles.

Never reduce the primary vertical stake, broad oxide red cloth read, hide wrap band, or hostile Blood Axe material identity before removing small detail.
