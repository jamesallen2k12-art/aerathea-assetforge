## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette: an oversized raw-log palisade gate with two massive flanking post clusters, a blunt heavy lintel, an uneven upper spike line, and a central entry read scaled for Giants. The shape should be broad, heavy, hostile, and temporary, as if built quickly by raiders with brute force and stolen hardware.

Key visual reads:

- Two dominant vertical post masses made from rough trunks, split logs, and lashed stake bundles.
- Heavy horizontal crossbeam or lintel that visibly bears weight and frames the entry.
- Uneven sharpened stake crown above the lintel, kept bold rather than crowded.
- Broad blackened iron bands, clamp plates, chain loops, or reforged scrap plates reinforcing the posts.
- Torn red gate drape, warning strips, or side banners for Blood Axe identification.
- Sparse horn, bone, or broken armor markers, non-graphic and limited so the gate does not become the separate `SM_GIA_BloodAxeTrophyGate_A01` variant.
- Static central gate slab, barred insert, or open threshold may be shown as a visual choice, but no moving gate parts are planned by this package.
- Ground-contact base should feel heavy: packed mud, rough stone weights, buried log feet, or iron collars.

Model primary post clusters, lintel, large gate slab or bars if used, major bindings, broad cloth panels, big chains, large spikes, main horn/bone markers, and structural scrap plates as real geometry. Use texture and normal detail for wood grain, soot streaks, small scratches, small nail heads, rope fibers, leather pores, minor chips, cloth weave, mud spatter, and tiny cracks.

Avoid civilized Giant blue-gray masonry, clean stoneworker craft, hearth-hall warmth, restrained blue runes, neutral highland clan banners, refined joinery, dense skull clutter, graphic gore, tiny modeled splinters, and decorative noise that harms the read.

## LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: 22k-30k tris target, 35k hard cap. Full gate silhouette, post clusters, lintel, static slab or bars if used, major braces, spike crown, broad iron bands, large chains, red cloth, sparse bone/horn markers, and mud base.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce secondary log cuts, small bevel loops, minor chips, chain subdivisions, cloth edge cuts, underside hardware, and back-facing detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve the two post masses, lintel, central entry read, spike crown, red cloth blocks, and main metal bands while flattening small bindings, trophy undercuts, and inner brace detail.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0. Preserve the huge gate outline, Blood Axe red accent, dark timber mass, and frame proportions. Remove most small hardware, chains, bone markers, backside geometry, and surface cuts.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, nail heads, stitch marks, rope fibers, splinter cuts, and metal pitting.
2. Small cloth holes, edge fray, minor chips, and mud clumps.
3. Secondary lashings, small straps, small plates, and backside clamps.
4. Minor chain links, trophy undercuts, and small horn/bone pieces.
5. Secondary log bevels, underside details, and side brace subdivisions.
6. Only then reduce the main post clusters, lintel, static gate slab or central bars, spike crown, and red cloth silhouette.

Never destroy the primary Giant-scale gate silhouette, central threshold read, top spike profile, or Blood Axe red cloth signal before removing small detail.
