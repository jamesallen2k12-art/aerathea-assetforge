`SM_GIA_BloodAxeLowThresholdCairn_A01` defines a squat Blood Axe threshold cairn made from a few large stones, cold ash, soot, mud, cave grit, and an optional restrained oxide red cloth tie. It should read as old static threshold dressing near a hostile cave edge, cliff opening, or abandoned shelter approach, not as a route device, gate, objective marker, interactive prop, or polished Giant civic marker.

Blood Axe visual language must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This cairn belongs to the hostile Blood Axe sub-faction: crude, dark, improvised, and threatening. It must not become the default read for neutral Giant cave towns, skilled blue-gray masonry, hidden highland terraces, waterworks, hearth warmth, civic stoneworker identity, or restrained blue-rune culture.

## Silhouette Notes

- Primary read: a squat, heavy cairn of three to seven large stones with an uneven low crown and broad ground footprint.
- The cairn should feel Giant-placed: oversized stones, blunt massing, thick contact points, and a low threshold shape that still reads beside a 470 cm male Giant.
- Overall profile should be wider than tall. Avoid tall guidepost language, gate language, or paired-marker framing.
- Use one dominant base stone, two or three mid stones, and one or two cap stones. Stones may lean or twist slightly but should not become a dense pebble pile.
- Ground the cairn with cold ash, soot-darkened recesses, mud buildup, and cave grit as broad base shapes or texture-led dressing.
- Optional Blood Axe accent: one restrained oxide red cloth tie, dirty red wrap, or faded red paint smear. It should be broad enough to read at distance but too sparse to become a UI cue.
- Keep the silhouette readable from front, side, and three-quarter views. Avoid arrows, readable symbols, magic glyph panels, polished civic cuts, skull piles, graphic gore, dense red flags, or warm neutral Giant settlement cues.
- Model large stone masses, broad chipped planes, major bevels, optional large cloth strip, and any thick lashing as future geometry if a later task approves source work.
- Keep fine cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, rope fibers, lichen specks, mud streaks, chipped paint, and small scratches in textures, normals, AO, or masks.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any shipping use.

- LOD0: full low cairn silhouette, three to seven large stones, broad chipped planes, embedded base, cold ash/mud/cave grit base, optional cloth tie, and any major lashing or iron scrap.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, underside cuts, cloth edge cuts, lashing subdivisions, minor mud forms, and hidden backside detail while preserving the squat cairn read.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; simplify stones into larger planes, merge small base forms, flatten cloth folds, remove non-silhouette accent geometry, and retain one controlled Blood Axe color beat if present.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the low wide mound shape, blunt crown, broad base footprint, and one red cloth or paint accent only if needed for faction read.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, chipped paint edges, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashing cuts, little knots, secondary cloth tears, tiny wedges, small iron nicks, and non-silhouette tokens.
3. Back-facing accent pieces, hidden underside bevels, buried base cuts, and non-visible mud undercuts.
4. Duplicate base stones, minor cloth holes, secondary chipped planes, and optional accent geometry.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and minor base-plane subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary squat cairn silhouette and broad threshold footprint.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, Blood Axe red warning read, the squat low-threshold silhouette, or the few-large-stones construction before removing small detail.
