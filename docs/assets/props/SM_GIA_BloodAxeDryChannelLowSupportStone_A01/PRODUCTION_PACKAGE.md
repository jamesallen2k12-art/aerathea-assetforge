`SM_GIA_BloodAxeDryChannelLowSupportStone_A01` is a squat, static, non-interactive side support or foot stone for dry Blood Axe channel-stone arrangements. It should read as a low, crude Giant-made stone prop tucked under or beside a channel slab to add weight, uneven elevation, and abandoned camp residue without becoming a gameplay object.

The Blood Axe visual language must remain tied to a hostile Giant raider sub-faction and must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Use rough highland stone, soot, cold ash, trampled mud, chipped edges, optional blackened iron scoring, and restrained oxide red traces. Do not use refined cave-town masonry, blue-gray civic stonework, terrace or waterwork motifs, warm hearth identity, restrained blue rune language, peaceful guidepost language, or polished Giant stoneworker finish.

This support stone is a static visual prop only. It is not a tactical cover object, not a route gate, not an interactable, not a traversal marker, not a physics prop, and not a candidate for gameplay behavior.

The gameplay purpose is visual dressing only. The support stone helps a dry channel-stone layout feel Giant-made, heavy, uneven, old, and abandoned without defining mechanics.

## LOD Plan

All important future static mesh variants require LOD0-LOD3 before any production import lane uses them.

- LOD0: Full squat stone silhouette, flattened bearing top, large chipped corners, compressed base contact, optional broad accent scar, and readable baked detail.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, minor underside cuts, tiny base clumps, and small non-silhouette breaks.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; preserve the low block footprint, main bearing plane, largest chipped corner, and broad ash/soot value read.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the squat support mass, low wedge/block profile, base contact value, and one Blood Axe accent read if needed.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud droplets, paint chips, lichen flecks, minor scratches, and iron pitting.
2. Small chips, tiny base stones, little surface notches, and minor accent scars.
3. Hidden underside bevels, backside cuts, and non-visible base-contact undercuts.
4. Optional secondary accent geometry, duplicate small wedges, and non-silhouette rubble.
5. Secondary bevel density and shallow plane subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary squat support silhouette and bearing-plane read.

Never reduce the Giant-scale relationship, low support identity, static remnant read, Blood Axe hostile material accents, or primary stone mass before removing small detail.
