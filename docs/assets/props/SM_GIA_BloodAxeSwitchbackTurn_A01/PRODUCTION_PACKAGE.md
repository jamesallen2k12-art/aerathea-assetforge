It should read as a Giant-built hostile approach turn: a heavy trampled mud shelf pressed into fractured cliff terrain, with raised mud banks, rough stone-chocked edges, half-buried logs, blackened scrap weights, and sparse oxide-red Blood Axe warning accents.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette: a wide crescent or hooked shelf-turn cut into rough highland terrain, with an oversized mud-packed inside bend, a heavier outer edge bank, and chunky side silhouettes that imply Blood Axe Giants forced a path through the cliff rather than engineered a refined road.

Key visual reads:

- Broad hairpin curve that reads from top-down, three-quarter, and approach-facing views.
- Thick outside mud bank or stone-chocked edge that frames the turn and prevents the path from reading like a flat decal.
- Compressed inside bend with churned mud, dragged scrape marks, gravel, ash, and boot-worn value contrast.
- Raised outer edge with half-buried logs, large field stones, dark scrap plates, or rough timber stops used sparingly as silhouette anchors.
- Optional inside-corner mud mound or rock shelf that makes the turn feel hand-cut and brutal.
- One to three Blood Axe identifiers: torn red cloth strips, chipped oxide-red paint, warning stakes, broken shield scrap, or non-graphic horn markers.
- Cliff-facing side should feel shadowed and heavy; open-edge side should have readable banks, log chocks, and crude warning silhouettes.
- The module should complement straight shelf path sections without defining exact modular snapping, route scripting, or terrain integration.

Model future real geometry for the main curved shelf mass, broad bank lips, major log or stone chocks, large scrap weights, large mud mounds, and primary warning markers. ## LOD Plan

All future implementations require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: 6k-12k tris target, 15k hard cap. Full crescent shelf-turn silhouette, outer mud bank, inside bend mass, major stone chocks, log edge pieces, broad ruts, scrap weights, and sparse Blood Axe marker accents.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce bank bevel cuts, small stone subdivisions, log end chips, minor mud relief, cloth edge cuts, and secondary scrap bevels while preserving the hairpin turn read.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Merge smaller stones and mud forms, flatten minor rut relief, simplify logs, reduce marker backs, and keep the broad curved path band, outer edge silhouette, and red accent read.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0. Preserve the hooked shelf-turn footprint, large outer bank, cliff-edge value block, and one readable Blood Axe red accent. Remove most small debris, individual stones, secondary logs, scrap weights, and minor bend detail.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny mud cracks, pebble noise, ash specks, bark chips, cloth fibers, scratches, and metal pitting.
2. Small stones, minor mud clumps, tiny red scraps, shield chips, and grass remnants.
3. Secondary log cuts, small edge bevels, and subtle rut subdivisions.
4. Backside detail, underside bank detail, and non-silhouette scrap.
5. Secondary warning markers, minor stone undercuts, and small chock pieces.
6. Only then reduce the primary crescent shelf-turn outline, outer mud bank, inside-bend mass, and main edge silhouettes.

Never destroy the broad hairpin silhouette, Giant-scale route read, hostile edge-bank profile, or sparse Blood Axe red warning identity before removing small detail.
