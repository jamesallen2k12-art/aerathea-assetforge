`SM_GIA_BloodAxeRitualBannerPole_A01` is a static Giant-scale warning and memory prop for abandoned Blood Axe ritual-stone approaches, rough camp edges, and hostile threshold dressing. The first read should be a tall uneven timber pole held by heavy stones and crude lashings, with old oxide red cloth as a controlled Blood Axe accent and an optional horn cap as sparse silhouette punctuation.

Blood Axe visual language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This prop belongs to a hostile raider sub-faction and must not replace Giant cave-town masonry, highland stoneworker craft, warm hearth settlement language, terrace or waterwork motifs, peaceful clan wayfinding, refined civic banners, or restrained blue-rune culture.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any production import lane uses them.

- LOD0: full pole profile, split top, broad static cloth strips, rope and rawhide lashings, major knots, stone-weighted base, optional horn cap, sparse blackened iron accents, and clear ground contact.
- LOD1: reduce rope wrap segments, simplify stone bevels, merge small cloth tears, lower horn-cap bevel density, reduce pole split interior cuts, and remove hidden back-side detail.
- LOD2: preserve tall vertical rhythm, old oxide red cloth read, stone-weight mass, and horn punctuation while removing secondary lashings, minor knots, small hardware, and non-silhouette cloth holes.
- LOD3: preserve pole height, one broad red cloth block, base mass, and optional horn read; remove most rope detail, small hardware, interior cuts, tiny chips, and secondary torn edges.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny fray, cloth weave, rope fibers, wood grain, soot speckles, ash flecks, paint chips, small scratches, and minor cracks.
2. Small knots, small wraps, minor ties, little stone chips, horn chips, and small iron nicks.
3. Interior cloth cuts, small hanging strips, hidden base undercuts, back-facing rope loops, and underside stone bevels.
4. Minor blackened iron hardware, secondary cloth knots, duplicate foot stones, and non-silhouette scrap.
5. Pole bevel complexity, split-top interior cuts, shallow cloth folds, and secondary stone cuts.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary pole, cloth, base, and horn silhouettes.

Never reduce the Giant-scale pole rhythm, static old red cloth read, stone-weight mass, hostile Blood Axe identity, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant culture before removing small detail.
