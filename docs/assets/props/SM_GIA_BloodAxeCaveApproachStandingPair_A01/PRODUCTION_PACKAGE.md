`SM_GIA_BloodAxeCaveApproachStandingPair_A01` defines a single static prop composition made from two rough standing stones that visually frame a cave approach, cliff opening, or abandoned shelter edge in Blood Axe territory. The pair should read as crude Giant-placed threshold stones: asymmetric, heavy, soot-stained, partly wedged into mud and ash, and marked with restrained oxide red cloth or paint.

This asset is hostile Blood Axe Giant language only. It must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture, which remains tied to hidden highland settlements, skilled blue-gray masonry, terraces, waterworks, hearth warmth, restrained civic rune language, and master stoneworker identity. The standing pair should feel threatening and improvised, not refined, peaceful, civic, or magical.

## Silhouette Notes

- Primary read: two upright rough stone slabs forming an uneven visual threshold. One stone should be taller and more vertical; the other should be shorter, broader, or slightly leaning.
- The pair should frame space visually without becoming a functional doorway, objective frame, gate, portal, trigger marker, or pathing device.
- Each stone should have a blunt fractured crown, broad chipped planes, irregular side cuts, and a heavy embedded base.
- The gap between stones should read as a hostile cave-edge framing beat, not as an approved passage size or gameplay lane.
- Use two or three large chock stones, mud buildup, cold ash, and soot at the bases to ground the pair.
- Add one or two restrained Blood Axe accents: faded oxide red cloth tied high enough for Giant hands, chipped dirty red paint, sparse hide lashings, or a single old horn or dull bone token.
- Keep the silhouette readable from front, side, and three-quarter views. Avoid clean obelisks, UI arrows, readable letters, magic glyph panels, polished monuments, or symmetrical civic markers.
- Model large stone masses, major chips, base stones, broad cloth strips, thick lashings, and any large horn shape as geometry if a later task approves source work.
- Keep fine cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, rope fibers, mud streaks, lichen specks, paint chips, and stone pitting in textures, normals, AO, or masks.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any shipping use.

- LOD0: full paired-stone silhouette, asymmetric height read, fractured slab planes, embedded bases, chock stones, broad cloth or paint accents, major lashings, and grounded mud/ash forms.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, minor base stones, cloth edge cuts, lashing subdivisions, and hidden backside detail while preserving the two-stone threshold read.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; simplify each slab into larger planes, merge or remove smaller chock stones, flatten cloth folds, reduce accent geometry, and keep only the strongest red faction beat.
- LOD3: 12-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the tall/short paired silhouette, main gap shape, broad base footprint, and one Blood Axe color or material accent.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen specks, chipped paint edges, cloth weave, rope fibers, fray, and minor scratches.
2. Small lashing cuts, little knots, secondary cloth tears, small stone wedges, horn chips, iron nicks, and non-silhouette tokens.
3. Back-facing accent pieces, hidden underside bevels, buried base cuts, and non-visible mud undercuts.
4. Secondary fracture cuts, duplicate base stones, minor cloth holes, and optional accent geometry.
5. Stone bevel density, cloth fold geometry, and minor base-plane subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary tall/short slab silhouettes and the visual gap between them.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, Blood Axe red warning read, the asymmetric standing-pair silhouette, or the cave-approach visual framing before removing small detail.
