# SM_GIA_BloodAxeTrophyHelm_A01 Production Package

## Art Direction Summary

- Asset name: `SM_GIA_BloodAxeTrophyHelm_A01`
- Asset type: Static Mesh prop / trophy helm and faceguard production package
- Source kit: `KIT_GIA_BloodAxeArmory_A01`
- Source child reference: `BloodAxeArmory.png#Armor_TrophyHelmFaceGuard`
- Faction/theme: Blood Axe Tribe, hostile Giant sub-faction
- Status: docs-only production package ready; DCC, FBX, Unreal Content, skeletal fitting, and wearable approval are not included
- Source-storage guardrail: keep the source concept external; do not copy, move, edit, embed, or commit source concept imagery as part of this package.

`SM_GIA_BloodAxeTrophyHelm_A01` is a brutal Giant-scale trophy helm with a skull-forward faceguard, heavy blackened metal brow, broad cheek plates, rough leather bindings, reduced hanging strips, and restrained trophy-chain language. It should read as hostile Blood Axe war gear from a distance, but it must not redefine neutral or civilized Giant culture. Civilized Giants remain remote mountain stoneworkers and highland people; the Blood Axe look is a separate raider sub-faction marked by red cloth, skull trophies, soot-dark metal, and intimidation.

This package plans a static mesh asset and future wearable candidate only. It does not fit the helm to a skeleton or approve wearable implementation.

## Gameplay Purpose

The helm supports Blood Axe Giant raider identity as camp dressing, armory display, loot silhouette, boss-room intimidation prop, or future approval-gated gear module for elite raiders and chieftains. Its main gameplay read is faction recognition: players should recognize hostile Blood Axe equipment through the skull faceguard, huge scale, blackened reforged metal, and red warning accents without relying on dense dangling clutter.

Expected use cases:

- Static armory prop on racks, tables, stakes, or scrap piles.
- Environmental storytelling in Blood Axe camps, forge corners, barracks, and raider trophy areas.
- Future gear candidate for a Giant head slot after separate skeletal fit, face/neck clearance, animation, and wearable approval.
- Non-graphic trophy marker that implies raider brutality without gore.

Out of scope:

- No skeletal mesh conversion.
- No socket attachment implementation.
- No physics-chain, cloth, or dangling-strip simulation.
- No loot, equip, armor-stat, or enemy behavior implementation.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary read must be a massive Giant helm with an aggressive skull-face front and heavy faceguard, not a normal humanoid helmet enlarged without adjustment. The design should use a broad forehead plate, deep brow shadow, wide jaw/cheek guard, thick side bands, raised crest or bone ridge, and a large open lower face/neck area so the head scale remains believable.

Silhouette priorities:

- Model the skull-forward faceguard, brow plate, cheek plates, neck rim, major horn or bone caps, thick side straps, and a few large chain or trophy elements.
- Keep the face opening readable, with eye, nose, mouth, jaw, and neck clearance visible from front and three-quarter views.
- Use only a small number of large hanging strips or chain drops; reduce tiny dangling elements from the source read so the helm stays MMO-readable.
- Preserve the Giant mass: wide, heavy, rough, and intimidating, with large planes and chunky bevels.
- Avoid excessive skull repetition, tiny chains, tooth clusters, tassels, gore strings, dense rivets, or small charms that would become visual noise.

Blood Axe identity should come from broad shapes and material blocks, not micro-detail. Red cloth, blackened metal, and bone should be visible as large accents.

## Scale Notes

Giant scale lock: female baseline 442 cm / 14'6" and male baseline 470 cm / 15'5", approved ranges females 14-15 ft and males 14'10"-16'0".

Author all future measurements in centimeters. 1 Unreal unit = 1 cm.

Planning scale targets:

- Overall helm height: 85-120 cm from lower neck rim to crest or highest bone cap, depending on final silhouette approval.
- Overall helm width: 90-130 cm across side plates or horn caps, sized for Giant head breadth and heavy hair/braid clearance.
- Faceguard depth: 60-95 cm front to back, with enough forward projection to read as a skull faceguard without clipping the Giant nose, brow, mouth, or jaw.
- Neck opening: wide and low enough to clear the Giant thick neck and upper trapezius mass in neutral pose.
- Eye opening: large enough for readable expression and sightline; do not create a full blank mask unless a future chieftain variant approves it.
- Lower jaw and throat clearance: keep the front lower edge lifted or broken open so the jaw can open and the neck can turn in future wearable review.

Scale and fit notes are planning constraints only. This package does not validate a skeletal fit, animation range, socket placement, skinning, or wearable implementation. Future DCC and Unreal tasks must compare the helm against the validated Giant head, neck, brow, jaw, hair, and shoulder mass before any wearable claim is made.

## Materials And Color Palette

Primary material language:

- Blackened iron and dark steel from the Blood Axe reforged-metal family.
- Hammered scrap plates with broad dents, chipped edges, and uneven plate seams.
- Bone or skull faceguard surfaces in aged ivory, ash gray, and dirty ochre.
- Scorched leather straps, hide padding, sinew wraps, and rough cord.
- Deep oxide red cloth strips or red war-paint slashes as restrained sub-faction identifiers.
- Soot, ash, dried grime, and dull rubbed metal on contact edges.

Recommended color ranges:

- Blackened iron: `#151719` to `#2A2C2E`
- Worn dark steel: `#555A5C` to `#787B78`
- Aged bone: `#A89A7C` to `#D0C1A0`
- Scorched leather: `#2A1A12` to `#5A3825`
- Oxide red cloth or paint: `#5F1513` to `#8B211B`
- Soot and grime: `#0B0A09` to `#403025`

Do not use neutral/civilized Giant blue-gray stoneworker, warm hearth, or restrained rune language as the default for this helm. Any stolen civilized ornament must be visually broken, blackened, or overridden by Blood Axe raider treatment and requires art-direction approval.

Default emissive is not approved. Ritual glow, forge heat, or shamanic markings would be a separate approved variant, not part of `A01`.

## Modeling Notes

This is a docs-only planning package. Future DCC work must start from large readable forms and stop before final fit claims until a separate wearable-review task exists.

Model as real geometry:

- Main metal helm bowl or brow band.
- Skull-forward faceguard planes, including brow, nose bridge, cheek plates, and jaw frame.
- Large bone plates, horn caps, or tooth-like silhouette pieces if approved.
- Primary neck rim, rear band, and side plate overlaps.
- Thick leather straps and major binding bands.
- A few large hanging strips, chain drops, or trophy tabs that remain readable at MMO distance.
- Large dents, broken plate edges, and silhouette chips where they affect the helm outline.

Use textures, normals, or material masks for:

- Fine scratches and pitting.
- Tiny rivets or nail heads.
- Dense chain-link texture.
- Leather pores, stitching, and small strap cuts.
- Bone grain, hairline cracks, and minor chips.
- Soot speckles, grime streaks, and small red-paint flakes.

Face and neck clearance planning:

- Leave an explicit eye slit or eye opening with enough height to avoid hiding the Giant face read.
- Keep nose, mouth, jaw, beard or braid, and neck clearance visible in the model plan.
- Avoid a deep lower mask that would obviously collide with the chin, throat, chest, or shoulder line.
- Keep rear and side plates high enough to avoid shoulder intersection in neutral pose, but do not claim animation clearance until later review.
- Build underside and rear views cleanly because the prop may be displayed on racks or tables.

Trophy-density rule:

- Use no more than a few large trophy elements on `A01`.
- Replace dense dangling clusters with broad cloth strips, large chain loops, or painted/normal detail.
- Do not model dozens of tiny hanging teeth, rings, beads, knots, or tags.

## LOD Plan

Create LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 for any future mesh implementation.

- LOD0: full skull faceguard, brow plate, cheek plates, neck rim, side bands, major bone pieces, a few large hanging strips or chains, primary dents, and readable material-zone breaks.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify secondary bevels, small strap cuts, minor chips, inner underside details, and chain-loop segmentation.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; reduce trophy tabs, merge minor plate overlaps, simplify rear structure, collapse small straps into texture planes, and reduce skull-face interior cuts.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the oversized Giant helm shape, skull-forward faceguard, dark metal versus bone contrast, and red accent blocks.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny rivets, stitches, scratches, and pitting.
2. Small hanging tabs, little chain segments, and minor knots.
3. Secondary plate cuts, small dents, and underside clutter.
4. Rear interior and display-only details.
5. Minor bone chips and strap subdivisions.

Never reduce the primary brow, skull faceguard, Giant-scale width, neck opening, or core Blood Axe color read before removing small detail.
