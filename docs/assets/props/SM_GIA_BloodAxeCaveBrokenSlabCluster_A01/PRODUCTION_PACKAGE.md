## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate implementation signoff.

- LOD0: full slab massing, broad fractured faces, visible thickness, chunky chipped corners, major chock stones, chipped dirty red paint support, and ash/mud base forms.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small bevels, secondary chips, underside cuts, minor chock stone bevels, small ash ridges, and paint-edge geometry.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify slabs into larger planes, merge small chock stones into the base read, flatten ash/mud lips, and remove non-silhouette accent pieces.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the broad broken threshold read, dominant slab silhouettes, low cave-edge grounding, and restrained red paint beat only.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, grit dots, lichen specks, paint chips, stone pitting, and minor scratches.
2. Small chock chips, minor paint edge cuts, tiny stone wedges, small horn chips, minor iron nicks, and shallow mud crumbs.
3. Back-facing stones, underside bevels, hidden contact cuts, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Secondary slab scars, duplicate foot stones, small paint islands, and minor decorative cuts.
5. Slab bevel density, ash/mud ridge geometry, and optional review-row helper detail.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary broken slab silhouettes.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, static visual history, Blood Axe red warning accents, or the main broken slab cluster silhouette before removing small detail.
