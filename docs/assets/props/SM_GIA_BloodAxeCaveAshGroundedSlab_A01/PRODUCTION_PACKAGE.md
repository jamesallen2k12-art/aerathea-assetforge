- LOD0: full slab massing, visible thickness, broad snapped faces, large chipped corners, irregular ash/mud/grit grounding, major mud lips, and sparse red residue support.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small bevels, secondary chips, underside cuts, minor ash ridge loops, small embedded stones, and non-silhouette edge notches.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify slab planes, merge embedded edge stones into the base read, flatten small ash/mud lips, and keep only the broad grounded silhouette.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve one broad slab, low irregular ash-grounded footprint, main stone/ash value contrast, and a restrained Blood Axe red beat if still readable.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, grit dots, lichen specks, paint chips, small scratches, and minor stone pitting.
2. Small edge chips, tiny stone wedges, minor residue islands, shallow mud crumbs, and secondary ash ridges.
3. Hidden underside cuts, back-facing bevels, small embedded stones, and non-visible ground undercuts.
4. Secondary slab scars, duplicate chipped corners, and minor decorative cuts.
5. Slab bevel density, ash/mud ridge geometry, and optional edge contamination.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the main broad slab and grounded silhouette.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, ash-grounded cave-edge memory read, hostile Blood Axe material identity, or the main slab silhouette before removing small detail.
