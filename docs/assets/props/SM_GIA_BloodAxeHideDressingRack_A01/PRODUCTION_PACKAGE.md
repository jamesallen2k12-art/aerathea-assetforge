`SM_GIA_BloodAxeHideDressingRack_A01` defines a large static hide-working rack for hostile Blood Axe Giant raider camps. It should read as a crude outdoor dressing zone: a rough timber A-frame or ladder frame, broad rawhide panels stretched for drying, thick crossbars, blackened iron hooks, oversized scraping boards, heavy lashings, soot-dark tool marks, mud at the feet, and restrained red Blood Axe identifiers.

The rack must stay readable and non-graphic. It should suggest hostile camp utility through scale, wear, and rough construction, not through gore, shock imagery, fresh remains, or dense trophy clutter. Blood Axe visual language must remain a hostile Giant sub-faction marker, not neutral/civilized Giant culture. Do not use blue-gray stoneworker pride, warm hearth identity, restrained blue rune language, refined cave-town masonry, or civilized highland settlement craft.

## Silhouette Notes

The primary silhouette must read at MMO camera distance as a Giant-scale hide dressing and drying rack, not a normal humanoid rack simply scaled upward.

Core read:

- Broad A-frame, ladder frame, or double-post rack with rough timber legs and a heavy ground footprint.
- One or two large stretched hide panels forming the main read, arranged as static shaped planes with bold sag and tension lines.
- Thick crossbars and side braces that feel hand-built by Giants using oversized logs.
- Large hide clamps, blackened iron hooks, and a few broad lashings where they affect silhouette.
- Tool hooks or pegs holding giant scrapers, dull knives, scraping paddles, or hide-stretching wedges as non-interactive dressing.
- Optional low drying rail or side rail for rolled hides, kept as simple readable masses.
- A restrained red cloth tie, painted slash, or marker stake to identify the Blood Axe camp zone.

Model the rack frame, major crossbars, large hide panels, primary pegs, big hooks, scraping board, and major lashings as real geometry. Put fine hide pores, wrinkles, dried edge roughness, stitching, scrape marks, small tears, rope fibers, tiny cracks, pitting, and dirt speckles into texture and normal-map planning, not modeled micro-straps or dense mesh detail.

Avoid graphic dressing: no fresh blood, no recognizable body parts, no hanging carcasses, no piles of remains, no wet gore materials, and no shock imagery. Hide panels should read as cured, stretched, smoked, or drying camp material.

## LOD Plan

All important variants require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full rack frame, broad hide panels, major folds, crossbar bevels, support braces, main hooks, big clamps, scraping board, rolled-hide forms, foot wedges, and Blood Axe red accent.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify timber bevels, reduce minor hide edge cuts, merge small lashings, reduce hook subdivisions, and remove tiny tool undercuts.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve A-frame or ladder-frame read, stretched-hide mass, main crossbars, red accent, and ground footprint while flattening secondary folds and backside tool detail.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the rack silhouette, hide-panel color block, Giant-scale height and width, and Blood Axe red/black read. Remove most small hardware, side clutter, and backside detail.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny hide pores, stitch impressions, dirt speckles, small scratches, and pitting.
2. Small cords, minor straps, secondary knots, and non-structural lashings.
3. Minor hide holes, edge nicks, and inner fold cuts.
4. Small tool bevels, hook undercuts, and backside pegs.
5. Secondary brace bevels and ground-contact chips.
6. Optional side-rail and rolled-hide detail that does not affect recognition.

Never reduce the primary rack frame, Giant-scale footprint, broad hide panels, main crossbars, or Blood Axe red accent before removing small detail.
