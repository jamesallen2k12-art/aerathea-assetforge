## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette: a wide, heavy, metal-forward Giant barricade with overlapping chained plates, broad scrap slabs, broken shield fragments, and blade-fence teeth lashed to a crude support spine. The read should be hostile and brutal at MMO camera distance, but clean enough for mid-poly production.

Required silhouette elements:

- Three to six large hanging or bolted plate slabs, each broad enough to read from the approach path.
- Heavy horizontal chain runs or chain loops that bind the plates into a single barrier rhythm.
- Blade-fence teeth made from broken weapon slabs, cleaver-like offcuts, or split plate corners, kept bold and sparse.
- A crude lower spine made from blackened iron beams, dark timber, or rough stone anchor weights.
- Two or three oversized clamp bars, ring plates, or vertical brace posts that make the chained mass feel intentionally fixed.
- Mud-packed feet, rough stone weights, buried logs, or bent foot plates so the barricade feels planted on stronghold approach terrain.
- Restrained oxide-red warning cloth, chipped red paint marks, or Blood Axe wraps used as faction identifiers.
- Optional broken shield fragments or horn/bone warning pieces only as secondary non-graphic accents.
- End shapes that can visually sit near palisade returns, cliff walls, switchback ledges, or other barricade rows in future composition planning, without defining modular snapping behavior.

Model real geometry for the main plate slabs, blade-fence teeth, broad chain silhouettes, clamp bars, brace posts, support spine, ground anchors, large red cloth strips, and any major shield or horn accent. Use textures, normals, AO, and masks for tiny rivets, fine scratches, pitting, soot speckles, chain scuffs, paint chips, cloth weave, rope fibers, minor dents, quench stains, and small cracks.

## LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: 7k-13k tris target, 16k hard cap. Full chained plate wall, blade-fence teeth, lower support spine, large chain loops, clamp bars, ring plates, brace posts, ground anchors, red warning cloth, and sparse optional marker accents.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce small bevels, secondary plate chips, minor chain subdivisions, cloth edge cuts, small rivets, non-silhouette lashings, and backside hardware.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Simplify plate overlaps, merge or flatten minor chain sections, reduce brace subdivisions, simplify cloth strips, and remove most optional accent undercuts.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0. Preserve overall barricade footprint, heavy plate mass, jagged blade-fence top line, large chain rhythm, dark metal read, grounded base, and red Blood Axe accent blocks.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, pitting, soot speckles, paint flakes, small cracks, nail heads, and micro dents.
2. Small rivets, minor metal band bevels, cloth edge nicks, fine lashing detail, chain scuffs, and small chips.
3. Secondary blade fragments, duplicate small scrap plates, backside clamps, and non-silhouette chain links.
4. Optional horn, bone, shield, or marker undercuts that do not define the primary read.
5. Interior plate overlap detail, underside base forms, and backside brace subdivisions.
6. Only then reduce the main plate slabs, blade-fence tooth rhythm, lower spine, large chains, brace posts, and broad red warning signal.

Never reduce the Giant-scale footprint, heavy chained-plate silhouette, blade-fence rhythm, anchored base, or Blood Axe red/black read before removing small surface and backside detail.
