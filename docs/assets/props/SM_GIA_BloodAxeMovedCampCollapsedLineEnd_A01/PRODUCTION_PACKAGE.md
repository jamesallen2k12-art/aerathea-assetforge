## LOD Plan

All future static mesh implementation requires LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any shipping use.

- LOD0: full collapsed line-end cluster with dominant fallen slab, secondary stones, broad mud/ash base, partly buried cloth, rawhide/rope accents, and sparse optional debris.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, minor fractured cuts, cloth edge subdivisions, small lashings, small mud ridges, and non-visible contact detail while preserving the broken-collapse read.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; merge smaller stones, simplify the fallen slab into broad planes, flatten the mud/ash base, and keep one restrained Blood Axe cloth beat.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the low failed cairn silhouette, mud-sunk footprint, and one subdued red cloth read only.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, fray cuts, rope fibers, paint chips, lichen specks, small scratches, and minor pitting.
2. Small knots, secondary lashings, tiny cloth holes, little stone wedges, small horn or bone chips, and minor iron nicks.
3. Hidden underside detail, back-facing stone contacts, interior overlaps, and non-visible mud/ash undercuts.
4. Optional accent pieces, tiny duplicate foot stones, secondary cloth tears, and non-silhouette debris.
5. Secondary stone bevel density and minor broken-plane cuts.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the dominant fallen slab and main collapsed stone masses.

Never reduce the Giant-scale collapsed-stone read, mud-sunk broken-end silhouette, environmental-history purpose, controlled buried cloth identity, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant culture before removing small detail.
