`SM_GIA_BloodAxeCookingPit_A01` defines an oversized Blood Axe camp cooking and fire pit for hostile Giant raider spaces. The prop should read as brutal, temporary, soot-blackened, and Giant-built: a rough field-stone fire basin, ash banks, charred fuel forms, crude blackened iron support hardware, sparse red warning cloth, and heavy use wear. It is not a neutral Giant hearth, civic fire circle, or civilized cave-town fixture.

This package is static visual production planning only. It does not authorize DCC source creation, FBX export, Unreal Content creation, material graph authoring, VFX, startup placement, source concept movement, interaction behavior, cooking systems, consumables, buffs, resources, economy, final visual approval, or first build target selection.

## Gameplay Purpose

The cooking pit supports Blood Axe camp readability and environmental storytelling. Its gameplay-facing purpose is visual only: players should recognize a hostile Giant raider camp work zone through the oversized fire basin, soot, ash, crude hardware, and red Blood Axe accents.

Expected static uses:

- Camp dressing near shelters, forge zones, path junctions, watch points, and raider staging areas.
- Scale reference for Giant-built daily-use props beside the 442 cm female and 470 cm male Giant baselines.
- Non-graphic camp-life marker that reinforces Blood Axe occupation without becoming gore, loot, crafting, resource, or buff content.
- Composition support for later Blood Axe camp layouts after separate approval.

Out of scope: cooking interaction, consumable creation, food buffs, heat damage, campfire healing, resource gathering, fuel economy, workstation behavior, loot containers, vendor use, quest objectives, destructible pit states, audio, VFX, AI behavior, or encounter scripting.

## Silhouette Notes

The primary silhouette must read from MMO camera distance as a Giant-scale hostile cooking/fire pit, not a small campfire enlarged uniformly.

Core read:

- Broad oval or circular rough-stone fire basin with uneven blocky rim stones.
- Soot-dark center bowl, ash banks, and charcoal shapes grouped into large readable masses.
- Low windbreak stones or slab backs on one side, giving the pit an asymmetrical raider-camp profile.
- Crude blackened iron spit anchors or low support lugs built into the rim.
- Optional simple crossbar support if included in A01; the taller standalone pot/spit frame remains a separate child candidate, `SM_GIA_BloodAxeSpitFrame_A01`.
- Charred fuel stack or split-log bundle staged beside the pit as a large simple form, not a gatherable resource pile.
- Sparse red cloth warning strip, painted slash, or tied marker stake for Blood Axe identity.
- One to three large bone or horn markers at most, kept non-graphic and secondary to the pit shape.

Model the rim stones, basin mass, windbreak slabs, main fuel logs, large iron brackets, simple support lugs, and broad marker stake as real geometry. Use texture and normal detail for soot streaks, small stone cracks, ash flecks, charcoal breakup, wood grain, tiny chips, rope fibers, and scorched stains.

Avoid graphic cooking language: no humanoid remains, no recognizable body parts, no blood pools, no piles of bones as the main read, no hanging carcasses, and no shock imagery. If provisions are needed for a later dressing pass, use generic covered pots, lidded cauldrons, sacks, sealed bundles, or unidentifiable stew silhouettes.

## LOD Plan

All important variants require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full stone ring, basin shape, windbreak slabs, ash banks, large charcoal/fuel shapes, major iron brackets, marker stake, and broad red accent.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify rim-stone bevels, merge smaller charcoal chunks, reduce fuel-log subdivisions, and remove tiny undercuts on brackets.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve oval pit footprint, raised rim, windbreak silhouette, dark basin read, and red marker while flattening secondary ash and stone cuts.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the large ground footprint, dark pit center, rough rim mass, and Blood Axe red/black color block. Remove most small hardware and fuel detail.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny stone chips, ash flecks, small cracks, and soot edge cuts.
2. Small charcoal pieces and minor fuel slivers.
3. Secondary rope or hide ties.
4. Small bracket bevels, rivets, and scrap undercuts.
5. Minor backside stones and non-visible interior details.
6. Marker-stake detail that does not affect recognition.

Never reduce the Giant-scale footprint, raised stone rim, dark basin center, windbreak silhouette, or Blood Axe accent before removing small surface and backside detail.

## Collision Notes

Default collision should be simple and predictable:

- Stone rim: low-count convex hulls or grouped boxes around the outer ring. No per-stone collision.
- Basin center: blocked or ignored based on later placement needs, but no damage, heat, healing, cooking, or interaction volume is authorized here.
- Windbreak slabs: one or two simple boxes or convex hulls.
- Fuel bundle: collision disabled by default, or one simple box if it must block movement.
- Integrated spit support or brackets: fold into simple hulls unless they visibly block traversal.
- Marker stake or red cloth: stake can use a simple capsule or be folded into the pit collision; cloth has no collision.
- Ash banks and ground blend: no collision unless raised enough to affect movement.

Do not add complex collision, per-charcoal collision, physics bodies, destructible pieces, navmesh rules, heat volumes, damage volumes, interactable traces, pickup collision, resource nodes, or gameplay hit shapes in this package. Future navmesh and traversal validation must be handled by a separate implementation task.
