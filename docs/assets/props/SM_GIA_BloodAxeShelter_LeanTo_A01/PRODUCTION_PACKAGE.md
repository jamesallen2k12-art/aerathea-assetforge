## Gameplay Purpose

The lean-to supports static Blood Axe camp readability and environmental storytelling. Its purpose is to make repeated shelter zones feel Giant-built, hostile, temporary, and occupied without adding gameplay systems.

Allowed visual planning uses:

- Repeatable camp-edge shelter for Blood Axe living areas, guard rest zones, forge-adjacent cover silhouettes, path edges, and rough perimeter dressing.
- Scale reference for Giant-sized shelter construction, sleeping space, material thickness, and clearance.
- Visual contrast against neutral/civilized Giant cave-town assets through crude hide, rough timber, soot, mud, blackened iron, and red warning scraps.
- Static set dressing that can later sit near bedding pallets, trophy poles, barricade leaners, awnings, or shelter clutter after separate approval.

Out of scope:

- Player shelter use, rest or sleep interaction, storage, loot, search behavior, crafting, resource gathering, vendors, camp alarms, quest objectives, shelter ownership, fire spread, destructible collapse, cloth simulation, physics, AI cover, nav-blocking rules, encounter scripting, startup placement, or final camp composition approval.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette: a low, asymmetric Giant-scale lean-to with one high rough-timber side, one low anchored side, a heavy sloped hide roof, exposed support logs, large ground stakes, and a dark recessed interior shadow. It should read as hastily built by raiders from available materials, not as finely joined Giant craft.

Key visual reads:

- Sloped wedge profile, high side facing the camp path or work zone and low side braced into the ground.
- Two to four massive upright or angled support logs with irregular chopped ends.
- One dominant ridge pole or front beam, visibly oversized for Giant construction.
- Static hide, leather, or rawhide roof panels stretched over the frame with broad sagging forms, not simulated cloth.
- Large lashings, hide ties, and blackened iron clamps at structural junctions.
- Short side braces, ground pegs, stone weights, or buried log feet that make the shelter feel heavy and temporary.
- Wide Giant-scale front opening with a readable dark interior pocket, but no interactive flap, door, or usable shelter state.
- Muted red warning scraps, torn repair patches, or painted slashes used sparingly as Blood Axe identifiers.
- Optional sparse bone, horn, broken shield, or scrap marker near one side, kept non-graphic and secondary.
- Adjacent bedroll or hide bundle silhouettes may be planned as separate dressing only, not baked into the core lean-to unless later approved.

Model the primary roof planes, support logs, ridge pole, braces, large bindings, iron clamps, heavy stakes, broad red cloth scraps, and major silhouette tears as real geometry. Use texture, normal, and AO detail for hide grain, small stitches, cloth weave, rope fibers, soot streaks, mud spatters, wood grain, tiny chips, minor scratches, and metal pitting.

Avoid dense skull curtains, gore, tiny modeled stitches, hundreds of splinters, excessive dangling strips, fine fray geometry, normal humanoid tent proportions, cozy domestic camp language, and any polished neutral Giant architectural motif.

## LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: 5k-8k tris target, 10k hard cap. Full sloped roof silhouette, major hide panels, front ridge beam, support logs, braces, ground stakes, large lashings, major clamps, broad red cloth scraps, sparse marker accent, mud base, and dark interior pocket.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce minor roof edge cuts, secondary folds, small lashing loops, clamp bevels, small stake bevels, minor cloth holes, and back-facing detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve the wedge roofline, high-side/low-side profile, front opening, main support frame, broad red accent, and dark interior read while flattening small roof folds and removing secondary hardware.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0. Preserve the large sloped shelter block, Giant-scale footprint, front opening color/value contrast, red Blood Axe accent, and dark timber/hide read. Remove most lashings, clamps, small tears, accents, underside forms, and ground dressing.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, fray threads, small scratches, nail heads, paint chips, rope fibers, and metal pitting.
2. Small cloth holes, minor roof cuts, tiny mud clumps, and fine wood chips.
3. Secondary lashings, hide knots, small straps, and small clamp bevels.
4. Sparse marker undercuts, bone/horn chips, and small red cloth edge detail.
5. Back-side braces, underside roof detail, interior non-silhouette forms, and ground dressing.
6. Secondary log bevels and support subdivisions.
7. Only after these reductions, simplify the main roof wedge, front ridge beam, support frame, high/low profile, and front opening.

Never reduce the primary sloped roofline, Giant-scale entrance read, front beam, support log mass, or Blood Axe red-cloth signal before removing small detail.
