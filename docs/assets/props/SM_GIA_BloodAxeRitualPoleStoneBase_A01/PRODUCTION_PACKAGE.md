`SM_GIA_BloodAxeRitualPoleStoneBase_A01` is a heavy static support prop for Blood Axe ritual banner poles. It should read as a crude Giant-handled stone anchor: rough highland stones lashed together with rope and rawhide, packed with mud and soot, and stained by controlled oxide red cloth scraps. The asset supports tall ritual poles visually, but it remains a standalone static base module with no cloth setup, physics setup, socket setup, pickup behavior, interaction behavior, or gameplay function.

Blood Axe visual language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This base belongs to a hostile raider sub-faction and must not use refined cave-town masonry, civic Giant stoneworker craft, warm hearth settlement language, terrace or waterwork motifs, peaceful highland wayfinding, polished clan symbols, or restrained blue-rune culture.

- A squat, heavy, asymmetrical stone base made from two to five large highland stones or one dominant block with secondary brace stones.
- Broad ground footprint, low center of mass, and visible mud-packed contact that make the base feel too heavy for normal humanoids.
- One central pole recess or flat bearing area suggested through shape only, not a socket or functional attachment contract.
- Thick rope and rawhide lashings crossing over the stone mass in large readable bands.
- Optional old oxide red cloth scrap tied into one lashing as a small Blood Axe accent, not a waving banner or cloth-simulation element.
- Sparse blackened iron clamp, ring, or wedge only where it improves construction readability.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any production import lane uses them.

- LOD0: full stone mass profile, major fracture planes, bearing depression, rope/rawhide lashings, large knots, mud-packed base contact, optional cloth scrap, sparse blackened iron accent, and clear heavy footprint.
- LOD1: reduce stone bevel density, simplify small fracture cuts, reduce rope segment count, merge tiny cloth tears, reduce mud-contact undercuts, and remove hidden underside detail.
- LOD2: preserve broad base footprint, dominant stone blocks, main lashing cross, bearing depression read, and one Blood Axe red accent if present while removing secondary chips, minor knots, small iron detail, and non-silhouette cuts.
- LOD3: preserve squat heavy mass, rough asymmetrical outline, visual pole-bearing read, and one simple lashing or red accent block; remove most bevels, secondary stones, small hardware, mud undercuts, and tiny surface breaks.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny chips, stone pitting, soot speckles, ash flecks, rope fibers, cloth weave, lichen, paint chips, scratches, and iron pitting.
2. Small knots, minor rope wraps, tiny cloth tears, small stone wedges, little iron nicks, and non-silhouette mud ridges.
3. Hidden underside cuts, back-facing lashing loops, interior stone overlaps, and low-value undercuts.
4. Secondary blackened iron hardware, duplicate brace stones, optional cloth knot detail, and non-silhouette cracks.
5. Stone bevel complexity, shallow bearing-depression subdivisions, rope-roundness segments, and secondary fracture planes.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary base silhouette and major lashing read.

Never reduce the heavy Giant-scale base footprint, stone mass, Blood Axe red/black warning identity, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant culture before removing small detail.
