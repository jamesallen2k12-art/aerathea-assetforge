`SM_GIA_BloodAxeBrokenLeaningCaveStone_A01` is a low, half-broken cave-edge slab for Blood Axe Giant remnant clusters. It should read as a once-upright marker that has slumped into mud, ash, and cave grit over time, with a broad leaning mass, blunt fracture crown, dark soot settling into chipped planes, and restrained oxide red residue only where it supports Blood Axe identity.

Blood Axe visual language must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This asset may feel hostile, abandoned, and rough, but it must not become refined cave-town masonry, peaceful Giant wayfinding, a magical device, an altar, an objective marker, a puzzle object, or an interactive prop.

## Silhouette Notes

- Primary silhouette: a thick low slab leaning hard to one side or forward, with a broken crown and broad chipped planes.
- Secondary silhouette: buried lower edge with heavy mud contact, ash buildup, and one or two large support stones or exposed root-like stone ridges at the base.
- Tertiary detail: restrained oxide red paint stain, weathered cloth trace, rope/rawhide remnant, or old blackened iron scrape may appear as minor accents only.
- The slab should feel lower and older than `SM_GIA_BloodAxeStandingStone_A01`, but taller and more marker-like than the low cairn.
- Keep the lean physically weighty rather than delicate. The stone should look sunk, wedged, and abandoned, not freshly posed.
- Model the slab mass, fractured crown, major chipped planes, lower embedded edge, and largest base contacts as real geometry.
- Use texture and normal detail for fine cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, rope fibers, small chips, mud streaks, and lichen.

Avoid clean obelisk shapes, arrow-like crowns, readable glyphs, UI marker silhouettes, active ritual marks, glowing channels, refined civic masonry, carved blue-rune culture, dense pebble clutter, fresh gore, or excessive micro-detail.
