## 10. LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full sagging sack body, tied neck, wide tie band, large rawhide patching, reinforced bottom, major folds, optional red accent, and grounded contact.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Preserve the sack mass, tied neck, tie band, reinforced bottom, and largest patches while reducing secondary folds and patch bevels.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve the bulky sack silhouette, band read, bottom reinforcement read, and main patch blocks while flattening small folds and tie tails.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0. Preserve a blocky sagging sack with a visible band and darker reinforced base. Remove most patch thickness, minor fold cuts, and small accent detail.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, cloth fibers, rawhide pores, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud grains, small scratches, and tiny fray.
2. Fine patch-edge waviness, tiny tie tails, small punctures, small red marks, and minor edge cuts.
3. Secondary cloth folds, underside cuts, and small patch bevels.
4. Secondary knot shaping and band subdivisions.
5. Only after these reductions, simplify the main sack mass, wide tie band, reinforced bottom, and Blood Axe material identity.

Never reduce the Giant-scale sack read, soot-dark cloth body, wide tie band, reinforced bottom, or hostile Blood Axe identity before removing small detail.
