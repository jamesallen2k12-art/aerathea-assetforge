## 10. LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full lumpy bundle, crude timber frame or sled rails, crossbars, large hide flaps, three to five lashing bands, large knots, optional tie rings or clamps, red cloth accent, and grounded support silhouette.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce secondary folds, knot subdivisions, rail bevels, minor hide-edge cuts, small clamp bevels, and hidden underside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve the lumpy bundle mass, frame outline, two main rails, strongest crossbar read, primary lashings, and red accent while flattening minor folds and removing secondary tie detail.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0. Preserve a blocky bundle on a simple frame silhouette with dark Blood Axe palette and one red accent block. Remove most knot detail, minor rails, small rings, underside cuts, and small flaps.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, rope fibers, hide pores, fur strands, soot speckles, mud flecks, small scratches, and metal pitting.
2. Fine fray, tiny splinters, small cloth tears, small paint chips, and minor edge cuts.
3. Secondary lashing loops, small knots, small rings, small clamps, and non-structural tie ends.
4. Hidden underside and back-facing frame details.
5. Secondary rail bevels, crossbar subdivisions, and small runner cuts.
6. Only after these reductions, simplify the main bundle mass, frame silhouette, primary lashings, and Blood Axe red accent.

Never reduce the Giant-scale bundle read, crude frame/sled support, primary lashings, or hostile Blood Axe material identity before removing small detail.
