`SM_GIA_BloodAxeCaveStandingPairChockStoneBase_A01` defines a low reusable support prop for paired Blood Axe standing stones. It should read as a few heavy wedge stones, half-buried base forms, trampled mud pads, cold ash pads, and cave-grit grounding beneath a tall/short standing-stone pair.

The asset is visual grounding only. It helps paired stones feel placed by a hostile Giant raider group near a cave approach without becoming a doorway function, path device, encounter cue, objective marker, or proof artifact.

Blood Axe language must remain separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This prop may use crude highland stone, soot, ash, mud, raw earth, oxide-red contamination, and rough raider wear. It must not borrow refined Giant cave-town masonry, terrace and waterwork identity, warm hearth settlement cues, peaceful highland guide marks, civic blue-gray craft language, or restrained blue rune culture as its default read.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette read:

- Three to six large wedge stones clustered around the implied feet of a paired standing-stone composition.
- Two or three half-buried base masses that look driven into mud and ash, not loose decorative pebble scatter.
- Broad low mud pads and cold ash pads feathering outward from the wedge stones.
- Cave-grit grounding around stone contact points, kept as a low secondary read beneath the standing pair.
- A broken, uneven footprint that supports the pair visually without forming a clean ring, route line, objective radius, trigger shape, or UI-readable mark.

Stone forms should be broad and practical: squat triangular wedges, buried slab noses, tipped block ends, and crude pressure stones large enough to feel moved by Giants. The base should sit below the main standing stones and never compete with their tall/short silhouette.

Optional Blood Axe accents may include faint oxide-red dust caught in mud, tiny cloth residue at one edge, or a dull blackened iron scrap partly swallowed by ash. These accents must remain sparse and secondary.

Model only the large wedge forms, buried base lumps, major mud lips, and broad ash/grit planes if a later task separately approves source work. Keep fine grit, ash flecks, soot speckles, small cracks, cloth fibers, mud streaks, stone scratches, lichen dots, and paint dust in future texture, normal, AO, or mask planning.

Avoid dense pebble fields, perfect circular bases, polished masonry, carved civic marks, readable warning text, arrow shapes, marker icons, glowing seams, trophy clutter, graphic gore, warm hearth cues, and refined neutral Giant settlement ornament.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate shipping use.

- LOD0: full wedge-stone cluster, buried base forms, mud pad lips, ash pad edges, cave-grit breakup, and sparse edge contamination.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary chip cuts, minor perimeter notches, small grit ridges, optional accent bevels, and hidden backside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; merge smaller wedges into broader block forms, simplify mud and ash edges, flatten minor grit planes, and preserve the few-large-stones read.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the broad non-circular base footprint, main wedge silhouettes, and high-contrast stone/ash/mud value grouping.

Reduction order:

1. Fine ash flecks, soot speckles, grit dots, tiny cracks, paint dust, lichen specks, cloth fuzz, rope fibers, horn rings, bone pores, and tiny scratches.
2. Small perimeter chips, minor mud crumbs, secondary grit ridges, and non-silhouette edge contamination.
3. Back-facing bevels, hidden underside cuts, and buried forms that do not affect the visible contact read.
4. Secondary wedge bevels, duplicate stone notches, small ash-pad lobes, and optional accent geometry.
5. Broad mud lip density and low mound subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the main wedge silhouettes and support footprint.

Never reduce Giant-scale readability, the few-large-wedge-stones read, Blood Axe material restraint, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant culture before removing small detail.
