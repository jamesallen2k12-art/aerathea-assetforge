`SM_GIA_BloodAxeRitualChannelStone_A01` is a broken Giant-scale ritual-stone fragment used as static Blood Axe site dressing. It should read as a rough, eroded stone module with broad carved troughs cut through its surface, soot and ash staining, cracked slab edges, and optional rough iron wedges hammered into major breaks. The channel stones are carved/eroded static visual channels only, non-graphic and inactive.

The Blood Axe look must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Use crude raider territory language: dark fractured stone, scorched ash, blunt force damage, rough field repair, and controlled oxide red staining only as a broad accent. Do not use refined cave-town masonry, civic stoneworker marks, terrace/waterwork motifs, warm hearth language, restrained blue runes, polished carving, readable symbols, or peaceful highland guidepost design.

## Silhouette Notes

- Primary read: a low, broken, heavy stone slab or paired slab module with one or two broad carved troughs running across the top surface.
- Troughs should be wide, shallow, and eroded, with rounded broken lips and uneven stone wear. They must not read as active liquid channels.
- Shape language should be blunt and Giant-made: oversized slab mass, cracked ends, shoved-together modules, and compressed ground contact.
- Broken stone modules may include offset seams, missing corners, embedded foot stones, and one larger fractured edge for asymmetry.
- Optional large accents: rough iron wedges, crude iron clamp plates, or dark metal pins used sparingly at major fracture points.
- Soot and ash staining should create broad value bands around trough mouths, base contact, cracks, and sheltered recesses.
- Avoid tiny pebble noise, dense chips, micro-scratches, readable symbols, carved text, arrow shapes, UI-like marks, skull piles, fresh gore, active magic marks, glowing grooves, decorative filigree, or refined Giant civic carving.

Model as future real geometry only when separately approved:

- Main slab body, broad trough lips, broken module seams, large cracked corners, major stone steps, optional rough iron wedges, and a simple embedded base.

Handle through future texture, normal, AO, or roughness detail:

- Fine stone grain, small soot flecks, ash deposits, minor chips, small cracks, abrasion, oxidation, iron pitting, and old stain feathering.

## LOD Plan

All important future static mesh variants require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: Full slab silhouette, broad trough lips, broken module seams, major chipped edges, optional rough iron wedges, ash-packed base, and readable baked detail.
- LOD1: 55-65 percent of LOD0; reduce underside cuts, simplify bevels, merge small fracture pieces, and remove minor trough edge cuts.
- LOD2: 30-40 percent of LOD0; preserve the main slab footprint, one dominant trough, broken end massing, and optional iron accent read.
- LOD3: 10-18 percent of LOD0; preserve only the low slab silhouette, broad channel line, dark stone/ash value read, and overall footprint.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny chips, soot flecks, ash flecks, minor scratches, iron pitting, and small stain feathering.
2. Small secondary cracks, narrow cuts, tiny rubble pieces, and small underside forms.
3. Minor trough-edge notches, small bevels, and hidden backside details.
4. Optional secondary iron wedges, extra broken module seams, and minor base stones.
5. Secondary trough complexity after the dominant trough remains readable.

Never reduce the primary low slab mass, broad carved trough read, Giant-scale footprint, or Blood Axe material separation before removing small detail.
