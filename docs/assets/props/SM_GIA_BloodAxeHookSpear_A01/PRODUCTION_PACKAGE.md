`SM_GIA_BloodAxeHookSpear_A01` is a Giant-scale Blood Axe pole weapon built around a long reach silhouette, a brutal spear point, and a clear side-hook profile. It should read as hostile raider gear from a distance: blackened reforged metal, a long dark haft, heavy socket collars, scorched leather grips, dull red Blood Axe markers, and restrained bone or hide trophy accents.

Blood Axe is a hostile Giant sub-faction, not neutral/civilized Giant culture. This asset must stay separate from the blue-gray stoneworker, cave-town, hearth, restrained rune, and practical highland craft language used by non-Blood Axe Giants.

This package is documentation only. It does not create DCC source, FBX exports, Unreal Content assets, runtime source, startup-scene actors, validators, copied concept art, or final visual approval.

## Gameplay Purpose

The hook spear supports future Blood Axe Giant raiders, camp sentries, gate guards, warband silhouettes, weapon racks, and armory dressing. Its production role is to establish a readable spear-and-hook shape at Giant reach scale, with attachment and display planning that later DCC, animation, and gameplay tasks can evaluate.

Expected use cases:

- Equipped Giant pole weapon silhouette for hostile Blood Axe units.
- Camp, gate, rack, wall-lean, or ground-display prop for Blood Axe armories.
- Reach-scale reference for Giant two-handed grip spacing and back-carry clearance.
- Visual contrast against the Blood Axe double axe, cleaver, crusher hammer, longbow, and quiver packages.

This package does not define pull mechanics, drag behavior, damage values, hit windows, trace timing, encounter behavior, loot behavior, or final combat implementation.

## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette: a long, heavy Giant spear with a broad spear point above a large offset hook, reinforced by thick socket bands and a long wrapped haft. The hook must be readable from front, side, and three-quarter views without becoming a dense tangle of barbs.

Key silhouette requirements:

- Preserve the long pole-weapon read first: length, straight shaft, spear point, and two-handed grip zones.
- Use one dominant side hook or meat-hook crescent beneath the spear point as the secondary hero shape.
- Keep the head assembly asymmetrical enough to feel Blood Axe and hostile, but not so busy that the spear point disappears.
- Make the hook thick and structural, with a blunt inner curve and a strong outer cutting profile.
- Use large socket collars and binding rings to show the head was reforged onto a Giant haft.
- Add red cloth or red paint as small Blood Axe identifiers, not full-surface color coverage.
- Use one restrained trophy accent at most, placed where it does not obscure the spear or hook outline.

Model as real geometry:

- Spear tip, hook blade, head socket, broad collars, binding rings, main shaft, grip wraps, butt cap, major chips, and the largest cloth or trophy accent.

Use texture, normals, or baked detail for:

- Tiny scratches, metal pitting, small rivets, leather grain, stitch lines, soot streaks, minor nicks, small hammer marks, small wrap fibers, and grime.

Avoid graphic gore, tiny hook clusters, dense chain, fragile filigree, excessive spikes, or neutral/civilized Giant ornamentation.

- Shaft diameter through main grip zones: 10-16 cm.
- Primary grip usable length: 60-80 cm.
- Offhand grip usable length: 60-90 cm.

Scale validation targets for future work:

- The long shaft must clear Giant shoulders, hips, knees, and back when carried on `back_large_weapon`.
- Two-handed hold markers must preserve visible Giant hand separation and avoid crossed wrists.

## Triangle Budget

`SM_GIA_BloodAxeHookSpear_A01` is a large Giant weapon prop with a simple long form and a more complex head assembly. It should stay lighter than the hero double axe while preserving the hook/spear silhouette.

Targets:

- LOD0 target: 4k-6.5k tris.
- LOD0 hard cap: 7.5k tris.
- Material slots: 1 preferred, 2 maximum.
- Texture set: 1K standard for common display use; 2K for equipped or elite-warband use.

Suggested LOD0 allocation:

- Spear tip, hook blade, socket, collars, and head bevels: 45-55 percent.
- Shaft, grip wraps, lower counterweight, and long-form bevels: 30-40 percent.
- Cloth, large trophies, chips, asymmetry, and secondary fittings: 10-15 percent.

Spend triangles on the hook/spear head, grip fit, and long readable outline before adding small scars, rivets, wrap fibers, dangling details, or surface noise.

## LOD Plan

All important equipped and display versions need LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: 4k-6.5k tris, hard cap 7.5k. Full spear point, hook blade, socket collars, shaft, grip wraps, butt cap, large chips, red accent, and restrained trophy detail.
- LOD1: 2.5k-4k tris. Reduce small bevels, minor chip cuts, small cloth folds, secondary collar subdivisions, and underside head details.
- LOD2: 1.2k-2.2k tris. Preserve overall weapon length, spear point, hook projection, grip zones, and major Blood Axe color blocks; flatten secondary wraps and chips.
- LOD3: 550-1k tris. Preserve long pole silhouette, spear tip, hook silhouette, and red accent read with simple geometry and baked texture detail.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny rivets, scratches, pitting, stitch marks, and small wrap ridges.
2. Minor chips, secondary nicks, and small dents.
3. Cloth frays, small trophy attachments, and tiny bindings.
4. Collar bevel loops and backside socket undercuts.
5. Grip-wrap subdivisions and butt-cap bevels.
6. Only then reduce the spear blade profile, hook projection, shaft length, and two-handed grip read.

Never destroy the long reach silhouette, spear point, or dominant hook profile before removing secondary decoration.
