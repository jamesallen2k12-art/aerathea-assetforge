`SM_GIA_BloodAxeCaveAshMudBase_A01` is a shallow ground-dressing prop for abandoned Blood Axe cave-edge remnants. It should read as old, cold, matte, and trampled: a dirty base layer of ash, soot, mud, earth, and cave grit that visually seats stones, old cloth, and low remnant forms into the ground plane. Its purpose is environmental memory, not an active marker, hazard, interaction affordance, or quest-readable shape.

Blood Axe visual language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This asset may use rough highland dirt, cave grit, soot, ash, trampled mud, blackened stone dust, and restrained oxide-red contamination. It must not use refined Giant cave-town masonry, peaceful highland civic ornament, warm hearth settlement language, blue-gray terrace or waterwork forms, or restrained blue-rune identity as its default read.

- Footprint: uneven and lopsided, with broad lobes and feathered edges so it reads as natural ash/mud accumulation rather than a perfect circle or readable marker.
- Height read: nearly flush to the ground, with occasional shallow ridges where mud, ash, and grit have been pushed around by Giant-scale movement or dragged stones.
- Edge detail: optional embedded stone chips, cold charcoal fragments, old rope residue, or dirty oxide-red cloth fibers may appear at the perimeter, but they must remain sparse.

3. Secondary ash ridges, small scuff geometry, underside cuts, and back-facing bevels.
4. Embedded stones that do not affect the main outline.
5. Mud lip density and broad mound subdivisions.
6. Main irregular footprint only after smaller details are already removed.
