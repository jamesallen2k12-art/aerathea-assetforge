`SM_GIA_BloodAxeDryChannelSunkenSupport_A01` is a partially buried low support stone for Blood Axe dry-channel dressing. It should read as a crude Giant-handled stone that has settled into mud, cold ash, soot, and old camp grit while still visually propping or weighting an abandoned dry channel-stone layout.

The Blood Axe visual language must remain tied to a hostile Giant raider sub-faction and must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Use rough highland stone, soot, cold ash, trampled mud, chipped edges, blackened wear, and restrained oxide red traces. Do not use refined cave-town masonry, blue-gray civic stonework, terrace or waterwork channel motifs, warm hearth settlement identity, peaceful highland guide markers, civic mason symbols, restrained blue rune culture, or polished Giant stoneworker finish.

This support stone is static visual dressing only. Mud and ash contact are part of the prop read, not a claim that the asset conforms to, cuts, blends, validates, or controls world ground. It is not a route blocker, tactical cover object, interactable, simulated object, breakable object, or gameplay marker.

- The stone should feel sunk by age and weight: low visible height, chipped top plane, buried lower edge, compressed base stains, and one heavier side settled deeper than the other.
- Keep the visible silhouette below the dry channel slab read. It should support mass and age, not become a standing marker, altar, stair, barricade, route sign, or cover piece.

## LOD Plan

All important future static mesh variants require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any production import lane uses them.

- LOD0: Full sunken support silhouette, uneven visible crown, flattened bearing face, large chipped corners, broad mud/ash contact band, optional broad accent scar, and readable baked detail.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, minor underside cuts, tiny base clumps, small non-silhouette breaks, and minor contact-band irregularities.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; preserve the low support mass, main bearing face, largest chipped corner, buried-edge read, and broad ash/soot/mud value groups.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the squat sunken support mass, low block profile, contact value, and one Blood Axe accent read if needed.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud droplets, paint chips, lichen flecks, minor scratches, and iron pitting.
2. Small chips, tiny base stones, little surface notches, and minor accent scars.
3. Hidden underside bevels, backside cuts, and non-visible base-contact undercuts.
4. Optional secondary accent geometry, duplicate small wedges, and non-silhouette residue clumps.
5. Secondary bevel density, shallow plane subdivisions, and small crown edge cuts.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary sunken support silhouette, bearing-face read, and contact-band read.

Never reduce the Giant-scale relationship, low sunken support identity, static remnant read, Blood Axe hostile material accents, or primary stone mass before removing small detail. Follow `DOC_GIA_BloodAxeRitualStoneLODAndCollision_A01` for shared policy.
