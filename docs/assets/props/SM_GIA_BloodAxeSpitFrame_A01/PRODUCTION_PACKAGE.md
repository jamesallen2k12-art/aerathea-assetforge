`SM_GIA_BloodAxeSpitFrame_A01` defines a Giant-scale hanging frame and pot stand for Blood Axe camp dressing. It should read as a crude raider-camp utility prop built from heavy raw timber, blackened iron, scorched hide ties, soot, and sparse red Blood Axe warning cloth. The asset supports the nearby `SM_GIA_BloodAxeCookingPit_A01` without becoming graphic set dressing or a gameplay cooking object.

Blood Axe must remain a hostile Giant sub-faction, not neutral/civilized Giant culture. Neutral/civilized separation wording: this prop must stay separate from civilized Giant cave-town masonry, warm hearth halls, blue-gray stoneworker architecture, restrained blue rune language, and highland nomad domestic camp identity.

This package is static visual production planning only. It does not authorize DCC source creation, FBX export, Unreal Content creation, material graph authoring, VFX, startup placement, source concept movement, interaction behavior, cooking behavior, consumable behavior, buff behavior, resource or economy behavior, final visual approval, or first build target selection.

## Gameplay Purpose

The spit frame supports Blood Axe camp readability and environmental storytelling. Its gameplay-facing purpose is visual only: players should recognize an enemy Giant camp utility zone through the oversized support frame, hanging pot silhouette, soot-blackened hardware, rough timber construction, and red Blood Axe accents.

Expected static uses:

- Non-graphic camp cooking dressing beside `SM_GIA_BloodAxeCookingPit_A01`.
- Giant-scale daily-use prop that reinforces the 442 cm female and 470 cm male Giant baselines.
- Layout support for shelters, forge-adjacent yards, raider staging areas, and camp path intersections.
- Readable hostile sub-faction dressing that does not replace neutral/civilized Giant culture.

Out of scope: cooking interaction, consumable creation, food buffs, campfire healing, heat damage, fuel logic, resource gathering, economy systems, workstation behavior, loot containers, pickup behavior, quest objectives, destructible states, audio, VFX, AI behavior, encounter scripting, startup placement, or material graph work.

## Silhouette Notes

The primary silhouette must read from MMO camera distance as a Giant-scale empty cooking support frame, not as a graphic trophy display, weapon rack, gallows, or smallfolk campfire prop.

Core read:

- Two heavy angled raw-log uprights or a broad tripod-like support structure with a high blackened crossbeam.
- Wide ground footprint with braced feet, buried stakes, or stone chocks to show Giant-built mass and instability-resistant construction.
- One simple hanging chain or thick iron hook centered under the crossbeam.
- Broad lidded pot, cauldron silhouette, or suspended kettle shape as non-graphic camp dressing.
- Optional side spit bar or empty support cradle, kept clean and unreadable as food or body-part detail.
- Rope or scorched hide lashings used as large readable bands, not dense fibers.
- Sparse red warning cloth, red paint slash, or tied marker strip for Blood Axe identity.
- Optional ash-dark contact stains and soot bands that visually link the prop to the cooking pit.

Model the uprights, crossbeam, major braces, hanging hook or chain, pot body, support feet, large lashings, crude metal collars, and marker stake as real geometry. Use texture and normal detail for wood grain, scorch marks, small nicks, rope fibers, soot streaks, minor cracks, tiny rivets, and worn red paint.

Avoid graphic cooking language: no humanoid remains, no recognizable body parts, no blood pools, no hanging carcasses, no gore hooks as the main read, no piles of bones as the silhouette, and no shock imagery. If contents are implied, keep the pot covered or unreadable.

## LOD Plan

All important variants require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full frame silhouette, angled supports, crossbeam, major braces, pot, hook or short chain, chocks, large lashings, and broad red accent.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; simplify log bevels, merge small collar details, reduce pot subdivisions, simplify chain links, and reduce brace cuts.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; preserve frame footprint, crossbeam, hanging pot mass, hook silhouette, red accent, and braced stance while flattening secondary lashings and chocks.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the large A-frame or tripod read, dark pot block, crossbeam line, and Blood Axe red/black color block. Remove most small hardware.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny wood chips, soot flecks, small cracks, and worn paint cuts.
2. Secondary rope fibers and hide wrinkles.
3. Small chain bevels and inner link cuts.
4. Minor collar rivets and small hammered dents.
5. Backside braces that do not affect the primary read.
6. Small marker-stake or cloth folds that do not affect recognition.

Never reduce the Giant-scale footprint, high crossbeam, angled support silhouette, hanging pot read, or Blood Axe accent before removing small surface and backside detail.

## Collision Notes

Default collision should be simple and predictable:

- Uprights and support legs: simple boxes or capsules aligned to major timber pieces.
- Crossbeam: one simple box or capsule.
- Ground chocks and large stakes: simple boxes or folded into the leg collision.
- Hanging pot: one simple convex hull if it blocks movement; collision disabled if it is placed above normal traversal height.
- Hook or chain: no collision by default, or folded into pot collision if necessary.
- Side spit bar or cradle: one simple capsule or box only if visibly blocking.
- Cloth, rope lashings, soot, ash stains, and small hardware: no collision.

Do not add complex collision, per-link collision, physics bodies, destructible pieces, navmesh rules, heat volumes, damage volumes, interactable traces, pickup collision, resource nodes, cooking volumes, or gameplay hit shapes in this package. Future navmesh and traversal validation must be handled by a separate implementation task.
