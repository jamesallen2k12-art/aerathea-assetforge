`SM_GIA_BloodAxeMovedCampCairnPair_A01` is an older collapsed pair of crude Giant-built Blood Axe cairns left behind after a camp moved on. It should read as weathered territorial residue: shifted stones, faded oxide red cloth, cold ash, trampled mud, and loose bindings that no longer feel maintained.

The pair must remain visual history only. It is not a tracking mechanic, breadcrumb system, pickup, salvage object, loot marker, patrol marker, spawn marker, interaction prompt, VFX/audio source, DCC source target, Unreal target, first implementation target, or final art approval.

Blood Axe visual language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. Use rough highland stone, soot, ash, mud, faded red cloth, hide ties, rope, sparse blackened iron, old horn, or dull bone. Do not use refined cave-town masonry, blue-gray civic stonework, terrace or waterwork motifs, warm hearth settlement language, peaceful highland wayfinding, restrained blue runes, or default neutral Giant cultural reads.

## LOD Plan

All future static mesh implementation requires LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any shipping use.

- LOD0: full two-cairn pair with taller remnant, lower collapsed partner, major fractured planes, broad bevels, static cloth scraps, large bindings, low ash/mud base, and sparse optional accents.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce secondary bevels, small chips, cloth edge cuts, minor wrap subdivisions, and non-visible contact detail while preserving the unequal pair read.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; merge smaller stones where needed, simplify large stones into broad planes, flatten ash base detail, and keep only the strongest faded red cloth beat.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the two-mass collapsed pair, base footprint, and one faded Blood Axe cloth accent only.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, soot speckles, ash flecks, cloth weave, fray cuts, rope fibers, paint chips, lichen specks, small scratches, and minor pitting.
2. Small knots, secondary lashings, tiny cloth holes, little stone wedges, small horn or bone chips, and minor iron nicks.
3. Hidden underside detail, back-facing stone contacts, interior overlaps, and non-visible ash/mud undercuts.
4. Optional accent pieces, small duplicate foot stones, secondary cloth tears, and non-silhouette debris.
5. Secondary stone bevel density and minor broken-plane cuts.
6. Only after small detail is removed, simplify the primary cairn pair while preserving Giant-scale readability and moved-camp history.

Never reduce the unequal collapsed-pair silhouette, static environmental-history read, controlled faded red cloth beat, or separation from neutral/civilized Giant culture before removing small detail.
