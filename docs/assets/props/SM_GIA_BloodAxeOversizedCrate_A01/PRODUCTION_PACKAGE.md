## 10. LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full blocky crate mass, major plank depth and offsets, blackened iron straps, blunt corner caps, heavy rope lashings, large knots, restrained oxide red mark, broad dirt and wear through texture.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Preserve crate mass, plank rhythm, primary straps, corner-cap read, largest rope bands, knots, and red mark. Reduce secondary bevels, minor plank offsets, small fasteners, and knot subdivisions.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve blocky crate silhouette, broad plank divisions, strap bands, corner cap blocks, one simplified rope path, one knot mass, and red mark. Flatten minor offsets and remove nonessential hardware cuts.
- LOD3: 10-20 percent of LOD0. Preserve a simple oversized crate block with dark strap bands and a small red accent block. Remove most knot detail, small cap bevels, small plank steps, and secondary rope details.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny wood grain, rope fibers, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud flecks, paint chips, fine cracks, scratches, and metal pitting.
2. Small fasteners, small nail heads, minor fray, tiny splinters, and tiny strap dents.
3. Secondary rope tails, small knots, minor lashing loops, and non-structural tie ends.
4. Hidden underside and back-facing plank detail.
5. Secondary plank bevels, small corner-cap cuts, and minor strap thickness.
6. Only after these reductions, simplify the main crate mass, primary strap read, blunt corner caps, primary rope lashings, and restrained red Blood Axe accent.

Never reduce the Giant-scale crate block, plank rhythm, primary strap/corner-cap read, or hostile Blood Axe material identity before removing small detail.
