`SM_GIA_BloodAxeCaveAshRemnantBase_A01` defines a low, cold, static ash-remnant base for Blood Axe cave approach dressing. It is a matte ground-contact piece made from dry ash, soot, trampled mud, cave grit, and restrained edge contamination from embedded stones or old cloth fibers. It supports future static cave approach markers by visually grounding cairns, low standing stones, old cloth scraps, or broken remnant clusters without becoming a decal system, gameplay zone, hazard, aura, objective read, or active ritual effect.

Blood Axe visual language must stay separate from neutral/civilized Giant culture. This asset may suggest hostile Giant raider occupation through cold ash, dirty soot, trampled earth, cave grit, and small oxide-red cloth contamination, but it must not become the default Giant cave-town identity. Neutral/civilized Giant culture remains tied to hidden highland settlements, skilled stonework, cave-town terraces, waterworks, hearth warmth, monumental masonry, and restrained civic blue-gray stone language.

This package does not approve source concepts, final visual direction, DCC source, FBX export, Unreal Content, material instances, texture assets, validators, runtime source, startup placement, visual approval, or implementation sequencing.

## LOD Plan

All future important static meshes need LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any separate implementation signoff.

- LOD0: full irregular ash footprint, shallow mud lips, broad ash ridges, embedded stone reads, and optional cloth-edge contamination.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0; reduce small perimeter cuts, minor ridge loops, secondary stone bevels, cloth-edge subdivisions, and hidden underside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0; simplify the perimeter into larger lobes, flatten small ridges, merge embedded stone forms into the base, and keep only broad value-read geometry.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0; preserve the non-circular cold ground stain silhouette and main low support shape only.

Reduction order:

1. Fine ash flecks, soot speckles, grit dots, cloth fibers, mud streak texture accents, and tiny scratches.
2. Minor perimeter notches, small stone chips, small cloth-edge tears, and tiny raised mud crumbs.
3. Secondary ash ridges, small scuff geometry, hidden underside cuts, and back-facing bevels.
4. Embedded stones that do not affect the main silhouette.
5. Broad mud lip density and low mound subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the main irregular footprint.

Never simplify the asset into a perfect circle, clean ring, objective zone, aura radius, trigger shape, or readable route marker.
