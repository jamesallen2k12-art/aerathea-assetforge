`SM_GIA_BloodAxeTippedAltarRemnant_A01` is a heavy altar remnant left behind at an abandoned Blood Axe camp, cave approach, or highland route edge. It should read as a static slab that has tipped, settled, or partially sunk into ash and mud over time. The prop communicates hostile camp memory, territory residue, and abandonment through silhouette and material wear only.

Blood Axe identity must remain the language of a hostile Giant sub-faction. This asset must not redefine neutral/civilized Giant culture. ## Silhouette Notes

Primary read:

- A massive altar slab tipped off level or partially sunk into ground, with one edge lifted and the opposite edge buried in ash, mud, or collapsed support stones.
- Broad low Giant-scale slab mass with an uneven axe-cut or split-stone outline.
- One dominant diagonal read, usually 10-28 degrees from level, so the asset reads as settled abandonment rather than an active falling object.
- Broken or shifted support stones visible under the raised side, built from a few large readable rocks rather than pebble clutter.
- Ash and mud buildup around the low edge, grounding the slab and showing time has passed.
- Optional old rope scars, rawhide remnants, blackened iron clamps, or sparse dull horn accents that support the silhouette without becoming trophy clutter.
- Faded oxide red cloth or pigment used as small warning beats, not a full red surface treatment.

Model as future real geometry only after separate approval:

- Main tipped slab mass, broad chipped corners, large cracks that affect silhouette, major support stones, raised-edge underside, large clamps, thick ties, and any large horn or bone accent.

## LOD Plan

All future static mesh implementation requires LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: Full tipped slab silhouette, raised underside, buried low edge, shifted support-stone read, broad chips, optional major clamps/ties, optional sparse horn/bone accents, ash/mud base, and authored material detail.
- LOD1: 55-65 percent of LOD0; simplify underside cuts, reduce support-stone bevels, merge small clamp geometry, reduce accent edge cuts, and flatten minor base undercuts.
- LOD2: 30-40 percent of LOD0; preserve the diagonal tipped footprint, low buried side, raised-edge read, soot/ash value, and strongest Blood Axe red accent while removing backside and underside detail.
- LOD3: 10-18 percent of LOD0; preserve only the tilted slab mass, broad ground-contact base, largest support-stone masses, and one controlled red or dark accent read.

Reduction order:

1. Fine chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, lichen, pigment scratches, mud droplets, and hairline cracks.
2. Minor bevels on support stones, clamps, ties, underside shapes, and buried-edge cuts.
3. Small secondary stones, small tie ends, tiny rubble, and small accent notches.
4. Backside and underside detail not visible from normal camera angles.
5. Optional horn/bone accents only after their silhouette stops reading.
6. Raised-edge underside complexity and secondary broken planes after the primary tipped read is secured.

Never reduce the tipped/sunk silhouette, Giant-scale footprint, static abandoned-camp read, or Blood Axe hostile material identity before removing small detail.
