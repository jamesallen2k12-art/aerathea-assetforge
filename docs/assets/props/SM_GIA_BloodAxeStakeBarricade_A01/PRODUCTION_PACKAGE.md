Primary silhouette: a low, wide, hostile Giant-scale pointed-log barricade segment with uneven sharpened stakes leaning forward, rear brace logs, heavy cross ties, and dark Blood Axe material accents. The form should read as an obstruction before the viewer resolves small details.

Required silhouette elements:

- Five to nine oversized vertical or forward-canted stake logs with uneven heights and bluntly sharpened tips.
- One or two heavy horizontal crossbeams binding the stakes into a single segment.
- Rear diagonal braces or ground feet that make the barricade feel planted and hard to shove aside.
- Mud-packed base, rough stone chocks, buried logs, or iron foot collars that ground the asset.
- Crude blackened iron clamp bands, staple plates, large peg blocks, or chain loops used sparingly.

Model real geometry for the main stake logs, crossbeams, broad braces, large sharpened tips, base chocks, major bindings, large metal bands, large red cloth pieces, and any major horn or shield accent. ## LOD Plan

All future approved mesh work should include LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: 6k-10k tris target, 12k hard cap. Full stake row, varied sharpened tips, crossbeams, rear braces, base chocks, major metal bands, large lashings, red warning cloth, and sparse optional marker accents.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce small bevels, minor chips, secondary lashing turns, non-silhouette peg detail, cloth edge cuts, small mud clumps, and backside detail.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Merge or simplify individual stake cuts, reduce brace subdivisions, flatten small bands and rope wraps, simplify cloth shapes, and remove most marker undercuts.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0. Preserve the wide barricade mass, pointed top rhythm, dark timber block, major crossbeam line, red Blood Axe accent, and rough base footprint. Remove most small hardware, minor lashings, optional markers, and backside forms.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny scratches, nail heads, small peg marks, rope fibers, cloth weave, metal pitting, and small paint chips.
2. Small cloth holes, frayed edges, mud flecks, minor bark cuts, and small stake chips.
3. Secondary lashings, small knots, small plates, and non-silhouette chain loops.
4. Optional horn, bone, shield, or marker undercuts that do not define the primary read.
5. Backside brace subdivisions, underside base detail, and small ground-contact forms.
6. Only then reduce the main stake row, pointed top rhythm, crossbeam mass, and broad red cloth signal.

Never destroy the primary wide obstruction silhouette, Giant-scale stake height, pointed-log rhythm, or Blood Axe red accent before removing small detail.
