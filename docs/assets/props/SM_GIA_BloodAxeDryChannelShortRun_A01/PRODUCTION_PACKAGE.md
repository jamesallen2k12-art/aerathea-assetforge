## Silhouette Notes

- Primary read: one low, broad, short straight slab segment with a single dry trough running across the top length.
- The trough should be wide, shallow, matte, and ash-stained, with eroded lips that stop clearly at broken or rough ends.
- The slab should feel Giant-made: heavy, blunt, asymmetrical, compressed into the ground, and too large for normal humanoid handling.
- Ends should be interrupted through chipped lips, rough cutoff planes, ash-packed seams, or slight offset breaks so the piece never reads as a seamless channel path.
- The strongest presentation side should expose the trough, one broken end, a broad chipped lip, and the low heavy footprint.
- Optional large accents may include one or two blackened iron wedges, crude repair plates, or rawhide ties around a major fracture only if they remain secondary to stone mass.
- Soot and ash should create broad value bands in the trough interior, undercut recesses, base contact, and broken end pockets.

## LOD Plan

All important future static mesh variants require LOD0, LOD1, LOD2, and LOD3 before any production import lane uses them.

- LOD0: full short slab silhouette, broad dry trough lips, interrupted end cuts, major chipped corners, ash-packed trough value, soot-dark base, optional large iron accent, and readable baked detail.
- LOD1: 55-70 percent of LOD0; reduce underside cuts, secondary bevels, small chips, minor end notches, small iron edge cuts, and non-silhouette trough noise.
- LOD2: 30-45 percent of LOD0; preserve the main slab footprint, one dominant dry trough, interrupted end identity, broad ash/soot value, and optional iron accent read.
- LOD3: 10-25 percent of LOD0; preserve only the low stone mass, short straight dry trough line, broken end read, and controlled Blood Axe red or ash accents.

Reduction order:

1. Tiny cracks, small chips, soot speckles, ash flecks, mud droplets, lichen flecks, paint chips, minor scratches, cloth weave, rope fibers, rawhide pores, and iron pitting.
2. Small side stones, minor knots, tiny horn chips, small metal nicks, little rubble pieces, and secondary cloth cuts.
3. Hidden underside bevels, backside cuts, non-visible ash undercuts, and interior broken-edge noise.
4. Optional secondary iron wedges, duplicate support stones, extra trough notches, and non-silhouette damage.
5. Secondary trough complexity and shallow groove subdivisions.
6. Only after small detail is gone, simplify the primary slab mass, interrupted ends, broad trough lips, and low support silhouette.

Never reduce the Giant-scale relationship, dry/no-flow read, static remnant identity, Blood Axe hostile material accents, or primary stone silhouette before removing small detail. Follow `DOC_GIA_BloodAxeRitualStoneLODAndCollision_A01` for shared policy.
