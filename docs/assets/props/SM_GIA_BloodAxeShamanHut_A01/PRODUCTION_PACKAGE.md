## Silhouette Notes

Primary silhouette: a Giant-scale hut shell with a low, heavy footprint, tall uneven roof crown, massive timber ribs, patched hide wall panels, a broad intimidating entrance, rough threshold stones, and a few ritual-adjacent marker poles. The asset should read as "Blood Axe shaman hut" from MMO camera distance through structure shape and material language, not through effects or dense small props.

Key silhouette goals:

- Squat but towering hut mass, broader and more ceremonial than a standard hide tent.
- Asymmetric roofline with heavy ridge beam, horn-like rib ends, broken stake crown, or a dark central smoke cap as static geometry.
- Wide Giant entrance framed by two oversized posts, blackened iron collars, red cloth ties, and rough threshold stones.
- Thick outer ribs or tripod-like support members that make the hut feel hand-built by hostile Giants.
- Patched hide, scorched leather, and timber wall planes grouped into large readable panels.
- Sparse ritual-adjacent markers: one to three large horn, bone, broken shield, or dark-stone tokens near the entrance or roofline.
- Red warning cloth and oxide-red paint used as faction identifiers, not as full coverage.
- Optional static smoke-hole cap or soot-black roof peak as shape language only. No smoke, fire, glow, or VFX is approved.
- Optional side leaners, bundled poles, or low barricade slabs may be planned as shell-adjacent dressing if they support the hut footprint, but do not turn this into a full camp cluster.

Model the hut's primary shell, roof mass, entrance frame, structural ribs, ridge beam, large hide panels, large metal clamps, major lashings, red cloth panels, rough threshold stones, and sparse marker objects as real geometry. Use texture, normal, and AO detail for hide pores, small stitch rows, soot gradients, wood grain, rope fibers, tiny scratches, minor cracks, paint chips, cloth weave, and ash speckles.

Avoid graphic gore, dense skull curtains, charm clutter, readable text, carved civilized Giant runes, polished ritual architecture, glowing magic symbols, spell diagrams, floating effects, tiny modeled stitches, hundreds of nails, and detailed interior dressing.

## LOD Plan

All future implementations require LOD0-LOD3.

- LOD0: full hut silhouette, roof crown, main shell, entrance frame, broad hide panels, structural ribs, ridge beam, threshold stones, marker poles, blackened hardware, red cloth, sparse bone/horn/stone accents, and ground-contact skirt.
- LOD1: 60-70 percent of LOD0. Reduce small hide cuts, secondary lashings, minor roof-edge chips, small clamp bevels, backside panel detail, and non-silhouette marker undercuts.
- LOD2: 35-45 percent of LOD0. Preserve hut footprint, roof crown, entrance clearance read, primary ribs, red cloth blocks, and dark threshold while flattening secondary folds, small hardware, and backside seams.
- LOD3: 15-25 percent of LOD0. Preserve the large hut mass, roofline, entrance opening, red/black Blood Axe read, and one or two major marker shapes. Remove most hardware, small accents, underside details, and ground clutter.

LOD reduction order:

1. Tiny stitches, fray cuts, nail heads, scratches, pitting, paint chips, ash flecks, and wood grain cuts.
2. Small cords, knots, secondary lashings, minor cloth holes, and non-structural straps.
3. Small marker chips, little bone fragments, and non-silhouette stone cracks.
4. Backside wall detail, underside roof detail, and interior-facing shell surfaces.
5. Secondary clamp bevels, panel undercuts, and minor rib chips.
6. Shell-adjacent dressing that does not support the hut read.
7. Only then simplify the primary roof crown, entrance frame, main shell, and red-cloth identity.

Never reduce the Giant-scale entrance, primary hut mass, roof crown silhouette, main support ribs, or Blood Axe red/black identity before removing small detail.
