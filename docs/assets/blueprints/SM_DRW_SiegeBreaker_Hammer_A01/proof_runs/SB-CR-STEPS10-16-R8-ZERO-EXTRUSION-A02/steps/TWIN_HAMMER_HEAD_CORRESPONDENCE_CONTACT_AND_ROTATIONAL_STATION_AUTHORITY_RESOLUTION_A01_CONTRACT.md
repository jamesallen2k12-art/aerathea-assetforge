## Index-Only Unequal-Rail Triangulation Rule

This rule may run only after the complete head embedding/incidence gate passes.
It creates integer index triplets in JSON only. It does not create points,
faces, geometry, or a visual solution.

For two explicitly approved corresponding rails:

`A=(A_0,...,A_(m-1))`

`B=(B_0,...,B_(n-1))`

the following prerequisites are mandatory:

- `m>=2` and `n>=2`;
- `A_0` and `B_0` share one approved physical corner;
- `A_(m-1)` and `B_(n-1)` share the other approved physical corner;
- sequence directions agree;
- every point has complete authoritative XYZ coordinates; and
- the approved closure equation names `A` and `B` as corresponding rails.

Starting at `i=0, j=0`, compare the next exact normalized ranks using integer
cross multiplication:

`L=(i+1)(n-1)`

`R=(j+1)(m-1)`

- If `L<R`, append `(A_i,A_(i+1),B_j)` and increment `i`.
- If `R<L`, append `(A_i,B_(j+1),B_j)` and increment `j`.
- If `L=R`, append
  `(A_i,A_(i+1),B_j)` and
  `(A_(i+1),B_(j+1),B_j)`,
  then increment both `i` and `j`.

Continue until `i=m-1` and `j=n-1`.

The independent validator must prove:

- every original rail index is used;
- no rail point is interpolated, averaged, resampled, skipped, or duplicated
  as a new sample;
- every triplet references only the two named rails;
- the first and last corners are preserved;
- the count and order follow the exact integer comparisons; and
- winding matches the separately approved patch orientation.

This combinatorial rule does not resolve a missing rail, missing coordinate,
missing corner, or missing closure equation. If its prerequisites fail, zero
triangle triplets must be emitted.
