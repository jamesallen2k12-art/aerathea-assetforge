## Step Decision

Determine whether the authoritative front, back, left, right, and top exact-
evidence records can form one coherent measurement authority without
averaging, stretching, silent selection, unapproved tolerance, view
prioritization, correction, or interpretation.

The step must produce one reviewable decision:

- `pass_exact_dataset` when every required exact correspondence is supported
  and every compared exact value agrees under an already approved rule;
- `pass_with_explicit_blocks` when the exact records remain mutually usable
  only as a bounded dataset with every unresolved correspondence,
  disagreement, world conversion, and unknown preserved as blocked;
- `blocked_no_coherent_exact_dataset` when unresolved conflicts prevent the
  records from functioning as one coherent exact measurement authority; or
- `fail_input_or_integrity` when an approved input, hash, formula, reference,
  authority classification, or audit invariant fails.

The step may pass with explicitly blocked unknowns. It may not pass by hiding,
repairing, selecting, or weakening a disagreement.

## Governing Core Principle

The Evidence-Bound Decision Rule, closed-world authorization, First
Principles, Stop-the-line, and evidence-versus-interpretation separation
govern this step. Current approved files, exact source-linked formulas,
recovery overrides, hashes, semantic correspondence IDs, and explicit blocked
states are authority. Visual agreement, convenience, old completion claims,
quarantined whole packages, approximate tolerance, and planned future
rectification are not authority.

## Locked Comparison And Tolerance Policy

- Coordinate indexing remains zero-based integer pixel centers.
- Recorded source spans retain their approved half-open or declared formula
  conventions; Step 09 may replay but not redefine them.
- Front/back panel axes map to world `X/Z`; left/right panel axes map to world
  `Y/Z`; top panel axes map to world `X/-Y` with normal `+Z`, exactly as
  recorded by Step 05.
- Pixels in different panels do not share a common pixel-distance metric.
- Cross-view correspondence exists only where an approved semantic ID,
  source-authored dimension label, station/span role, component/contact ID,
  and formula chain support it.
- Hash, integer-coordinate, and full/local round-trip tolerance remains `0`.
- Cross-view pixel-distance tolerance remains `null`.
- World-space translation and rotation tolerance remains `null`.
- No approximate numeric tolerance may be invented from display resolution,
  antialiasing, visual closeness, rounding, or production convenience.
- Each calibration observation remains independent unless exact equality and
  an approved unification rule are both present. Step 09 does not create such
  a rule.
- The Step 06, Step 07, and Step 08R disagreement records must remain intact.
  New cross-view conflicts must be appended and source-linked, never merged
  away or substituted for an older blocked entry.
- Direct printed dimensions remain authoritative as direct dimensions; they
  do not silently convert every observed pixel contour into world space.
