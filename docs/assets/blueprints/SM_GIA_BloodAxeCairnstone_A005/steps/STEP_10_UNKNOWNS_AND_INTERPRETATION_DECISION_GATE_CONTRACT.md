## Step Decision

Prepare a complete, source-linked decision package through which Flamestrike
can approve, reject, declare unnecessary, or leave blocked every unavoidable
interpretation required before a coherent 360-degree geometry specification
can exist.

Step 10 must preserve two parallel truths:

1. approved exact evidence and blocked exact-data conclusions remain
   unchanged; and
2. any accepted interpretation becomes separately labeled construction
   authority only within the precise rule Flamestrike approves.

An approved interpretation does not retroactively convert an unknown,
disagreement, occlusion, inferred closure, or selected convention into exact
source measurement.

Step 10 is complete only when every required decision item has exactly one
Flamestrike classification:

- `approved_interpretation_rule`;
- `rejected_interpretation_option`;
- `approved_omission_not_required_for_geometry`; or
- `remains_blocked`.

`approved_omission_not_required_for_geometry` requires a written reason and
may not hide a dependency needed by Step 11. `remains_blocked` prevents every
dependent Step 11 construction rule and later geometry action from advancing.

Principles, Stop-the-line, and evidence-versus-interpretation separation
govern this step. Approved A005 records and exact source evidence remain the

## Decision-Item Schema

Every decision item must record:

- stable decision-item ID;
- inherited source IDs and exact JSON pointers or record headings;
- blueprint unknown class;
- component, contact, interface, or whole-asset scope;
- source-owned facts that remain exact;
- blocked fact or missing authority;
- why the decision is or is not required for coherent 360-degree geometry;
- downstream Step 11 dependency;
- one or more clearly labeled written interpretation options, including the
  option to leave the item blocked;
- for every option, its proposed rule, visible-source effect, hidden-surface
  effect, scale or rectification dependency, topology implication, and known
  risk;
- whether a diagram is necessary to understand the option;
- exact distinction between evidence and interpretation;
- candidate recommendation, if one is made, labeled as recommendation rather
  than authority;
- Flamestrike decision field, initially `pending`;
- final artifact classification, initially `candidate`.

An option must be concrete enough that its later use in Step 11 is auditable.
Words such as `reasonable`, `natural`, `best fit`, `clean`, `approximately`,
or `artist discretion` do not constitute a construction rule without an
explicit bounded method and approval.

## Option And Recommendation Policy

- Every decision item must include `leave blocked` as an available outcome.
- Options must be materially distinct and described without preference-
  loading language.
- A recommendation may be included only when its reason is traceable to the
  approved project goal, minimum distortion, visible-source preservation,
  buildability, or another explicit governing rule.
- The recommended option does not gain authority until Flamestrike approves
  it.
- No option may overwrite, repair, relabel, or weaken an exact-data record.
- A selected convention must remain separately attributable as
  interpretation in every later consumer.
- If one decision depends on another, the dependency order must be explicit.
- If a decision cannot be made without corrected imagery, a distortion
  study, or a new authority source, the item must remain blocked and name the
  required sub-gate.
