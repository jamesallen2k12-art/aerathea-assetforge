## Proposed Rule Family

Rule candidate ID: `BTIR_SAME_VIEW_LINEAR_FALLOFF_A01`.

The execution may test only the following rule family:

1. Work in one approved source panel at a time. No cross-panel pixel pairing
   or transfer is allowed.
2. Use only exact approved C-004 outer points or visible outer-segment
   endpoints and exact approved CL-003 samples as candidate anchors.
3. Enumerate every possible same-side outer-to-inner anchor pairing before
   selecting a candidate pairing. The same-side classification and selection
   method must be declared and replayable.
4. A candidate trace may be a straight, unfilled line segment only. No curve,
   spline, smoothing, tangent fitting, freehand correction, or added
   intermediate control point is allowed.
5. Parameterize a trace from locked inner anchor `p_i` to outer anchor `p_o`
   as `p(t) = (1-t) p_i + t p_o`, where `0 <= t <= 1`.
6. If a future outer displacement `d_o` is tested, the only permitted
   displacement falloff is `d(t) = t d_o`. Therefore the CL-003 endpoint has
   exactly zero displacement and the outer endpoint receives the full declared
   displacement.
7. Every trace, pairing, and falloff calculation is `candidate
   interpretation`; none is source evidence, a closed boundary, a surface,
   deformation topology, or geometry.
8. Adjacent traces may not be joined, lofted, filled, triangulated, averaged,
   or treated as a continuous surface during this pass.
9. An unowned gap remains visibly labelled `BLOCKED / UNKNOWN`. The candidate
   line may cross the gap only as an explicit interpretation proposal for
   Flamestrike's later decision; it may not make the gap source-owned.
10. If anchor pairing is non-unique, crosses a component boundary, exits an
    approved source-owned containment region, or requires an invented
    endpoint, stop that trace as blocked.

The contract deliberately tests the narrowest reproducible line rule first.
It does not pre-authorize a continuous 2D deformation field. If such a field is
later required, it needs another separately approved rule.

  only after the eligible same-side set is established from approved source

  is `blocked`; no tie-breaker may be invented.

  it may not replace it.
