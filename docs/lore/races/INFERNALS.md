# Infernals

## Lore Entry

The Infernals were once mortal.

Their ancestors were cultists who bent their knees to demonic powers and the forgotten gods of the Abyss. They did not serve out of desperation alone. They served with hunger, envy, devotion, and the belief that mortality was a weakness to be cut away. Over generations, their rites were answered. The cult bloodlines were blessed by Balgoroth, and those blessings changed them beyond the reach of ordinary flesh.

At first the signs were small: eyes that saw too much in darkness, wounds that closed too quickly, children born with sharpened nails, fever-red skin, or the first buds of horns beneath the scalp. Later came the greater gifts. Wings tore through shoulders. Tails thickened behind the spine. Claws blackened into natural weapons. Their bodies grew stronger, stranger, and harder to kill. By the time the old mortal kingdoms understood what had happened, the cultists were gone. In their place stood the Infernals, a race that no longer wished to remember itself as mortal.

The Infernals do not see their transformation as a curse. They call it elevation.

They believe they are the chosen of Balgoroth, shaped to prove that strength is the only prayer worth offering. Every kill taken in battle is an act of worship. Every enemy broken by claw, fang, wing, spell, or terror is a hymn. They have long since forsaken mortal weaponry, which they consider the crutch of lesser beings. An Infernal warrior would rather tear an armored knight apart with black claws than lower themselves to carry a sword. Their rogues stalk from above, using wing, tail, claw, and impossible sight. Their hunters track prey that believes itself invisible. Their mages draw on Abyss-touched power with a confidence that borders on ecstasy.

They abhor weakness, but their hatred of weakness is not simple slaughter. An Infernal who is offended by cowardice, pleading, frailty, or failure may fly into a rage and kill everyone near the offender while leaving the weak one alive. This is not mercy. In Infernal belief, the unworthy should not be given to Balgoroth. A sacrifice made from weakness stains the altar. Better to leave the offender breathing, surrounded by ruin, forced to witness the cost of being unworthy. Some Infernals believe that such torment may one day harden the survivor into a soul fit for sacrifice.

Among their own kind, the Infernals judge status through strength, will, predation, and the visible favor of demonic gifts. Larger horns, stronger wings, faster regeneration, keener invisible sight, and more controlled rage can all become marks of standing. Their society honors warriors, rogues, hunters, and mages, but each path is expected to prove devotion through personal power rather than tools, machines, or inherited titles.

To outsiders, Infernals are terrifying because they are not mindless demons. They remember enough of mortal ambition to scheme, rule, hunt, and hate with patience. They are close enough to the Abyss to feel monstrous, but still mortal-descended enough to build cult courts, bloodline hierarchies, hunting packs, ritual arenas, and sacrificial laws. They are not merely invaders from beyond the world. They are what worship can become when an entire people chooses damnation and calls it divinity.

## Lesser Infernals

Lesser Infernals are the children of the Infernal bloodline, born through mortal flesh or sired by mortals touched by Infernal power. To outsiders this makes them even more disturbing than their elders, because they are not summoned creatures, crafted servitors, or mindless spawn. They are born.

A Lesser Infernal enters the world fully autonomous. It does not pass through the helpless infancy expected of mortal children. From its first breath it can move with purpose, feed, hide, strike, and survive. Its mind is not fully mature, but its instincts are already sharpened by the demonic inheritance of the bloodline. This makes the first year of a Lesser Infernal's life one of the most dangerous stages of its existence.

The young of this bloodline are violently aggressive far earlier than mortal children. Infernal cults call this the culling temper: the first proof that the demon and mortal halves of the child are at war inside one body, forcing the stronger nature to dominate. Lesser Infernals bite, claw, challenge, ambush, and test anything that shows fear. They are not merely unruly. Their aggression drives them to feed, fight, heal, adapt, and seek stronger prey. Weakness is exposed quickly, and Infernal society treats that exposure as necessary.

During that first year, the young Infernal grows at a terrifying pace by feeding on the slain. The act is not only nourishment; Infernal cults often treat it as the first proof that Balgoroth's blessing has taken root. Each kill-fed growth spurt hardens the body, strengthens the claws, darkens the horn buds, thickens the tail, and draws the wing structure out of the back. By its first birthday, a Lesser Infernal is physically and mentally equivalent to a mortal child of roughly seven years. Infernal cults call the first stable stage of this existence Spawn. The first successful kill marks the 1st Kill stage, when the child has proven that it can turn the culling temper into survival. Those that continue to feed, fight, heal, and adapt become Blooded, recognized as true young members of the bloodline rather than unstable offspring.

After that first year, the violent acceleration slows. Lesser Infernals age only one mortal year for every decade that passes. Their childhoods, apprenticeships, rivalries, and brutal lessons can therefore stretch across centuries. They do not become patient because they are gentle; they become patient because time is cheap to them.

The average Infernal lifespan is roughly one thousand years. Once a Lesser Infernal reaches full maturity, it no longer appears to age in any obvious mortal sense. The face holds, the body remains strong, the wings stay powerful, and the claws do not dull. Age becomes visible only very late. Infernals who survive beyond nine centuries begin to show signs of age again, and from that point forward they age at approximately one visible year for every year lived.

Among Infernals, the term "Lesser" is not an insult in the way outsiders assume. It marks a stage of bloodline development rather than a lack of worth. A Lesser Infernal can still be dangerous, clever, and favored. What they lack is the full refinement of mature demonic gifts: complete wing power, full regenerative control, disciplined rage, perfected invisible sight, and the authority to claim sacrifices in Balgoroth's name without oversight.

Infernal courts, hunting packs, and cult enclaves treat Lesser Infernals as future weapons. They are taught early that mercy toward weakness is contamination, that mortal parentage is only a doorway, and that every slain enemy may become strength. Some are raised openly among Infernal kin. Others are hidden within mortal societies until their horns, claws, appetite, or impossible survival reveals what they are. These hidden Lesser Infernals are feared because their first year can turn an ordinary household, village, or battlefield aftermath into the beginning of an Infernal bloodline cell.

This early aggression is also how Infernal bloodlines weed out weakness without needing formal judgment. Lesser Infernals that cannot control the culling temper are destroyed by it, by rivals, or by the prey they underestimate. Those that survive learn discipline through violence, becoming stronger, more cunning, and more worthy in the eyes of Balgoroth's faithful. Infernal elders do not try to remove the aggression. They shape it into hunting rites, arena trials, ambush lessons, and dominance contests until the young learn when to rage, when to stalk, and when to spare the unworthy for later torment.

## Race Anchor

- Proposed race code: `INF`.
- Classification: mortal-descended demonic race, separate from true Abyss demons and devils.
- Patron power: Balgoroth.
- Adult height range: 5'0"-9'0" / 152-274 cm.
- Lesser Infernal lifecycle stages: Spawn, 1st Kill, Blooded, Elder, Ancient.
- Lesser Infernal lifecycle rule: autonomous at birth, violently aggressive from a very young age, first-year kill-fed growth, first birthday equivalent to a mortal age of 7, then 1 apparent year per 10 lived years until mature stasis; visible aging resumes after 900 years.
- Approved Lesser Infernal behavior term: culling temper.
- Sexual dimorphism: pending dedicated body sheet; current canon gives a shared adult range.
- Core roles: warrior, rogue, hunter, mage.
- Core combat identity: natural weapons, wings, tail, claws, demonic magic, predatory senses, regeneration.
- Cultural identity: chosen of Balgoroth, kill-as-worship doctrine, contempt for weakness, ritual sacrifice standards.
- Weapon rule: they reject mortal weaponry as weakness. Armor, ritual bindings, jewelry, brands, piercings, trophies, and magical focus marks are allowed if they read as body enhancement or devotion rather than reliance on tools.

## Physical Traits

- Reddish skin hue, ranging from muted ember-red to deeper crimson or ash-red variants.
- Horns are required and should be real silhouette geometry in production.
- Large leathery wings are required and must affect the full body silhouette.
- Long thick tail is required and should support balance, threat display, and animation readability.
- Black claws on hands are required; feet may also use black claw or talon language if approved later.
- Regenerative powers should be represented by healed scars, darkened fissure marks, or subtle ember-vein texture, not gore.
- Ability to see invisible creatures should be represented by eyes, brow markings, or restrained supernatural eye glow.

## Culture And Belief

- Infernals believe Balgoroth did not corrupt them, but perfected them.
- Sacrifice must be worthy. The weak may be spared specifically because they are judged unfit to offer.
- Rage at weakness often redirects toward bystanders, companions, guards, or loved ones around the offender.
- Lesser Infernal aggression is treated as a culling force that exposes weakness and drives the young toward strength.
- They value strength, predatory patience, controlled brutality, magical confidence, and visible demonic gifts.
- They distrust mercy when it protects weakness, but may show cold restraint when a victim is not worth killing.
- Their rituals should feel severe and hierarchical rather than chaotic.

## Visual Direction

Infernals must read as a race, not a random demon encounter type. They should be more humanoid and socially legible than the existing Abyss creature packages, while still visually closer to demonic forms than to ordinary mortals.

Primary silhouette language:

- Horn crown or sweeping horn profiles.
- Large folded or spread leathery wings.
- Thick tail with readable mass.
- Black clawed hands.
- Athletic to powerful humanoid bodies across a broad 5'0"-9'0" scale range.
- Upright predatory posture; avoid hunched monster default unless a specific class variant calls for it.

Material and color language:

- Ember-red, crimson, ash-red, and dark umber skin.
- Black claws, black horn tips, dark leathery wings.
- Charcoal leather, ritual wraps, scorched metal accents, bone or obsidian ornaments.
- Sparing abyssal glow for eyes, brands, spell marks, and mage variants.
- Keep glow restrained and readable; do not turn every surface into fire.

Distinction from Abyss demons:

- Infernals are mortal-descended, culturally organized, and race-scaled.
- True Abyss demons remain more entropic, ancient, monstrous, and creature/enemy focused.
- Infernals may worship or emulate demonic powers, but should not share every Abyss creature material rule by default.

## Gameplay Hooks

- Regeneration can support self-healing, reduced bleed effects, or post-combat recovery.
- Invisible sight can support stealth-counter gameplay, hunter tracking, mage detection, and rogue duels.
- Wings can support gliding, short aerial bursts, leap attacks, wing-shield poses, or traversal abilities depending class balance.
- Tails can support balance, knockback attacks, stealth silhouette, and animation identity.
- Natural weapon doctrine makes their claws, wings, tail, and magic central to class animation sets.
- Lesser Infernal NPCs can support aggressive juvenile ambush behavior, pack challenge encounters, bloodline growth events, and controlled arena/trial content.
