# Dark Elves

## Canon Status

This document has been reconciled against the Hermes Dark Elf records from June 29-30, 2026, plus the approved Aerathea race and asset anchors.

Confirmed anchors:

- Dark Elves are elegant, shadowed, moonlit, secretive, oath-bound, refined, dangerous, and ancient.
- Female height range: 5'2"-5'8" / 157-173 cm.
- Male height range: 5'8"-6'0" / 173-183 cm.
- Approved production house spelling: House Nytherion.
- Deprecated production spelling: Nytharion.
- Goddess name: Nytheria.
- False goddess / demoness name: Seramus. The Hermes history also contains the variant `Suramus`; use `Seramus` unless Flamestrike approves a spelling change.
- Major homeland: the Valley of Night.
- Core visual language: dark silver, obsidian, black leather, veiled cloth, violet lunar glow, crescent motifs, refined oath-bound culture.
- Core roles: shadowblade, priestess, spellbow, sentinel, rogue, and arcane defender.

Open expansions in this document are clearly marked where they extend beyond the Hermes records.

## High Concept

The Dark Elves of Aerathea are an ancient people born of magik, exile, betrayal, shame, survival, and moonlit beauty. They are not evil by nature. They are pragmatic, relentless, wounded, proud, and dangerous because history taught them that hesitation can kill an entire people.

Their culture was shaped in the Aetherial world after their kingdoms were torn away from Aerathea. Driven from their lands, hunted by darkness, and forced to survive where the light of day never reached, they changed into something distinct from their High Elf kin. They looked into a darkness that tried to devour them and learned to answer with precision, secrecy, power, and memory.

Dark Elves do not present themselves through loud celebration or bright conquest. Their civilization favors calm remembrance, periods of silence, hidden paths, elegant structures, moonlit runes, veiled law, and ruthless readiness.

## Aetherial Origin

The High Elves and Dark Elves were both born of magik. During the ancient shielding of Aerathea, Elven kingdoms were torn from the physical world and thrust into the Aetherial world. One group stayed true to their origins and refused to forget the world they came from. They would become the High Elves as remembered in current lore.

The second group suffered a harsher fate. They were attacked, driven from their lands, and forced deep into the Aetherial realm. They fought for survival in a land of darkness and power where daylight never reached, but the light of the moon was eternal.

In that place they changed.

They did not become cruel for cruelty's sake. They became pragmatic and relentless. They sought power because power kept the darkness away. Their seal became cracked and damaged, and void-born minions pressed against them, seeking to devour what remained of their people.

In isolation, they wondered what had become of their kin. When they found the High Elves again, the discovery enraged them. Their kin seemed unchanged, optimistic, noble, and sheltered. The future Dark Elves had suffered in the dark while their kin appeared to have been preserved in the palm of their creator.

That pain made them vulnerable to Seramus.

## Seramus

Seramus came to the wounded Elves as a goddess of darkness, the hidden, and the forgotten. She offered protection and guidance. Over time, many Dark Elves turned away from what they had been and embraced her as their new goddess, Mistress of Dark Magik and patron of assassins.

This was a deception.

Seramus was no true goddess. She was a demoness of great power who sought to enslave the Dark Elves through their despair, need, and hunger for survival.

## The Diadem Of Nytheria

In return for her protection, Seramus demanded tribute and a test of loyalty. She sent the Dark Elves to reclaim an ancient artifact: a diadem of incredible power hidden at the Ancient Temple of Nytheria.

The sorceress Morgwen agreed and sent a Dark Elf force to secure the artifact. Seramus had also given the artifact's location to the High Elves, knowing that the surest way to test and bind the Dark Elves was to set them against their former kin.

At the temple, the Dark Elves found Prince Elegast and his entourage already inside. The prince held the diadem and refused to surrender it, saying it would be taken home and destroyed. The Dark Elves struck first. Prince Elegast was killed, and nearly all of his company died. One survivor was sent back to the High Elves with the prince's head and the message that the Temple of Nytheria and its treasures belonged to the Dark Elves.

Elora, a Dark Elven maiden, then placed the diadem upon her head. In that moment she understood the truth. The Temple of Nytheria was not an abandoned shrine to a forgotten power. Nytheria was present, waiting. The Dark Elves had forgotten her, but she had not forgotten them.

Through the diadem, Elora saw that Seramus was a demoness and that a spell had clouded the Dark Elves for millennia. As the spell broke around Elora, the truth began to spread. Nytheria guided them to a secret passage and hidden treasures with which they armed themselves for the struggle to free their people had just begun
.

## The Breaking Of Seramus

When the truth reached the Dark Elves, Morgwen and her coven were confronted and destroyed. Seramus was driven out, but the victory came at terrible cost. A third of the Dark Elven people lay dead.

What had been done could not be undone. The High Elves mourned Prince Elegast and never forgot the betrayal. The Dark Elves survived Seramus, but they could not erase the shame of what they had done under her influence or what they had allowed to happen to themselves.

The wound between High Elves and Dark Elves is therefore not a simple rivalry. It is an irreconcilable historical and spiritual break rooted in exile, envy, demonic deception, murder, and a betrayal that both peoples remember differently but deeply.

## Nytheria And The Hidden Path

After Seramus was driven out, the Dark Elves followed Nytheria and began to walk the hidden path. Nytheria is the true goddess remembered in the Hermes records: the goddess whose temple held the diadem, whose presence broke the demonic deception, and whose guidance allowed the Dark Elves to save what remained of their people.

Nytheria's worship should feel moonlit, secretive, solemn, and beautiful rather than bright, triumphant, or public. Her rites are calm remembrances, periods of silence, hidden-path observances, lunar ceremonies, and sacred acts of memory. Dark Elves honor the goddess, but they do not turn devotion into overt public spectacle.

Production note: earlier draft language about "the Veiled Moon" should be treated only as an optional title, ritual phrase, or later religious sub-concept. It is not the primary Hermes canon. The primary goddess is Nytheria.

## House Nytherion

Sylvari Moonveil and Vaylorin Nightsong rose to lead the house founded in honor of Nytheria, who had saved the Dark Elven people. The Hermes record uses the older spelling `Nytharion`, but current Aerathea production canon has corrected the house name to House Nytherion.

House Nytherion has ruled for thousands of years, though not without challengers. It should read as old, severe, elegant, politically dangerous, and deeply tied to Nytheria's deliverance.

Nytherion identity:

- Founded in the aftermath of Seramus's defeat.
- Named in honor of Nytheria.
- Associated with hidden paths, moonlit law, memory, shame, and survival.
- Uses crescent, veil, dark silver, obsidian, and violet lunar Aetherium motifs.
- Produces sentinels, shadowblades, spellbows, priestesses, rogues, and arcane defenders.
- Prefers preparation, leverage, secrecy, and perfect timing over brute force.

## Houses And Council

Unlike the High Elves, the Dark Elves refuse to be ruled permanently by a king or queen. Their history taught them that too much power centralized in too few hands can doom a people.

Other Dark Elf houses formed after the breaking of Seramus. Houses rise and fall, but the Council is eternal. The strongest house leads, while the other houses advise, challenge, and oppose when required.

This political structure should create constant tension without turning Dark Elves into chaotic schemers. Their politics are formal, dangerous, and bound by memory. A house may seek power, but if it repeats the mistake of Seramus and Morgwen by endangering the people, the Council exists to resist it.

## Physiology And Lifespan

Dark Elves share the graceful height range of Elves, but their presentation should feel more severe, guarded, and moon-shadowed.

Female Dark Elves stand 5'2"-5'8" / 157-173 cm. Male Dark Elves stand 5'8"-6'0" / 173-183 cm. Their silhouettes should be elegant and controlled rather than exaggerated, monstrous, or hunched.

The wider Hermes magic record states that the more magical an individual or race is, the longer lived they tend to be after the Cataclysm and the return of Aetheric influence. The Elves, Dark Elves, Drakhar, and Valar are among the most magical peoples. Exact Dark Elf lifespan, maturity rate, and elder appearance remain open for approval.

Proposed visual traits:

- Cool-toned skin suited to moonlit environments.
- Silver, white, black, violet-black, or cold ash hair variants.
- Eyes that catch violet, silver, or lunar tones in darkness.
- Refined facial planes and controlled posture.
- Elegant body language shaped by court discipline and predatory caution.

## The Valley Of Night

The major Dark Elf society exists in the Valley of Night.

The Valley of Night is a deep gorge where waterfalls and rivers descend toward a lake at the bottom of the chasm. The Dark Elves built their civilization into this hidden gorge because it is inaccessible, difficult to find, and protected by narrow passes, secret passages, and magical defenses.

It is a place of incredible beauty. Bioluminescent markings, moon-touched flora, and silver runes glow in the moonlight, providing faint illumination that Dark Elves can see by as if it were daylight. Their structures are elegant and beautiful, and everything that grows in the valley has adapted to life there.

The valley is oriented to align with the movement of the world and is consistently bathed in moonlight. That light varies with the phase of the moon, changing the mood, shadow, color, and spiritual tone of the place.

Dark Elves do have ceremonies and holidays honoring Nytheria, but they do not practice overt celebration as a cultural default. They walk the hidden paths of the world through calm remembrance, silence, and solemn observance.

Style rule: do not describe the Valley of Night with modern scientific language such as wavelengths, lumens, photonics, or technical ecology. This is fantasy lore. Use moonlight, silver runes, bioluminescent growth, reflected starlight, sacred geometry, water, mist, stone, and goddess-linked mystery.

## Magic

The Elves, Dark Elves, Drakhar, and Valar are among the most magical peoples in Aerathea. Dark Elves are deeply connected to magik, but their relationship to it is shaped by exile, Seramus's deception, Nytheria's deliverance, and survival in the Valley of Night.

Dark Elf magic should feel lunar, shadowed, precise, and controlled. It is not generic evil magic. It is not Abyssal entropy. It is not Infernal flame. It is not necromantic rot. It is magik filtered through moonlight, hidden paths, silver runes, oath, memory, and old shame.

Common magical lanes:

- Veilcraft: concealment, silence, altered visibility, and hidden passage.
- Moonbinding: oath marks, seals, containment circles, and vow enforcement.
- Shadowstep: short controlled repositioning through prepared shadows.
- Reflected sight: seeing through mirrors, pools, silvered glass, and moonlit surfaces.
- Spellbow channeling: focused Aetherium shots through crescent bows and lunar crystals.
- Wardcraft: barriers, counter-magic, forbidden-door seals, and sentinel glyphs.
- Memory rites: preservation, obscuring, or ritual replay of sworn testimony.
- Hidden-path rites: goddess-linked ceremonies for safe travel through guarded passages.

The disciplines above are production expansions consistent with the Hermes records, not all individually named in Hermes.

## Law, Shame, And Memory

Dark Elf culture carries collective shame for Seramus's deception and for the killing of Prince Elegast. That shame does not make them submissive. It makes them formal, guarded, and relentless about memory.

They know that forgetting can kill. They know that despair can invite a false savior. They know that power without witness can become enslavement.

Major legal and cultural principles:

- Memory must be preserved, even when painful.
- No ruler should hold unchecked authority over the people.
- Hidden knowledge must be guarded by more than ambition.
- A promise made before witness has spiritual and political weight.
- Betrayal of the people is worse than betrayal of a rival house.
- Mourning and silence can be public duties.

Proposed extension: Dark Elf justice may use witnesses, oath marks, memory mirrors, moonlit testimony, and council judgment. These are not yet individually locked as Hermes canon, but they fit the confirmed themes.

## Religion And Rites

Dark Elf religion centers on Nytheria.

Nytheria's worship is not a bright festival faith. It is solemn, moonlit, quiet, and rooted in remembrance. The Dark Elves honor her because she did not forget them when they had forgotten her. Her temple, diadem, secret passage, and hidden treasures are central to the liberation from Seramus.

Ritual tone:

- Calm remembrance.
- Periods of silence.
- Moonlit observance.
- Hidden path processions.
- Ceremonies in chasm mist, temple halls, and silver-rune gardens.
- Sacred memory of the dead from the Breaking of Seramus.
- Shame rites acknowledging the betrayal of the High Elves.

Priestesses and priests of Nytheria should be judges, memory-keepers, guides of hidden paths, keepers of relics, and wardens against demonic deception.

## Warfare

Dark Elf warfare favors preparation, ambush, denial, misdirection, and elegant lethality. This follows directly from their history. They survived by acting before the darkness could devour them.

Military roles:

- Sentinels hold gates, bridges, halls, shrine paths, and citadel approaches.
- Shadowblades remove threats before battles begin.
- Spellbows control distance with crescent bows and violet lunar focus shots.
- Arcane defenders combine shields, ward magic, and counter-spell techniques.
- Priestesses and priests act as battlefield witnesses, ward casters, and judgment authorities.
- Rogues gather proof, sabotage enemy supply, and recover stolen names, seals, or relics.

Dark Elves prefer to win before an enemy realizes the battle has started. They map escape routes, study moon phases, plant false trails, bind doorways, and mark targets long before weapons are drawn.

## Outsider Relations

High Elves:
The divide between High Elves and Dark Elves is irreconcilable in current Hermes lore. The High Elves remember Prince Elegast, the temple betrayal, and the message sent home. The Dark Elves remember exile, suffering, Seramus's deception, and the shame of what they did. Neither side can simply forget.

Valar, Dwarves, and Gnomes:
Hermes records place High Elves, Valar, Dwarves, and Gnomes among the closest allies. Dark Elves can still interact with them, but they are not part of that closest-allied bloc by default.

Drakhar, Orcs, and Minotaurs:
Hermes records place Dark Elves, Drakhar, Orcs, and Minotaurs as reluctant allies who often work together out of necessity.

Ogres, Giants, Anubisath, and Basari:
Hermes records identify these peoples as wildcards and independents who may trade with or ally with others depending on circumstance.

Drakhar:
Dark Elves and Drakhar are both highly magical, but Drakhar obsession with artifacts and Volcreon-linked motives makes them dangerous around Dark Elf ruins, temples, and hidden roads.

Infernals and Abyssal forces:
Dark Elves should be especially sensitive to false patrons, demonic deception, and sealed threats because of Seramus. Their lunar-shadow identity must remain visually distinct from Abyssal entropy and Infernal flame.

## Player Fantasy

A Dark Elf player fantasy should emphasize precision, secrecy, moonlit elegance, survival-born pragmatism, and the burden of inherited shame.

Strong class fantasies:

- Shadowblade: close-range stealth duelist shaped by Seramus's old assassin legacy, now reclaimed under Nytheria.
- Spellbow: ranged lunar-Aetherium archer using marked shots and silence effects.
- Sentinel: disciplined defender of the Valley of Night, temple roads, and hidden passes.
- Priestess or priest: servant of Nytheria, memory-keeper, judge, and hidden-path guide.
- Rogue: infiltrator, proof-gatherer, lock specialist, and political operative.
- Arcane defender: shielded battlemage focused on containment and denial.

Dark Elf gameplay should reward planning, positioning, timing, marks, and controlled bursts rather than chaotic brawling.

## Visual Production Notes

Dark Elves must remain distinct from Abyss entities. Abyss visuals use entropy, black flame, scorched basalt, bone, charred iron, ruin magic, and broken binding chains. Dark Elves use refined lunar elegance, dark silver, obsidian, veiled cloth, crescent motifs, silver runes, and restrained violet glow.

For character art:

- Keep silhouettes graceful, upright, and controlled.
- Use crescent, veil, moon, silver-rune, and hidden-path motifs sparingly but clearly.
- Preserve readable edge contrast; do not let black gear collapse into flat darkness.
- Use violet emissive only for eyes, oath marks, lunar crystals, spell foci, silver runes, and selected weapon accents.
- Avoid savage spikes, excessive skulls, gore, or chaotic demon language.

For environments:

- Use verticality, silence, reflection, water, mist, moonlight, and chasm scale.
- Include crescent arches, dark stone, reflective pools, veiled banners, silver runes, and violet lanterns.
- Use bioluminescent markings and moon-adapted growth without scientific explanation.
- Keep halls ceremonial and intentional rather than cluttered.
- Use hand-painted material detail and baked-AO-style depth.
